## What Is a Plunge Pool?

A plunge pool is a compact swimming pool, typically ranging from 6'√ó12' to 8'√ó20', designed for relaxation, exercise, and cooling off rather than swimming laps.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Pre-fabricated construction** ‚Äî Most are fiberglass or modular concrete shells
- **Compact footprint** ‚Äî Fits in small backyards where traditional pools won't
- **Depth-focused** ‚Äî Usually 4‚Äì7 feet deep (designed for standing/cooling, not diving)
- **Versatile use** ‚Äî Can be heated for year-round use or chilled for cold plunge therapy

## Permits and Regulations in PA and DE

### Pennsylvania Requirements

**Building Permit:** Required in every township we serve (Delaware County, Chester County, Main Line area)

**Key code requirements:**
- **Fence/barrier:** 4-foot minimum height, self-closing/self-latching gate
- **Setbacks:** Typically 10 feet from property lines (varies by township)
- **Electrical:** Must be installed by licensed electrician, inspected by township
- **Plumbing:** Backflow prevention device required

**What we handle:**
- Permit application and drawings
- All inspections
- Certificate of Occupancy

**What you handle:**
- HOA approval (if applicable)
- Payment of permit fees ($200‚Äì$500 depending on township)

### Delaware Requirements

Delaware pools are regulated at the county level:

- **New Castle County:** Building permit required, similar fence/setback rules to PA
- **Electrical permit:** Separate from building permit
- **Health Department:** May require inspection for certain pool types

We handle all Delaware permitting as well ‚Äî it's part of our service.

## Is a Plunge Pool Right for You?

**Consider a plunge pool if:**
- You have a small backyard (or want to preserve yard space)
- You want a pool primarily for relaxation and cooling off
- You're budget-conscious but still want a high-end backyard feature
- You want faster installation (swimming this summer, not next)
- You're interested in cold plunge therapy or year-round heated use

**Consider a traditional pool if:**
- You have swimmers who want to do laps
- You entertain large groups regularly
- You have the budget and yard space for a larger pool
- Resale value is your top priority

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can you really swim in a plunge pool?**  
A: You can tread water, do water exercises, and cool off ‚Äî but you can't swim laps. Think of it as a deep soaking pool rather than a swimming pool.

**Q: How deep are plunge pools?**  
A: Typically 4‚Äì7 feet deep. Most are one consistent depth (no shallow end), though some models have a bench or seating area.

**Q: Can I use a plunge pool in winter?**  
A: Absolutely ‚Äî if you add a heater. Many of our customers use heated plunge pools year-round for relaxation and cold plunge therapy.

**Q: Do plunge pools require the same chemicals as regular pools?**  
A: Yes, the chemistry is the same (chlorine, pH, alkalinity), but you use less because there's less water volume.

**Q: What about maintenance?**  
A: Less than a traditional pool. Smaller surface area = less debris. Less water = less chemicals. Most owners spend 30‚Äì45 minutes per week or hire weekly service for $150‚Äì$200/month.

**Q: Will a plunge pool increase my home value?**  
A: Yes, though not as much as a traditional pool. Expect a 50‚Äì70% return on investment vs. 70‚Äì100% for full-size pools. The bigger value is your enjoyment while you live there.
