## What to Look for in a Pool Maintenance Company

Not all pool services are created equal. Here's what separates the pros from the pretenders:

### 1. Licensed and Insured (Non-Negotiable)

In Pennsylvania, any contractor doing $500+ in work must be registered with the Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) program. Ask for their registration number and verify it at [hicsearch.pa.gov](https://hicsearch.pa.gov).

Insurance matters too. Pool work involves electricity, chemicals, and expensive equipment. If a technician breaks your $3,000 pump or slips on your deck, you want to know their insurance covers it ‚Äî not your homeowner's policy.

**Red flag:** They hesitate or make excuses when you ask for proof of insurance.

### 2. Consistent Schedule (Same Day, Every Week)

The best pool services show up like clockwork. You should know that every Tuesday (or whatever day), your pool tech is coming. This consistency matters because:

- You can plan around it (gates unlocked, dogs inside)
- Your water chemistry stays stable
- Problems get caught early (before they become expensive)

**Red flag:** "We'll be there sometime this week" or frequent rescheduling.

### 3. Real Chemical Knowledge (Not Just Dumping Chlorine)

Anyone can pour chlorine in a pool. A professional understands water chemistry:

- pH balancing (7.2‚Äì7.6 is the sweet spot)
- Total alkalinity (prevents pH bounce)
- Calcium hardness (protects your plaster and equipment)
- Cyanuric acid (stabilizes chlorine so it doesn't burn off in the sun)
- Phosphates and nitrates (algae food ‚Äî remove them proactively)

A good tech tests your water every visit and adjusts based on actual readings, not a guess.

**Red flag:** They show up with a bucket of tablets and no test kit.

### 4. Communication (You Should Know What Happened)

After every visit, you should know:
- What chemicals were added
- What the water test results were
- Whether any equipment issues were spotted
- When they'll return next

Some companies leave paper logs. Others text or email digital reports. Either works ‚Äî but silence doesn't.

**Red flag:** You never hear from them between visits, even when you have questions.

### 5. Local Presence (Not a National Franchise)

National pool franchises have their place, but they come with drawbacks:
- High turnover (different tech every month)
- Rigid scheduling systems
- Less flexibility with unusual pool setups
- Corporate policies over customer relationships

A locally owned Main Line pool service knows your neighborhood, understands common pool types in your area, and treats you like a neighbor ‚Äî because you are.

## Red Flags: When to Fire Your Pool Company

Sometimes you don't realize how bad your pool service is until you've experienced a good one. Here are the warning signs:

### Inconsistent Visits
If they show up Monday one week and Thursday the next ‚Äî or skip weeks entirely ‚Äî that's unacceptable. Your pool depends on consistent care.

### Green Water Between Visits
An occasional algae bloom after a heatwave happens. But if your pool is green every few weeks, your service isn't doing their job. Period.

### Can't Explain What Chemicals They're Adding
You have a right to know what's going into your pool. If your tech can't explain why they added something, they might not know themselves.

### No Insurance or Registration
We mentioned this above, but it's worth repeating. Unlicensed pool work is illegal in PA for jobs over $500. Don't risk it.

### Equipment Keeps Breaking
If your pump, filter, or heater keeps having problems "out of nowhere," your tech might be missing warning signs ‚Äî or causing issues through neglect.
