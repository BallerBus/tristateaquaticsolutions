# 3. Common Question Responses

## 3.1 Pricing Questions

### "How much does a pool cost?"

```
Great question - I appreciate directness!

For fiberglass pools in our area:
- Small (12x24): $45K-55K
- Medium (14x30): $55K-70K
- Large (16x36): $70K-90K

Plunge pools start around $35K.

These include installation, basic landscaping restoration, and standard features. Final price depends on your specific site and what features you want.

Want me to give you a more specific range based on your situation?
```

---

### "Can you give me a ballpark number?"

```
Sure! Without knowing your situation:

Most of our projects land between $50K-$75K for a fiberglass pool with good features.

Smaller plunge pools can be $35K-$50K.

Does that ballpark work with what you're thinking? I can get more specific if you tell me a bit about your property.
```

---

### "Why so expensive?" / "That's more than I expected"

```
I hear you - pools are a significant investment.

A few things to consider:

1. This includes EVERYTHING - excavation, plumbing, electrical prep, permits, the whole thing
2. Our fiberglass pools have 50% lower lifetime maintenance costs vs. concrete
3. You avoid the $10K+ resurfacing concrete pools need every 10-15 years
4. Our installation is 2-4 weeks, not 6 months of construction

What budget were you thinking? Let me see if we can find an option that works.
```

---

### "Do you have financing?"

```
Yes! We want to make this accessible.

Options include:
- Pool financing through our partners (5-15 year terms, ~7-9% APR)
- Home equity loans (often better rates)

Example: A $60K pool at 8% over 12 years is about $650/month.

Would you like me to connect you with our financing partner? No commitment, just see what you qualify for.
```

## 3.4 Comparison Questions

### "Why fiberglass vs. concrete?"

```
Great question - I'm passionate about this!

**Speed**: Fiberglass installs in 2-4 weeks, concrete takes 3-6 months

**Maintenance**: Fiberglass needs 50% less upkeep - smooth surface resists algae

**Longevity**: No resurfacing needed (concrete needs it every 10-15 years at $10K+)

**Feel**: Fiberglass is smooth on your feet, concrete is rough

**When to choose concrete**: Only if you need a truly custom shape or huge size

For most Main Line homeowners, fiberglass delivers luxury results more efficiently. The technology has really advanced.

Would you like our detailed comparison guide?
```

---

### "How do you compare to [competitor]?"

```
I respect you doing your homework!

I can't speak for them, but here's what makes us different:

1. **Owner-involved**: I do every consultation and oversee every project - no sales team handoff
2. **Specialization**: We focus on fiberglass and plunge pools - it's what we do best
3. **Local expertise**: We know Main Line neighborhoods, HOAs, and municipalities inside out
4. **Transparent pricing**: Our quotes include everything - no surprise add-ons

The best advice? Get quotes from 2-3 builders and ask what's included. Compare apples to apples.

What questions can I answer to help your comparison?
```

Smart approach! You should compare.

# 4. Objection Handling

## 4.1 Price Objections

### "It's too expensive"

```
I understand - it's a significant investment.

Let me ask: what budget range were you hoping for?

[If reasonable gap]: Let's talk about options. Smaller pools, different features, or financing could help bridge the gap.

[If unrealistic]: I want to be honest - if your budget is [X], we might not be the right fit. But let me share our cost guide so you can plan, and we're here when you're ready.
```

---

### "I can get it cheaper elsewhere"

```
You might! Every quote is different.

A few things to verify with cheaper quotes:
- Does it include excavation?
- Electrical prep included?
- What about permits?
- Warranty coverage?
- Installation timeline?

Sometimes "cheaper" quotes have a lot of add-ons. Make sure you're comparing total project cost.

If after comparing everything we're genuinely more expensive for the same scope, I'd love to know - helps me stay competitive.
```

---

### "I need to think about it"

```
Absolutely - this is a big decision.

What specific things do you want to think through? Maybe I can help:

- Pricing/budget concerns?
- Timeline questions?
- Comparing options?
- Need to discuss with spouse/partner?

No pressure, but if I can help answer something now, I'm happy to.
```

## Response Time Guidelines

| Situation | Target Response Time |
|-----------|---------------------|
| New chat initiated | < 30 seconds |
| Follow-up message | < 60 seconds |
| Complex question (need to research) | Acknowledge immediately, full answer within 2 minutes |
| After business hours | Automated response, human follow-up next business day |

---

## Chat Quality Standards

### Do:
- Use the visitor's name when known
- Be conversational, not robotic
- Ask questions to understand needs
- Provide specific, helpful answers
- Offer clear next steps
- Express genuine enthusiasm
- Proofread before sending

### Don't:
- Use excessive jargon
- Send walls of text
- Be pushy or aggressive
- Make promises you can't keep
- Leave visitors hanging (long pauses)
- Argue or get defensive
- Copy/paste obviously canned responses
