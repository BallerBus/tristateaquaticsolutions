# 5. Social Media Chat Best Practices

## 5.1 Tone & Voice Guidelines

### Do:
- Be conversational and friendly
- Use the person's name
- Include relevant emojis (sparingly)
- Share visual content (photos, videos)
- Be helpful and informative
- Respond promptly
- Show personality

### Don't:
- Be overly formal or corporate
- Use too many emojis (unprofessional)
- Send walls of text
- Be pushy or salesy
- Leave messages unanswered
- Share confidential info publicly
- Argue or get defensive

---

## 5.2 Message Length Guidelines

| Type | Ideal Length | Max Length |
|------|--------------|------------|
| Quick response | 1-2 sentences | 3 sentences |
| Standard response | 3-5 sentences | 1 short paragraph |
| Detailed answer | 1-2 paragraphs | 3 paragraphs max |
| Photo caption | 1 sentence | 2 sentences |

*For longer responses, break into multiple messages*

---

## 5.3 Visual Content Guidelines

### When to Send Photos:
- Answering style/design questions
- Showing recent work
- Demonstrating features
- Before/after comparisons
- Responding to inspiration requests

### Photo Quality:
- High resolution
- Good lighting
- Professional-looking
- Relevant to conversation
- Branded if possible

### Video Options:
- Quick walkthrough of completed projects
- Feature demonstrations
- Behind-the-scenes installation
- Personal introduction from Brandon

---

## 5.4 Privacy & Compliance

### Do:
- Move to private DM for personal details
- Get permission before sharing customer photos
- Keep pricing discussions private
- Protect customer information

### Don't:
- Share customer addresses publicly
- Post customer names without permission
- Discuss specific customer projects publicly
- Share pricing in public comments

---

## 5.5 Escalation Guidelines

### When to Escalate to Phone/Email:
- Complex technical questions
- Detailed pricing discussions
- Customer complaints
- Contract/legal questions
- Lengthy back-and-forth
