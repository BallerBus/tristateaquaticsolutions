### Why Choose a Local Pool Builder?

**Q: Why should I choose a local pool builder over a national company?**

A: Local builders offer advantages that large companies simply cannot match:

- **Accountability**: We live in your community. Our reputation is personal.
- **Accessibility**: You can reach decision-makers directly, not call centers.
- **Knowledge**: We understand local soil conditions, permitting requirements, and architectural styles.

---

### What Questions Should I Ask Pool Builders?

**Q: What questions should I ask any pool builder before signing?**

A: These questions reveal how a company operates. The answers matter more than glossy brochures:

**About Communication:**
1. Who will be my primary contact throughout the project?
2. How often will I receive updates, and in what format?
3. If I have a concern, who do I call? Can I reach them directly?

**About Quality:**
4. Will my contract specify materials by brand and model?
5. Can I visit a current project or speak with recent customers?
6. What happens if a material needs to be substituted?

**About Warranties:**
7. Exactly what does your warranty cover - and for how long?
8. What is the process if I need warranty service?
9. Are there situations where warranty claims are denied?

**About Pricing:**
10. Is this quote all-inclusive, or should I expect additional costs?
11. What triggers a change order?
12. What is your payment schedule, and how does it align with project milestones?

**About the Company:**
13. How long have you been in business? How many pools have you installed?
14. Are you BBB accredited? Can I see your complaint history?
15. Will the same crew work on my project start to finish?

---

### Red Flags When Choosing a Pool Company

**Q: What are red flags when evaluating pool builders?**

A: Be cautious if you encounter any of these warning signs:

**Communication Red Flags:**
- Difficulty reaching the salesperson after initial enthusiasm
- Vague answers to direct questions
- Pressure to sign quickly without time to review contracts

**Contract Red Flags:**
- Materials described generically ("quality coping") rather than specifically ("XYZ Brand limestone coping, 12" bullnose")
- Large deposits required upfront (industry standard is 10-20% to start)
- Payment schedule front-loaded (most money due before most work completed)
- Warranty section full of exclusions and conditions

**Reputation Red Flags:**
- High volume of BBB complaints (check bbb.org)
- Pattern of negative reviews mentioning the same issues
- Company requires NDAs or discourages public reviews
- No references available in your specific area

**Process Red Flags:**
- Cannot provide a detailed timeline with milestones
- Unwilling to meet on-site before providing a quote
- Different crews on-site with no clear supervision
- No written change order process

**Trust your instincts.** A company that will treat you well as a customer demonstrates that from the very first interaction.

## 8. Sales Talking Points

### When Prospects Mention They're Also Talking to Larger Companies

**Opening Response:**
> "That's smart - you should absolutely compare your options. What I'd encourage you to do is ask them the same questions you ask us. The differences in how companies answer will tell you a lot."

**Follow-Up Questions for Them:**

Ask the prospect what matters most to them, then address:

**If they value communication:**
> "Ask them: After I sign, who do I call if I have a question? Can I reach a decision-maker directly, or will I go through a customer service line? How often will I get updates, and from whom?"

**If they value quality:**
> "Ask them to show you exactly what materials will be used - by brand and specification, not just 'premium quality coping.' If it's not in the contract, it's not guaranteed."

**If they're worried about problems:**
> "Ask them what happens if something goes wrong after the pool is complete. What's their warranty process? Have they ever denied a claim, and why? How quickly do they respond to service requests?"

**If they mention price:**
> "Price matters, but the lowest quote isn't always the best value. Ask what's included, what could cause the price to increase, and what their change order process looks like. And ask about long-term costs - some pools cost more to maintain year after year."

### Handling Specific Competitor Mentions

**If they mention Anthony & Sylvan specifically:**
> "They're a well-known company with a lot of history. As you do your research, I'd encourage you to look at recent reviews - not just the company's marketing - to see what current customers say about their experience with communication, warranties, and follow-through. Then compare that to what you hear from our references."

**Never say:**
- "They have terrible reviews"
- "They'll rip you off"
- "Don't use them"

**Always redirect to:**
- "Here's what to ask them..."
- "Here's what we do differently..."
- "Here's what our customers say..."

### Closing Framework

After discussing competitors:

> "At the end of the day, you're not just buying a pool - you're entering a relationship with the company that builds it. The pool will be in your backyard for decades. The question is: who do you want to be in relationship with for that long?

> We're a local company. I live in this community. My reputation is my business. And I'll be here - not just during construction, but for years afterward - to make sure you're happy with your investment.

> I can't speak for how other companies operate, but I can tell you exactly how we operate. And I can give you the names and numbers of neighbors who've worked with us.

> Take your time, do your research, and when you're ready, I'd love to earn your business."

## Summary: Positioning Do's and Don'ts

### DO:

- Lead with your strengths
- Encourage prospects to ask questions of all builders
- Provide specific differentiators (owner involvement, warranty process, communication guarantees)
- Let your track record speak for itself
- Offer references confidently
- Focus on the relationship, not just the transaction

### DON'T:

- Directly attack competitors by name
- Quote competitor review statistics in sales conversations
- Use fear-based language ("you'll regret it")
- Seem defensive or dismissive about competition
- Promise things you can't deliver to beat a competitor
- Trash-talk to seem superior
