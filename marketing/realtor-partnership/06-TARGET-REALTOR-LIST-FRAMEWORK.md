# Section 1: Ideal Partner Profile

## Who Makes the Best Pool Referral Partners

---

### Tier 1: High-Priority Targets

**Profile: Luxury/High-End Specialists**
- Focus on homes $750,000+
- Clients with discretionary income
- Properties with large lots/backyards
- Already recommending premium contractors

**Why they're ideal:**
- Clients can afford $50,000-$100,000 pools
- Used to premium service expectations
- Referral reflects their brand positioning
- Higher average commission per referral

**Examples:**
- Luxury home specialists at major brokerages
- Agents with "luxury" in their marketing
- Those who advertise in Main Line magazines
- Consistent high-volume producers ($10M+/year)

---

**Profile: New Construction/Spec Home Agents**
- Work with builders on new developments
- Sell homes with "pool-ready" backyards
- Clients are already investing heavily
- Often work in affluent subdivisions

**Why they're ideal:**
- New homeowners are prime pool buyers
- Backyards are clean slates
- Buyers often have remaining budget
- Relationship can yield multiple referrals per development

---

**Profile: Relocation Specialists**
- Help executives relocating to the area
- High-income clients
- Looking for "dream home" features
- Often have signing bonuses/relocation packages

**Why they're ideal:**
- Clients coming from pool-culture regions (South, Southwest)
- Higher income brackets
- Want to recreate lifestyle from previous home
- Company relocation packages can cover extras

---

### Tier 2: Strong Candidates

**Profile: High-Volume Residential Agents**
- 25+ transactions per year
- Mix of price points
- Strong referral networks
- Professional, systems-driven

**Why they're strong:**
- Higher volume = more opportunities
- Organized enough to remember referrals
- Already have vendor relationships
- Understand the value of partnerships

---

**Profile: Family/Lifestyle-Focused Agents**
- Market to families with children
- Focus on school districts
- Emphasize backyard space in listings
- Active in community

**Why they're strong:**
- Clients are buying "family homes"
- Pool = family lifestyle amenity
- Backyard is already a selling point
- Long-term residents (more likely to invest)

---

**Profile: Established Community Agents**
- 10+ years in specific neighborhoods
- Known by name in the community
- Deep local knowledge
- Trusted recommendations carry weight

**Why they're strong:**
- Referrals from them are highly trusted
- Know which homes have pool potential
- Can identify pre-sale pool opportunities
- Community reputation aligns with ours

---

### Tier 3: Worth Pursuing

**Profile: Rising Stars**
- 3-5 years in business
- Growing transaction volume
- Active on social media
- Hungry for differentiators

**Why worth pursuing:**
- Will appreciate partnership more
- Eager to provide value to clients
- More likely to actively promote
- Relationship builds as they grow

---

**Profile: Team Leaders**
- Run agent teams of 3+
- Can refer to entire team
- Single point of contact for multiple agents
- Often have marketing coordinators

**Why worth pursuing:**
- One relationship = multiple referral sources
- Team meetings for presentations
- Marketing support already in place
- Leverage across more transactions

# Section 4: How to Find Target Realtors

## Research Methods and Tools

---

### Method 1: MLS Production Rankings

**Where to find:**
- Local Association of Realtors websites
- Philadelphia Magazine annual "Best Realtors" lists
- Main Line Today "Top Agents" features
- Delaware Today real estate rankings

**What to look for:**
- Annual sales volume
- Number of transactions
- Average sale price (higher = better for us)
- Geographic focus areas

---

### Method 2: Zillow/Realtor.com Agent Search

**Process:**
1. Search our target zip codes on Zillow
2. Filter for "Agents" or "Find an Agent"
3. Sort by recent sales activity
4. Note agents with multiple $500K+ transactions
5. Cross-reference with luxury listings

**What to capture:**
- Agent name
- Brokerage
- Phone/email
- Recent transaction volume
- Average price point

---

### Method 3: LinkedIn Research

**Process:**
1. Search "Real Estate Agent" + "Main Line" or specific towns
2. Filter by 2nd connections (for warm introductions)
3. Look for agents with "Luxury" or "Executive" in title
4. Check for mutual connections who can introduce

**What to capture:**
- Profile URL
- Current brokerage
- Any mutual connections
- Recent activity/posts

---

### Method 4: Luxury Listing Research

**Process:**
1. Browse Zillow/Redfin for homes $1M+ in our service area
2. Note the listing agents
3. Check their other listings (pattern of luxury focus?)
4. Research their recent sales history

**What to capture:**
- Agent handling luxury listings
- Repeat presence in high-end market
- Contact information
- Listing price range

---

### Method 5: Open House Research

**Process:**
1. Attend open houses in target neighborhoods
2. Meet agents in person (low-pressure introduction)
3. Collect business cards
4. Note which agents are active in premium areas

**What to capture:**
- Agent name and contact
- Property they were showing
- Brief conversation notes
- Follow-up opportunity

---

### Method 6: Brokerage Website Mining

**Process:**
1. Visit target brokerage websites
2. Navigate to "Our Agents" or team pages
3. Filter by location or specialty if available
4. Identify agents with luxury designations or high sales

**What to capture:**
- Agent roster
- Contact information
- Specialties noted
- Bio highlights (volume, awards, etc.)

---

### Priority Scoring System

**Assign points (0-5) for each factor:**

| Factor | 5 Points | 3 Points | 1 Point | 0 Points |
|--------|----------|----------|---------|----------|
| Avg Sale Price | $1M+ | $500K-$1M | $300K-$500K | Under $300K |
| Annual Volume | 50+ deals | 25-49 deals | 10-24 deals | Under 10 |
| Luxury Focus | Primary focus | Has luxury listings | Occasional | None |
| Location | Core areas (Gladwyne, etc.) | Secondary areas | Edge of service | Outside service |
| Brokerage | Luxury boutique | Major national | Regional | Discount |

**Total Score Interpretation:**
- 20-25: Tier 1 - Personal outreach immediately
- 15-19: Tier 2 - Priority email sequence + follow-up call
- 10-14: Tier 3 - Standard email sequence
- Under 10: Tier 4 - Low priority, include in mass outreach only

---

### Outreach Status Options

- **Not Started** - On target list, no contact yet
- **Email 1 Sent** - Initial outreach sent
- **In Sequence** - Going through email series
- **Replied - Positive** - Interested, schedule meeting
- **Replied - Questions** - Needs more info
- **Replied - Not Now** - Not ready, nurture
- **Meeting Scheduled** - Date/time set
- **Partner Active** - Signed up, can receive referrals
- **Not Interested** - Declined, remove from active outreach
- **No Response** - Completed sequence, no reply
