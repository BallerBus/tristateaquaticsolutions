### A2 --- The Value Email

**Send date:** Week of March 10
**Subject:** 5 things to check before your pool opens this spring
**Preview text:** A quick checklist from your pool team

---

Hey [First Name],

Spring is coming fast. Before you start thinking about opening day, here are five things worth checking now --- they'll save you time, money, and headaches when the season starts.

**1. Pool cover condition**
Walk around the perimeter and look for tears, excessive standing water, or heavy debris load. A damaged cover can let algae start growing before you even pull it off.

**2. Equipment inspection**
Take a look at your pump, filter, and heater connections. Winter brings critters looking for warm spots, and freeze-thaw cycles crack fittings. If anything looks cracked, chewed, or loose, flag it now.

**3. Deck and coping**
Those freeze-thaw cycles do a number on concrete and stone too. Walk the deck and look for new cracks, especially along the pool edge. Small cracks now become expensive repairs by July.

**4. Chemical supply check**
If you've got leftover chemicals from last season, most of them have degraded over the winter. Chlorine loses potency, test strips expire, and pH adjusters clump up. Start fresh --- it's not worth guessing with old product.

**5. Landscaping around the pool**
Trim back anything that grew over the winter. Overhanging branches and encroaching shrubs mean more leaves in your pool all season. Ten minutes with clippers now saves hours of skimming later.

### Compliance Notes

**CAN-SPAM (Email):**
- Include physical mailing address in every email footer
- Include a working unsubscribe link in every email
- Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
- Subject lines must not be deceptive
- "From" name and email must be accurate
- Identify the message as an ad if it is purely promotional (our emails are relationship-based, but the later urgency emails lean promotional --- include unsubscribe regardless)

**TCPA / A2P 10DLC (SMS):**
- Send SMS only to contacts who have explicitly opted in to receive text messages
- Maximum 4 promotional SMS per contact per month (this campaign sends max 3 total per segment --- compliant)
- Include "Reply STOP to opt out" in every SMS message
- Honor STOP replies immediately and automatically
- Send SMS only during reasonable hours (this campaign targets Tue--Thu 10am--2pm --- compliant)
- Ensure A2P 10DLC campaign registration is active before sending (see A2P-CAMPAIGN.md)

**General best practices:**
- Send Tuesday through Thursday, between 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM ET
- Never send on weekends or holidays
- Space email and SMS touches at least 24 hours apart when they fall in the same week
- If a contact books at any point, immediately suppress them from remaining campaign touches
- If a contact replies (even to decline), move them to manual follow-up --- remove from automated sequence
