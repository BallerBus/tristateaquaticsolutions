### Section 4: Myths vs. Reality (6 minutes)

**Slide 21: Fiberglass Myths**

**Myth:** "Fiberglass pools look cheap"
**Reality:** Modern fiberglass pools feature premium gel coat finishes, designer colors, and elegant designs indistinguishable from high-end concrete pools.

**Myth:** "Fiberglass pools are small"
**Reality:** Fiberglass pools are available up to 40+ feet long - larger than most residential backyards need.

**Myth:** "The gel coat will fade or crack"
**Reality:** Quality gel coats are UV-resistant and carry 25+ year warranties. Cracking is extremely rare with proper installation.

**Slide 22: Concrete Myths**

**Myth:** "Concrete pools are indestructible"
**Reality:** Concrete pools require resurfacing every 10-15 years, and the porous surface requires more chemical treatment and maintenance.

**Myth:** "Concrete is always better quality"
**Reality:** Quality depends on the builder, not the pool type. A well-installed fiberglass pool can outlast a poorly-built concrete pool.

**Myth:** "Concrete pools last longer"
**Reality:** Both can last 50+ years with proper maintenance. Fiberglass shells often require less intervention over their lifespan.

## Engagement Tactics

### During Presentation

**Poll Questions (2-3 per webinar):**
- "What type of pool are you most interested in?" (Multiple choice)
- "When are you hoping to have a pool installed?" (Multiple choice)
- "What's your biggest concern about pool installation?" (Multiple choice)

**Chat Prompts:**
- "Drop your city/neighborhood in the chat - let's see where everyone is joining from!"
- "Type YES if you've already started getting quotes"
- "What feature is most important to you? Type your answer!"

**Q&A Management:**
- Address questions by name ("Great question, Sarah...")
- Save complex questions for dedicated Q&A time
- Upvote system if platform supports it
- "We're getting a lot of questions about X - let me address that..."

### Keeping Attention

**Pacing:**
- Change slides every 1-2 minutes
- Use images heavily (less text)
- Tell stories and give examples
- Vary tone and energy
- Move around if standing

**Audience Involvement:**
- Ask rhetorical questions
- "Raise your hand if..." (virtual hand raise)
- "Type in chat if you agree..."
- Reference their poll answers

**Energy Management:**
- Start strong (set the tone)
- Build to most valuable content
- End with clear call to action
- Leave time for Q&A (don't rush)
