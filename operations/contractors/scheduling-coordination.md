### Critical Path Analysis

#### Critical Path Items
These items directly affect project completion date:

1. **Permits** - Cannot start until approved
2. **Excavation** - All trades depend on this
3. **Shell (gunite/shotcrete)** - Long cure time
4. **Tile and coping** - Must complete before deck
5. **Decking** - Must complete before equipment
6. **Interior finish** - Must complete before fill
7. **Final inspection** - Required for handover

#### Float Activities
These items can shift within constraints:

- Fence (can install after barrier inspection point)
- Landscape (can install after fence)
- Some electrical finishing
- Equipment mounting details

### Trade Dependencies Matrix

| Trade | Depends On | Must Complete Before |
|-------|------------|---------------------|

| Tile | Shell cure | Coping |

| Equipment | Deck cure | Finish, Start-up |
| Plumbing (final) | Equipment | System test |
| Electrical (final) | Equipment | System test |
| Finish | All equipment | Fill, Start-up |
| Fence | Deck | Final inspection |
| Landscape | Fence | Final cleanup |

### Issue Escalation Protocol

#### Escalation Levels

| Level | Issue Type | First Contact | Response Time | Escalate To |
|-------|------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Minor coordination | PM | 2 hours | - |
| 2 | Schedule impact | PM | 1 hour | Ops Manager |
| 3 | Quality/Safety concern | PM immediately | 30 minutes | Ops Manager |
| 4 | Work stoppage | PM + Ops Manager | Immediate | Owner |
| 5 | Emergency/Injury | 911 then PM | Immediate | All levels |

#### Escalation Process

**Level 1 Issues:**
- Coordination questions
- Minor material needs
- Small scope clarifications
- Process: Contact PM, resolve same day

**Level 2 Issues:**
- Potential schedule delays
- Equipment breakdowns
- Weather impacts
- Process: Contact PM, document, plan mitigation

**Level 3 Issues:**
- Quality deficiencies discovered
- Safety concerns observed
- Inspection failures
- Process: Stop work if needed, contact PM immediately, document

**Level 4 Issues:**
- Major schedule impacts
- Significant disputes
- Contract issues
- Process: Contact PM and Ops Manager, meeting within 24 hours

**Level 5 Issues:**
- Injury requiring medical attention
- Property damage
- Safety emergency
- Process: Address emergency, then report up chain

### Customer Communication

#### Rules for Subcontractor-Customer Communication

**DO:**
- Be polite and professional
- Answer basic questions about your work
- Direct questions about project to PM
- Keep noise/disruption to minimum
- Respect property and privacy

**DO NOT:**
- Discuss pricing or costs
- Make promises about schedule
- Agree to additional work
- Discuss other trades' work
- Enter home unless necessary and permitted
- Use customer's utilities without permission

**Customer Concerns:**
1. Listen politely
2. Acknowledge their concern
3. State you'll notify the PM
4. Contact PM immediately
5. Document conversation

## Delay Handling

### Delay Categories

#### Excusable Delays (Not Subcontractor's Fault)

| Category | Examples | Documentation Required |
|----------|----------|----------------------|
| Weather | Rain, extreme heat/cold, wind | Weather logs, photos |
| Owner-caused | Design changes, access issues | Written record |
| TSAS-caused | Late materials, prior trade delays | PM acknowledgment |
| Force majeure | Natural disaster, pandemic | Official sources |
| Inspection | Failed inspection (not sub's fault) | Inspection report |

#### Non-Excusable Delays (Subcontractor's Responsibility)

| Category | Examples | Consequences |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Crew no-show | Workers didn't arrive | Back-charge for delays |
| Equipment failure | Sub's equipment broke | Sub's responsibility |
| Material shortage | Sub failed to order | Sub's responsibility |
| Poor workmanship | Rework required | Sub's responsibility |
| Coordination failure | Sub didn't communicate | Sub's responsibility |

### Delay Notification Requirements

#### Immediate Notification (Within 2 Hours)

**Required For:**
- Crew will not arrive as scheduled
- Equipment breakdown preventing work
- Safety concern stopping work
- Discovery of unforeseen condition

**Notification Method:**
1. Phone call to PM (primary)
2. Text message (backup/confirmation)
3. Email for documentation

**Required Information:**
- Nature of delay
- Expected duration
- Impact on schedule
- Mitigation measures

### Delay Recovery Procedures

#### Immediate Actions

| Delay Cause | Immediate Action |
|-------------|------------------|
| Weather | Protect work, reschedule |
| Prior trade | Identify partial work possible |
| Material | Source alternatives |
| Equipment | Rent replacement |
| Crew | Call in additional workers |
| Inspection fail | Schedule re-inspection |

#### Recovery Planning Meeting

**When Required:**
- Delay exceeds 2 days
- Multiple trades affected
- Critical path impacted

**Attendees:**
- PM
- Affected subcontractors
- TSAS Operations (if major)

**Agenda:**
1. Review delay cause
2. Assess current status
3. Identify recovery options
4. Assign responsibilities
5. Set new milestones
6. Document decisions

## Multi-Trade Coordination

### Coordination Meeting Structure

#### Pre-Construction Meeting

**Timing:** Before project starts
**Attendees:** PM, all subcontractors, TSAS Ops
**Duration:** 1-2 hours

**Agenda:**
1. Project overview and timeline
2. Scope review by trade
3. Schedule walkthrough
4. Site logistics (access, parking, staging)
5. Communication protocols
6. Safety requirements
7. Quality expectations
8. Questions and concerns

**Outputs:**
- Confirmed schedule
- Contact list
- Site logistics plan
- Coordination issues identified

#### Weekly Coordination Meeting

**Timing:** Same day/time each week
**Attendees:** PM, active subcontractors
**Duration:** 30-60 minutes

**Agenda:**
1. Last week's progress review
2. Issues and resolutions
3. Next week's look-ahead
4. Material status
5. Inspection schedule
6. Coordination needs
7. Safety items

**Format Options:**
- In-person on site
- Phone/video conference
- Combination

### Conflict Resolution

#### Common Conflicts and Solutions

| Conflict | Cause | Solution |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Schedule overlap | Poor coordination | Pre-planning, communication |
| Space competition | Limited staging | Assigned areas, JIT delivery |
| Resource sharing | Tools, equipment | Prior arrangement |
| Sequence disputes | Both want to go first | Critical path priority |
| Quality finger-pointing | Unclear responsibility | Interface documentation |

#### Conflict Resolution Process

**Step 1: Immediate Discussion**
- Trades discuss issue on site
- Seek mutually agreeable solution
- If resolved, document and proceed

**Step 2: PM Involvement**
- Contact PM if unresolved
- PM facilitates discussion
- PM makes decision if needed
- Document resolution

**Step 3: Escalation**
- If PM cannot resolve, escalate to Ops Manager
- Formal meeting with affected parties
- Binding decision issued
- Document for future reference
