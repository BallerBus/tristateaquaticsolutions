## Quality Standards

### General Quality Requirements

#### Workmanship Standards
- All work shall be performed in a professional, workmanlike manner
- Work shall conform to industry best practices
- Work shall meet or exceed manufacturer specifications
- Work shall comply with all applicable codes and standards

#### Material Standards
- All materials shall be new unless otherwise specified
- Materials shall meet or exceed specifications
- Substitutions require written approval
- Material submittals required before installation

### Trade-Specific Quality Standards

#### Excavation
- **Dimensional Tolerance:** +/- 2 inches from plan dimensions
- **Grade Tolerance:** +/- 1 inch from specified elevations
- **Slope:** Per plan specifications, maximum variance 1%
- **Compaction:** Minimum 95% standard proctor for backfill
- **Cleanliness:** No debris in excavation

#### Electrical
- **Code Compliance:** NEC and local codes
- **Bonding:** Continuous bonding grid, tested
- **Connections:** All connections accessible and weatherproof
- **Labeling:** All circuits labeled at panel
- **Testing:** Continuity and resistance testing documented

#### Plumbing
- **Pressure Test:** Hold pressure without loss for specified duration
- **Slope:** Minimum 1/4" per foot for drainage
- **Support:** Pipe supported per code requirements
- **Connections:** Leak-free, properly torqued
- **Cleanliness:** Lines flushed before startup

#### Concrete/Decking
- **Strength:** Meet specified PSI at 28 days
- **Finish:** Uniform appearance, no cold joints
- **Levelness:** Maximum 1/4" in 10 feet
- **Joints:** Straight, properly spaced
- **Curing:** Proper curing methods applied

#### Fencing
- **Plumb:** Posts within 1/4" of vertical
- **Level:** Top rail level within 1/4" in 10 feet
- **Spacing:** Consistent picket spacing
- **Hardware:** All hardware properly installed and functional
- **Code:** Meet all barrier code requirements

#### Landscaping
- **Plant Health:** Healthy, disease-free plants
- **Installation:** Plants at proper depth
- **Grading:** Positive drainage away from structures
- **Coverage:** Mulch at specified depth
- **Irrigation:** Full coverage, no dry spots

### Quality Documentation

#### Required Submissions
1. **Material Submittals:** Before installation
2. **Shop Drawings:** If applicable
3. **Test Reports:** As required
4. **As-Built Drawings:** At completion
5. **Warranties:** Manufacturer and workmanship

#### Photo Documentation
- Before conditions
- Progress photos (daily minimum)
- Covered work (before covering)
- Completed work
- Any deficiencies and corrections

### Inspection and Testing

#### Self-Inspection
- Subcontractor inspects own work
- Document deficiencies
- Correct before requesting TSAS inspection

#### TSAS Inspection
- Scheduled with PM
- Minimum 24-hour notice
- Inspection report provided
- Deficiencies documented

#### Third-Party Testing
- When required by specifications
- Subcontractor coordinates
- Results submitted to PM

### Non-Conformance Process

#### Deficiency Identification
1. Document with photos
2. Written notice to subcontractor
3. Define correction requirements
4. Set correction deadline

#### Correction Requirements
- Correct within specified timeframe
- Re-inspection required
- Document correction
- No additional cost to TSAS

#### Failure to Correct
1. Second notice with shorter deadline
2. TSAS may correct with back-charge
3. Potential termination for cause
4. Impact on future work

## Safety Requirements

### General Safety Requirements

#### Compliance
- OSHA regulations (29 CFR 1926 - Construction)
- State and local safety regulations
- TSAS safety policies
- Manufacturer safety guidelines

#### Safety Program
Subcontractors must have a written safety program including:
- Hazard communication
- Personal protective equipment
- Fall protection
- Excavation safety (if applicable)
- Electrical safety (if applicable)
- Emergency procedures

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

#### Minimum Requirements (All Sites)
| PPE | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| Hard hat | ANSI Z89.1 compliant |
| Safety glasses | ANSI Z87.1 compliant |
| Work boots | Steel/composite toe recommended |
| High-visibility vest | Class 2 minimum |
| Gloves | Task-appropriate |

#### Task-Specific Requirements
| Task | Additional PPE |
|------|----------------|
| Excavation | Hard hat, hearing protection |
| Concrete | Rubber boots, chemical gloves, eye protection |
| Electrical | Arc-rated clothing, insulated gloves, face shield |
| Welding | Welding helmet, leather gloves, leather apron |
| Working at height | Full body harness, lanyard |

### Site Safety Rules

#### General Rules
1. No alcohol or drugs on site
2. No horseplay
3. Maintain clean work area
4. Report all injuries immediately
5. Report all near-misses
6. Follow traffic/pedestrian controls
7. Respect barricades and warning signs

#### Hot Work
- Permit required
- Fire extinguisher on site
- Fire watch during and 30 minutes after
- Clear combustibles from area

#### Confined Space Entry
- Permit required
- Atmospheric testing
- Trained entrant/attendant
- Rescue plan in place

### Excavation Safety

#### Requirements per OSHA 1926 Subpart P
1. **Competent Person:** Required on all excavations
2. **Utility Locates:** Complete before digging
3. **Soil Classification:** Document soil type
4. **Protective Systems:**
   - Sloping/benching for Type C soil
   - Shoring/shielding as required
   - Spoils 2+ feet from edge

#### Excavation Checklist
- [ ] Competent person designated
- [ ] Utilities located and marked
- [ ] Soil classified
- [ ] Protective system in place
- [ ] Safe access (ladder within 25 feet)
- [ ] Daily inspection documented

### Electrical Safety

#### Requirements
1. **Lockout/Tagout:** Before working on energized equipment
2. **Qualified Workers:** Only qualified electricians work on electrical
3. **Arc Flash:** PPE per hazard analysis
4. **GFCI:** Required for all temporary power
5. **Inspection:** Cords and equipment inspected daily

### Fall Protection

#### Requirements (6 feet or higher)
1. **Guardrails:** Preferred method
2. **Safety Nets:** Where guardrails impractical
3. **Personal Fall Arrest:** Harness and lanyard
4. **Training:** Required for all workers at height

### Safety Violations

#### Violation Categories
| Category | Examples | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Minor | PPE violation, housekeeping | Verbal warning, correct immediately |
| Serious | No fall protection, unsafe excavation | Written warning, work stoppage |
| Willful | Repeated violations, endangering others | Removal from site, potential termination |

#### Progressive Discipline
1. **First Minor Violation:** Verbal warning
2. **Second Minor Violation:** Written warning
3. **Third Minor Violation:** Removal from site
4. **Any Serious Violation:** Immediate removal pending investigation
5. **Willful Violation:** Permanent removal from all TSAS projects
