## Channel Preferences

### Communication Channels by Type

| Communication Type | Primary Channel | Backup Channel |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Urgent/Emergency | Phone call | Text |
| Schedule updates | Text | Email |
| Daily progress | Text with photos | Email |
| Formal notifications | Email | Certified mail |
| Weekly summaries | Email | Customer portal |
| Billing/Invoices | Email | Mail |
| Warranty information | Email | Mail |
| Marketing/Promotions | Email | Direct mail |

### Customer Preference Collection

At contract signing, collect and record:
- Preferred communication channel (call/text/email)
- Best time to reach customer
- Secondary contact information
- Communication frequency preference (more/standard/less)
- Special considerations (work schedule, etc.)

### Channel Guidelines

#### Phone Calls
- Best for: Urgent matters, complex discussions, complaints
- Hours: 8 AM - 6 PM local time (unless emergency)
- Always leave voicemail if no answer
- Follow up with text if no response within 4 hours
- Document all calls in project file

#### Text Messages
- Best for: Quick updates, daily progress, scheduling
- Keep messages concise (under 160 characters when possible)
- Include photos when relevant
- Respond to customer texts within 2 hours during business hours
- Use professional language (no emojis except üëç for confirmation)

#### Email
- Best for: Formal documentation, detailed updates, attachments
- Use templates for consistency
- Include project reference in subject line
- Respond within 24 hours (same day preferred)
- CC relevant team members as needed

### Response Time Standards

| Channel | Business Hours | After Hours |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Phone (urgent) | 30 minutes | 1 hour |
| Phone (routine) | 4 hours | Next business day |
| Text | 2 hours | Next morning |
| Email | 8 hours | Next business day |

## Photography Requirements

### Daily Photo Documentation

#### Minimum Requirements
- **Quantity:** 10+ photos per work day
- **Resolution:** 4 megapixels minimum
- **Format:** JPEG preferred

#### Required Shots (Daily)

1. **Overall site view** - Wide shot showing entire work area
2. **Work in progress** - Close-up of current day's work
3. **Detail shots** - Specific elements (connections, measurements, etc.)
4. **Safety setup** - Barriers, signage, safety measures
5. **Material staging** - Materials on-site
6. **End of day** - Site secured and cleaned

#### Phase-Specific Requirements

**Excavation:**
- Before excavation begins
- During excavation (multiple depths)
- Completed excavation from each side
- Soil conditions
- Depth measurements with tape measure visible

**Steel & Plumbing:**
- Steel layout before installation
- Installed steel with spacing visible
- Plumbing runs (all lines)
- Connections and fittings
- Pressure test gauge

**Shell:**
- Before gunite application
- During application
- Completed shell (all surfaces)
- Thickness measurements

**Tile & Coping:**
- Before installation
- Pattern layout
- Installed tile (close-up and overall)
- Coping installation
- Completed work

**Decking:**
- Forms and preparation
- During pour
- Finished surface
- Drainage slope
- Expansion joints

**Equipment:**
- Equipment pad prepared
- Each piece installed
- Plumbing connections
- Electrical connections
- Completed pad

### Photo Quality Standards

**Do:**
- Include reference scale when showing dimensions
- Capture from multiple angles
- Ensure good lighting
- Keep lens clean
- Hold camera steady
- Include date/time stamp

**Don't:**
- Take blurry or dark photos
- Miss critical details
- Include personal information in frame
- Take photos with fingers over lens
- Miss required daily shots

### Photo Organization

**File Naming Convention:**
`[ProjectID]_[Date]_[Phase]_[Sequence]`
Example: `SMI-2026-001_20260215_Steel_003.jpg`

**Folder Structure:**
```
/Projects/[ProjectID]/Photos/
  /Pre-Construction/
  /Excavation/
  /Steel-Plumbing/
  /Shell/
  /Tile-Coping/
  /Decking/
  /Equipment/
  /Final/
```

### Customer Photo Sharing

**Daily Text Updates:**
- 2-3 best photos showing progress
- Quick selection showing "before" vs. "after" for day

**Weekly Email:**
- 5-10 curated photos

**Milestone Updates:**

## Complaint Handling Procedure

### Complaint Categories

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Quality | Workmanship concerns | Uneven surface, visible defects |
| Communication | Information issues | Missed updates, unclear info |
| Schedule | Timeline concerns | Delays, missed dates |
| Behavior | Conduct issues | Crew behavior, property respect |
| Billing | Payment issues | Incorrect charges, disputes |
| Safety | Safety concerns | Hazards, incidents |

### Response Time Standards

| Severity | Definition | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|----------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Safety, major damage | 1 hour | 24 hours |
| High | Work stoppage, major complaint | 4 hours | 48 hours |
| Medium | Quality concern, schedule issue | 8 hours | 5 business days |
| Low | Minor concern, suggestion | 24 hours | 10 business days |

### Complaint Handling Process

#### Step 1: Receive and Document (Immediate)

1. Listen actively without interrupting
2. Take detailed notes
3. Express empathy and concern
4. Thank customer for bringing it up
5. Confirm understanding by restating
6. Provide case number and timeline
7. Log in complaint tracking system

**Key Phrases:**
- "I understand how frustrating this must be."
- "Thank you for bringing this to our attention."
- "I want to make sure I understand correctly..."
- "Let me tell you exactly what will happen next."

#### Step 2: Investigate (Per Timeline)

1. Gather all relevant information
2. Review photos, logs, communications
3. Interview crew members if needed
4. Consult with supervisors
5. Identify root cause
6. Develop resolution options
7. Get approval for resolution

**Documentation Required:**
- Complaint details and date
- Investigation steps taken
- Findings
- Root cause analysis
- Resolution options considered
- Approved resolution

#### Step 3: Resolve and Communicate

1. Contact customer with findings
2. Present resolution clearly
3. Get agreement on resolution
4. Implement resolution per agreed timeline
5. Verify customer satisfaction
6. Document resolution

**Resolution Communication:**
- Be honest about what happened
- Don't blame or make excuses
- Focus on the solution
- Be specific about next steps
- Follow through exactly as promised

#### Step 4: Follow-Up

1. Check in 24-48 hours after resolution
2. Confirm satisfaction
3. Ask if anything else needed
4. Thank customer again
5. Update case as closed
6. Complete lessons learned

### Escalation Triggers

Escalate to next level if:
- Customer requests escalation
- Initial resolution rejected
- Issue involves safety or legal concerns
- Issue involves potential damage claims
- Complaint goes unresolved past target
- Social media or public posting
