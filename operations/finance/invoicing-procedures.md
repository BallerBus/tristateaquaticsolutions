## Invoice Templates

### Standard Invoice Elements

Every invoice must include:

#### Header Information
- Company logo and name
- Company address, phone, email
- Invoice number (sequential)
- Invoice date
- Due date
- Customer billing information
- Project/job address (if different)
- Project number/reference

#### Body Information
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity and unit price (where applicable)
- Line item totals
- Subtotal
- Tax (if applicable)
- Credits/payments applied
- Total due

#### Footer Information
- Payment terms
- Accepted payment methods
- Late payment policy
- Contact for questions
- "Thank you for your business"

### Invoice Numbering System

**Format:** YYYY-XXXXX

- YYYY = Year
- XXXXX = Sequential number (resets each year)

**Examples:**
- 2026-00001 (first invoice of 2026)
- 2026-00156 (156th invoice of 2026)

## Progress Billing Process

### Billing Milestone Triggers

| Milestone | Trigger Event | Documentation Required |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Deposit | Contract signed | Signed contract |
| Pre-Construction | Permits approved, excavation scheduled | Permit copy, schedule confirmation |
| Shell Set | Pool set and plumbed, passed inspection | Inspection report, photos |
| Substantial Completion | Deck complete, equipment running | Inspection report, photos |
| Final | Walkthrough completed, punch list done | Signed completion certificate |

### Progress Billing Workflow

#### Step 1: Verify Milestone Completion
- [ ] Work for milestone is complete
- [ ] Quality check performed
- [ ] Required inspections passed
- [ ] Photos taken for documentation
- [ ] Project manager sign-off

#### Step 2: Generate Invoice
- [ ] Create invoice in accounting system
- [ ] Verify customer information
- [ ] Apply correct amounts from contract
- [ ] Include any approved change orders
- [ ] Review for accuracy

#### Step 3: Distribute Invoice
- [ ] Email to customer (primary)
- [ ] Mail hard copy if requested
- [ ] Copy to project file
- [ ] Note in CRM
- [ ] Update AR aging

#### Step 4: Follow Up
- [ ] Send reminder 2 days before due
- [ ] Call if not received by due date
- [ ] Escalate per collection procedures if needed

### Financing Partner Billing

For financed projects:

1. **Complete milestone work**
2. **Generate completion certificate**
   - Description of work completed
   - Percentage of contract complete
   - Amount requested
   - Customer signature required
3. **Submit to lender portal**
   - Upload completion certificate
   - Attach photos
   - Include inspection reports
4. **Monitor funding**
   - Track funding status
   - Follow up if delayed
   - Notify customer of funding

### Progress Billing Best Practices

- Invoice promptly upon milestone completion
- Don't let invoices "stack up"
- Maintain consistent communication with customer
- Document everything photographically
- Get customer acknowledgment of completion
- Keep detailed notes in project file

## Change Order Billing

### Change Order Invoice Timing

| Change Order Size | Billing Timing |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Under $1,000 | Add to next progress invoice |
| $1,000 - $5,000 | Invoice with next milestone or separately |
| Over $5,000 | Invoice immediately, payment before work |

### Change Order Invoice Requirements

Every change order invoice must reference:
- Original contract number/date
- Change order number
- Change order approval date
- Description of additional work
- Breakdown of costs (labor, materials, etc.)
- Customer authorization signature

## Final Billing Checklist

### Pre-Final Invoice Checklist

Before generating final invoice, verify:

#### Construction Completion
- [ ] All contracted work complete
- [ ] All change order work complete
- [ ] Punch list items resolved
- [ ] Final grading/cleanup done
- [ ] All materials removed from site

#### Documentation
- [ ] All inspections passed (building, electrical, etc.)
- [ ] Final inspection signed off
- [ ] Photos of completed project
- [ ] Equipment manuals delivered
- [ ] Warranty cards registered
- [ ] As-built drawings provided (if applicable)

#### Customer Walkthrough
- [ ] Walkthrough scheduled and completed
- [ ] Customer orientation performed
- [ ] Operation instructions provided
- [ ] Maintenance schedule reviewed
- [ ] Questions answered
- [ ] Completion certificate signed

#### Financial
- [ ] All previous invoices paid
- [ ] All change orders documented
- [ ] Final contract amount verified
- [ ] Lien waivers from subs obtained
- [ ] Ready to provide final lien waiver

### Final Invoice Procedure

1. **Complete all checklist items above**

2. **Calculate final amount**
   ```
   Original Contract Amount
   + Approved Change Orders
   - Payments Received
   = Final Amount Due
   ```

3. **Generate comprehensive final invoice**
   - Include full project summary
   - List all billing history
   - Show all payments received
   - Calculate final balance

4. **Send invoice with cover letter**
   - Thank customer for their business
   - Summarize project
   - Explain warranty activation
   - Include maintenance tips

5. **Deliver final documents**
   - Equipment manuals
   - Warranty information
   - Permit closeout copies
   - Maintenance schedule
   - Emergency contact information

### Post-Final Payment Actions

Upon receipt of final payment:

1. **Verify funds have cleared**
2. **Provide unconditional lien waiver**
3. **Register all warranties**
4. **Send warranty confirmation to customer**
5. **Request review/referral**
6. **Close out project in system**
7. **Archive project file**
8. **Schedule follow-up call (30 days)**

## Collections Procedures

### Accounts Receivable Aging

| Age | Status | Action Required |
|-----|--------|-----------------|
| Current | On time | No action |
| 1-15 days | Grace period | Friendly reminder |
| 16-30 days | Past due | Formal follow-up |
| 31-60 days | Seriously past due | Collection efforts |
| 61-90 days | Default | Legal consideration |
| 90+ days | Bad debt risk | Legal action/write-off |

### Collections Timeline

#### Current - Day 0 (Invoice Issued)
- Invoice sent via email
- Hard copy mailed if requested
- Due date clearly stated

#### Day 1 - Reminder
- Automated reminder email
- "Your invoice is now due"

#### Day 5 - Grace Period Warning
- Phone call if payment not received
- Email with late fee warning
- Confirm receipt of invoice

#### Day 6 - Late Fee Applied
- 1.5% late fee added
- Work suspended if ongoing
- Updated invoice sent

#### Day 15 - Formal Collection
- Formal demand letter sent
- Certified mail with return receipt
- 10-day cure period given

#### Day 30 - Final Demand
- Final demand letter
- Lien notice if applicable
- Account flagged for legal review

#### Day 45 - Legal Preparation
- Attorney consultation
- Mechanics lien filed (if applicable)
- Collection agency consideration

#### Day 60+ - Legal Action
- File suit or
- Assign to collection agency
- Report to credit bureaus

### Payment Arrangement Guidelines

If customer requests payment plan:

**Eligibility:**
- Account must be under 60 days past due
- Customer must be in good faith communication
- Minimum 25% immediate payment required
- Balance within 90 days

**Payment Plan Terms:**
- Interest continues to accrue (1.5%/month)
- Specific due dates for each payment
- Automatic default if payment missed
- Must be documented in writing

**Payment Plan Agreement Template:**
