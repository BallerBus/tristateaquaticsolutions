## Standard Payment Milestones

### Standard Pool Installation (4-Payment Schedule)

| Milestone | Percentage | Timing | Triggers Work |
|-----------|------------|--------|---------------|
| Deposit | 15% | At contract signing | Permits & scheduling |
| Pre-Construction | 35% | Before excavation begins | Excavation & delivery |
| Shell Set | 35% | After pool is set and plumbed | Electrical, backfill, deck |
| Final | 15% | At completion and walkthrough | Warranty activation |

**Total: 100%**

### Large Projects ($150,000+) (5-Payment Schedule)

| Milestone | Percentage | Timing | Triggers Work |
|-----------|------------|--------|---------------|
| Deposit | 10% | At contract signing | Design finalization |
| Design Approval | 15% | Design/engineering complete | Permits & ordering |
| Pre-Construction | 25% | Before excavation begins | Excavation & delivery |
| Shell Set | 30% | After pool is set and plumbed | Electrical, backfill, deck |
| Substantial Completion | 15% | Deck complete, equipment running | Punch list items |
| Final | 5% | Final walkthrough approved | Warranty activation |

**Total: 100%**

### Service/Renovation Projects

| Project Size | Payment Structure |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Under $5,000 | 50% deposit, 50% at completion |
| $5,000 - $15,000 | 40% deposit, 30% midpoint, 30% completion |
| $15,000 - $50,000 | Standard 4-payment schedule |
| Over $50,000 | Large project 5-payment schedule |

### Equipment-Only Sales

| Sale Type | Payment Terms |
|-----------|---------------|
| In-Stock Equipment | Payment in full at purchase |
| Special Order | 50% deposit, 50% at delivery |
| Installed Equipment | 50% deposit, 50% at installation complete |

### Financing Process

#### Step 1: Pre-Qualification
1. Discuss financing options during sales presentation
2. Provide customer with rate sheet and program comparison
3. Customer selects preferred lender
4. Assist with pre-qualification application

#### Step 2: Application
1. Customer completes full application online
2. Lender reviews and provides decision
3. Loan documents sent to customer
4. Customer signs and returns documents

#### Step 3: Funding
1. Lender funds based on milestone schedule
2. We submit completion certificates for each milestone
3. Funds deposited within 2-5 business days per milestone

#### Step 4: Documentation Required

For each funding request:
- Signed completion certificate
- Photos of completed work
- Customer signature confirming milestone
- Updated invoice showing payments

### Financing Considerations

**When to Recommend Financing:**
- Customer hesitant on larger package
- Cash flow constraints mentioned
- Multi-feature project with upgrades
- Comparison shopping with lower-priced competitors

**Financing Sales Tips:**
- Present monthly payment alongside total price
- "For only $XXX/month, you could add the spa"
- Emphasize no equity required (vs. HELOC)
- Quick approval process highlight

**Do Not:**
- Guarantee approval or specific rates
- Advise on customer's financial decisions
- Pressure customers into financing
- Provide tax or financial advice

## Payment Collection Procedures

### Accepted Payment Methods

| Method | Accepted For | Fee to Customer |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| Check | All payments | None |
| ACH/Bank Transfer | All payments | None |
| Wire Transfer | Large payments ($25K+) | None |
| Credit Card | Deposits and payments under $5,000 | 3% convenience fee |
| Cash | Deposits under $2,000 | None |
| Certified Check | Final payments, lien releases | None |

### Collection Timeline

#### Before Each Milestone

**5 Days Before:**
- Send payment reminder email with invoice
- Include milestone description and amount due
- Provide payment instructions

**Day Before:**
- Phone call reminder if payment not received
- Confirm payment method and timing

**Day Of:**
- Verify payment received before work begins
- No work proceeds until payment clears

### Payment Processing

#### Check Payments
1. Record check number, amount, date received
2. Deposit within 24 hours
3. Allow 3 business days for clearance
4. Hold work until funds clear for new customers

#### ACH/Bank Transfers
1. Provide routing and account information securely
2. Confirm receipt in accounting system
3. Send receipt to customer within 24 hours

#### Credit Card Payments
1. Process through Square/payment terminal
2. Add 3% processing fee to invoice
3. Provide receipt immediately
4. Funds typically available next business day

### Invoice Timing

| Event | Invoice Issued |
|-------|----------------|
| Contract Signing | Same day |
| Pre-Construction | 7 days before scheduled start |
| Each Milestone | Upon milestone completion |
| Change Orders | Within 48 hours of approval |
| Final | Upon substantial completion |

### Documentation Requirements

For every payment:
1. Issue receipt within 24 hours
2. Update project accounting file
3. Note payment in CRM
4. Apply to correct invoice/milestone

## Late Payment Handling

### Grace Period Structure

| Payment Type | Grace Period | Late Fee |
|--------------|--------------|----------|
| Deposit | None - required for scheduling | N/A |
| Progress Payments | 5 business days | 1.5% of amount due |
| Final Payment | 10 business days | 1.5% of amount due |

### Escalation Process

#### Day 1-5 (Grace Period)
- Payment reminder email sent Day 1
- Phone call on Day 3
- Second email on Day 5 with late fee warning

#### Day 6-14 (Initial Collection)
- Late fee applied (1.5%)
- Work suspended if ongoing
- Written notice of late payment sent
- Daily interest begins accruing (1.5% monthly = 18% APR)

#### Day 15-30 (Formal Collection)
- Formal demand letter sent (certified mail)
- Account placed on hold
- Project completion suspended
- Collection calls every 3 days

#### Day 31-60 (Pre-Legal)
- Final demand letter with 10-day cure period
- Lien notice preparation (if applicable)
- Attorney consultation
- Credit reporting warning

#### Day 61+ (Legal Action)
- File mechanics lien (if within deadline)
- Engage collection agency OR
- Pursue small claims/civil court
- Report to credit bureaus

### Communication Templates

**Day 1 - Friendly Reminder:**
```
Subject: Payment Reminder - [Project Name]

Dear [Customer Name],

This is a friendly reminder that payment of $[Amount] for [Milestone]
is now due. Please remit payment at your earliest convenience.

Payment can be made via [methods].

Thank you for your business!
```

**Day 5 - Grace Period Ending:**
```
Subject: Payment Past Due - Action Required

Dear [Customer Name],

Your payment of $[Amount] for [Milestone] is now past due. To avoid
a late fee of $[Fee Amount], please remit payment within the next
24 hours.

If you have already sent payment, please disregard this notice.

Please contact us if you have questions or need to discuss payment
arrangements.
```

**Day 15 - Formal Notice:**
```
Subject: FORMAL NOTICE - Past Due Account

Dear [Customer Name],

Your account is now seriously past due. As of today, you owe:

Original Amount: $[Amount]
Late Fee: $[Fee]
Total Due: $[Total]

Work on your project has been suspended until payment is received.
Interest will continue to accrue at 1.5% per month.

Please contact our office immediately to resolve this matter and
avoid further collection action.
```

### Payment Arrangements

When customers cannot pay in full:

**Criteria for Payment Plans:**
- Customer in good standing prior to issue
- Documented financial hardship
- Minimum 25% immediate payment
- Balance paid within 90 days
- Interest continues to accrue
- Manager approval required

**Payment Plan Agreement Must Include:**
- Total amount owed
- Payment schedule with specific dates
- Consequence of missed payments
- Interest/late fee terms
- Signed by customer

## Lien Rights Information

### Ohio Mechanics Lien Law Overview

**Who Can File:**
- General contractors
- Subcontractors
- Material suppliers
- Equipment rental companies

**Protected Property:**
- Residential and commercial real estate
- Improvements to land

### Timeline Requirements (Ohio)

| Action | Deadline | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Notice of Furnishing | Within 21 days of first work | Subcontractors/suppliers |
| Affidavit for Mechanic's Lien | 60 days from last work | File with county recorder |
| Lawsuit to Enforce | 6 years from filing | Must file to enforce |

### Our Lien Filing Process

#### Step 1: Documentation Throughout Project
- Detailed daily logs of work performed
- Material delivery receipts
- Photos with timestamps
- Signed milestone completions
- All correspondence

#### Step 2: Pre-Lien Notice (If Required)
- Send preliminary notice within 21 days of first work
- Certified mail, return receipt requested
- Keep copy of notice and proof of mailing

#### Step 3: Lien Affidavit Preparation
Required information:
- Property legal description
- Property owner name and address
- Our company information
- Amount owed
- Description of work/materials provided
- First and last date of work
- Notarized signature

#### Step 4: Filing
1. Complete lien affidavit
2. Have notarized
3. File with county recorder
4. Serve copy on property owner (certified mail)
5. Record filing information

### Lien Release Process

When payment is received after lien filed:

1. Verify funds have cleared
2. Prepare lien release document
3. Have notarized
4. File with county recorder (same county as lien)
5. Provide copy to property owner
6. Record release information

### Lien Waiver Forms

**Conditional Waiver (Progress Payment):**
Used for progress payments; waives lien rights only for amount and through date specified, conditioned on actual receipt of payment.

**Unconditional Waiver (Progress Payment):**
Used after payment has cleared; unconditionally waives lien rights for amount and through date specified.

**Conditional Waiver (Final Payment):**
Used with final payment; waives all lien rights conditioned on receipt of final payment.

**Unconditional Waiver (Final Payment):**
Used after final payment clears; unconditionally waives all lien rights.

### Important Lien Notes

1. **Always** provide appropriate waiver with each payment received
2. **Never** provide unconditional waiver before payment clears
3. **Document** all waiver exchanges in project file
4. **Consult attorney** before filing lien if situation is complex
5. **Time is critical** - missing deadlines loses lien rights
