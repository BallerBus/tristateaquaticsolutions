## Employment Policies

### Employment Classification

#### Full-Time Employees
- Work 40 hours or more per week
- Eligible for full benefits package
- Subject to all company policies

#### Part-Time Employees
- Work less than 40 hours per week
- May be eligible for limited benefits
- Subject to all company policies

#### Seasonal Employees
- Employed during peak season (April-September)
- Hours vary based on business needs
- May transition to permanent status based on performance

#### Independent Contractors
- Engaged for specific projects or specialized services
- Not eligible for employee benefits
- Must comply with relevant company policies while on-site

### Equal Employment Opportunity

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic under federal, state, or local law.

### At-Will Employment

Employment with Tri-State Aquatic Solutions is at-will unless otherwise specified in a written employment agreement. This means either you or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice.

### Immigration Compliance

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions complies with all federal immigration laws. All employees must complete Form I-9 and provide documentation establishing their identity and authorization to work in the United States.

### Background Checks

All positions require successful completion of a background check, which may include:
- Criminal history review
- Driving record verification (for positions requiring driving)
- Employment verification
- Drug screening

### Confidentiality

Employees may have access to confidential information including:
- Customer data and project details
- Pricing and vendor information
- Business strategies and plans
- Employee information

All confidential information must be protected during and after employment. Unauthorized disclosure may result in termination and legal action.

### Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations that create actual or perceived conflicts of interest. This includes:
- Personal relationships with vendors or competitors
- Outside employment that conflicts with company interests
- Using company resources for personal gain
- Accepting gifts that could influence business decisions

Any potential conflicts must be disclosed to management in writing.

### Social Media Policy

Employees are welcome to use personal social media but must:
- Not disclose confidential company information
- Clearly distinguish personal opinions from company positions
- Not disparage the company, customers, or colleagues
- Refrain from posting during work hours on company time
- Report any work-related social media harassment or defamation

## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior Standards

All employees are expected to:

1. **Treat others with respect**
   - Use professional language at all times
   - Value diverse perspectives and backgrounds
   - Address conflicts constructively

2. **Act with integrity**
   - Be honest in all communications
   - Keep commitments and meet deadlines
   - Report errors or issues promptly

3. **Maintain professionalism**
   - Represent the company positively
   - Arrive on time and prepared
   - Complete work to the best of your ability

### Workplace Harassment Policy

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind, including:

- **Sexual harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature
- **Discriminatory harassment**: Offensive conduct based on protected characteristics
- **Bullying**: Repeated inappropriate behavior that creates a hostile work environment

**Reporting Procedure:**
1. Report incidents to your supervisor, HR, or any manager
2. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially
3. Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment is prohibited
4. Substantiated claims will result in disciplinary action up to termination

### Substance Abuse Policy

Employees are prohibited from:
- Using, possessing, or being under the influence of illegal drugs on company premises or while working
- Using alcohol on company premises or while working (except at authorized company events)
- Misusing prescription medications in a way that affects job performance

The company may conduct drug testing:
- Pre-employment
- Post-accident
- Reasonable suspicion
- Random testing for safety-sensitive positions

Employees struggling with substance abuse are encouraged to seek help through available resources before issues affect job performance.

### Violence Prevention

Workplace violence of any kind is prohibited, including:
- Physical violence or threats
- Intimidation or stalking
- Possession of weapons on company premises or in company vehicles
- Verbal threats or abusive language

All threats or acts of violence must be reported immediately to management or HR.

### Technology and Equipment Use

Company technology and equipment are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted if it:
- Does not interfere with work responsibilities
- Does not violate company policies
- Does not consume excessive resources
- Complies with all applicable laws

The company reserves the right to monitor all technology use, including email, internet activity, and phone records.

## Time Off Policies

### Paid Time Off (PTO)

Full-time employees accrue PTO based on years of service:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO | Accrual Rate |
|-----------------|------------|--------------|
| 0-1 years | 10 days | 3.33 hrs/month |
| 2-4 years | 15 days | 5.00 hrs/month |
| 5-9 years | 20 days | 6.67 hrs/month |
| 10+ years | 25 days | 8.33 hrs/month |

**PTO Guidelines:**
- Accrual begins on date of hire
- Maximum carryover: 40 hours per year
- Unused PTO above carryover limit is forfeited on anniversary date
- PTO may be used in minimum 4-hour increments
- Advance notice required: 1 week for 1-2 days, 2 weeks for 3+ days
- Peak season (May-August) requests may be limited

### Holidays

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions observes the following paid holidays:

1. New Year's Day
2. Memorial Day
3. Independence Day
4. Labor Day
5. Thanksgiving Day
6. Day After Thanksgiving
7. Christmas Eve
8. Christmas Day

Holiday pay is provided at regular rate for 8 hours. If a holiday falls on a weekend, the observed day will be communicated in advance.

### Sick Leave

**Policy:**
- Full-time employees receive 5 days of sick leave annually
- Sick leave does not carry over or pay out at separation
- May be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for ill family members
- Documentation may be required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days

### Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for:
- Birth and care of a newborn child
- Placement of a child for adoption or foster care
- Care for an immediate family member with a serious health condition
- Medical leave for a serious health condition

**Eligibility:**
- Employed for at least 12 months
- Worked at least 1,250 hours in the past 12 months
- Work at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### Bereavement Leave

Employees may take paid bereavement leave as follows:
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle): 3 days
- Close friend: 1 day

Additional unpaid leave may be approved upon request.

### Jury Duty

Employees called for jury duty will receive:
- Full pay for up to 5 days
- Partial pay (50%) for days 6-10
- Unpaid leave thereafter (job protected)

Proof of jury service required.

### Voting Leave

Employees may take up to 2 hours of paid time to vote if polls are not open for 2 consecutive hours outside of work hours.

### Military Leave

Leave for military service is provided in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).

## Safety Requirements

### Safety Commitment

Safety is our top priority. Every employee is responsible for:
- Following all safety policies and procedures
- Reporting hazards and unsafe conditions immediately
- Using required personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Participating in safety training
- Never taking shortcuts that compromise safety

### General Safety Rules

1. **Report all injuries immediately**, no matter how minor
2. **Use proper lifting techniques** (lift with legs, not back)
3. **Keep work areas clean and organized**
4. **Do not operate equipment without proper training**
5. **Follow lockout/tagout procedures**
6. **Never work under the influence of drugs or alcohol**
7. **Wear appropriate PPE for the task**
8. **Follow all posted safety signs and instructions**

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Required PPE by Task:**

| Task | Required PPE |
|------|-------------|
| Excavation | Hard hat, safety glasses, steel-toe boots, high-vis vest |
| Concrete work | Safety glasses, gloves, steel-toe boots |
| Electrical work | Insulated gloves, safety glasses, arc-rated clothing |
| Chemical handling | Chemical-resistant gloves, safety glasses/goggles, apron |
| Trenching | Hard hat, steel-toe boots, high-vis vest |
| Welding | Welding helmet, gloves, protective clothing |

Employees must inspect PPE before each use and report any damage.

### Hazard Communication

All employees working with chemicals must:
- Review Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before handling chemicals
- Understand chemical hazard labels
- Use appropriate PPE
- Know spill response procedures
- Never mix chemicals unless specifically trained

### Vehicle Safety

Employees operating company vehicles must:
- Possess a valid driver's license appropriate for the vehicle
- Maintain an acceptable driving record
- Complete defensive driving training
- Never use cell phones while driving (hands-free only)
- Wear seatbelts at all times
- Report all accidents immediately
- Perform daily vehicle inspections

### Emergency Procedures

**In case of emergency:**
1. Ensure personal safety first
2. Call 911 if emergency services are needed
3. Notify supervisor immediately
4. Render first aid only if trained and safe to do so
5. Do not move injured persons unless necessary for safety
6. Preserve the scene for investigation

**Emergency contacts:**
- Emergency services: 911
- Company emergency hotline: [Posted at all locations]
- HR emergency contact: [Posted at all locations]

### Accident Reporting

All accidents, injuries, and near-misses must be reported within 24 hours using the Incident Report Form. This includes:
- Personal injuries (even minor)
- Property damage
- Vehicle accidents
- Near-miss incidents

Failure to report may result in disciplinary action.

### Workers' Compensation

Employees injured on the job are covered by workers' compensation insurance. To receive benefits:
1. Report the injury to your supervisor immediately
2. Seek medical attention as needed
3. Complete all required paperwork
4. Follow medical provider's return-to-work instructions

## Disciplinary Procedures

### Purpose

Our disciplinary procedures are designed to:
- Address performance and conduct issues fairly and consistently
- Give employees opportunity to improve
- Protect employees, customers, and the company
- Document issues and corrective actions

### Progressive Discipline Process

For most performance or conduct issues, we follow a progressive discipline approach:

#### Step 1: Verbal Warning
- Discussion of the issue with the employee
- Clarification of expectations
- Documentation in employee file
- No formal written notice

#### Step 2: Written Warning
- Formal written notice of the issue
- Description of required improvement
- Timeline for improvement (typically 30 days)
- Consequences of continued issues
- Employee acknowledgment signature required

#### Step 3: Final Written Warning
- Formal notice that employment is at risk
- Specific improvement requirements
- Short timeline (typically 14-30 days)
- May include suspension without pay
- Employee acknowledgment required

#### Step 4: Termination
- Employment ends
- Final paycheck provided per state law
- Exit interview conducted
- Company property returned

### Immediate Termination Offenses

Certain conduct may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline:

- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats of violence
- Harassment or discrimination
- Gross insubordination
- Falsification of records
- Illegal drug use on premises
- Being under the influence at work
- Serious safety violations
- Bringing weapons to work
- Disclosure of confidential information
- Criminal conduct affecting employment

### Appeal Process

Employees who believe disciplinary action was unjust may appeal:

1. Submit written appeal to HR within 5 business days
2. Include specific reasons for the appeal
3. Provide any supporting documentation
4. HR will review and respond within 10 business days
5. HR decision is final

### Documentation

All disciplinary actions are documented and maintained in personnel files. Employees may request to review their file with HR present.
