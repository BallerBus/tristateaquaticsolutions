## Program Overview

### Purpose

The Tri-State Aquatic Solutions onboarding program is designed to:
- Welcome new team members and help them feel part of the family
- Provide the knowledge and skills needed for success in their role
- Ensure understanding of company culture, policies, and expectations
- Accelerate time to full productivity
- Establish strong foundations for long-term success and retention

### Onboarding Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Pre-Arrival | Before Day 1 | Preparation and logistics |
| Orientation | Days 1-2 | Company overview, paperwork, setup |
| Initial Training | Week 1 | Role-specific basics |
| Skill Building | Weeks 2-4 | Hands-on training and development |
| Integration | Days 30-60 | Independent work with support |
| Full Productivity | Days 60-90 | Autonomous performance |

### Stakeholder Responsibilities

**Human Resources:**
- Coordinate pre-arrival preparation
- Conduct orientation sessions
- Process paperwork and benefits enrollment
- Track onboarding progress

**Hiring Manager:**
- Prepare workspace and equipment
- Create training schedule
- Assign mentor
- Conduct regular check-ins
- Set performance expectations

**Mentor/Buddy:**
- Provide day-to-day guidance
- Answer questions and provide support
- Introduce to team and culture
- Share practical knowledge and tips

**New Employee:**
- Complete all required paperwork
- Attend all training sessions
- Ask questions and seek help
- Meet milestones and expectations

## Pre-Arrival Preparation

### HR Checklist (Before Day 1)

**1 Week Before Start Date:**

- [ ] Send welcome email with first day details
  - Start time and location
  - Parking instructions
  - Dress code reminder
  - What to bring (ID, documents for I-9)
  - First day agenda
- [ ] Prepare new hire paperwork packet
- [ ] Set up employee in payroll system
- [ ] Prepare benefits enrollment materials
- [ ] Order uniform items (field staff)
- [ ] Request IT setup (email, software access)
- [ ] Add to company directory and org chart

### Manager Checklist (Before Day 1)

**1-2 Weeks Before Start Date:**

- [ ] Identify and brief mentor/buddy
- [ ] Create 90-day training plan
- [ ] Prepare workstation/workspace
  - Desk, chair, supplies (office staff)
  - Tool kit, PPE (field staff)
  - Vehicle assignment (if applicable)
- [ ] Schedule key meetings for first week
- [ ] Prepare first week assignments
- [ ] Notify team of new hire
- [ ] Plan team introduction/welcome

**Day Before Start:**

- [ ] Confirm new hire is coming
- [ ] Verify workspace is ready
- [ ] Review first day schedule
- [ ] Prepare welcome materials
- [ ] Brief reception/office about arrival

## First Day Checklist

### Hour-by-Hour Schedule

**8:00 AM - Arrival and Welcome**
- [ ] Greet new employee at entrance
- [ ] Provide parking pass (if applicable)
- [ ] Tour of facility/office
- [ ] Introduction to reception/front office staff
- [ ] Provide welcome packet and company swag

**9:00 AM - HR Orientation (Part 1)**
- [ ] Complete required paperwork:
  - W-4 Tax Withholding Form
  - I-9 Employment Eligibility (with documentation)
  - Direct deposit authorization
  - Emergency contact information
  - Employee handbook acknowledgment
  - Confidentiality agreement
  - Drug testing consent (if applicable)
  - Background check authorization (if not completed)

**10:30 AM - Break**
- [ ] Tour break room/kitchen facilities
- [ ] Explain break policies

**10:45 AM - HR Orientation (Part 2)**
- [ ] Company overview presentation:
  - Company history and mission
  - Organizational structure
  - Products and services overview
  - Company culture and values
- [ ] Benefits overview and enrollment
- [ ] Payroll and time tracking explanation
- [ ] Safety overview and emergency procedures

**12:00 PM - Lunch**
- [ ] Manager or mentor takes new hire to lunch
- [ ] Casual conversation and relationship building
- [ ] Introduction to any colleagues present

**1:00 PM - Manager Meeting**
- [ ] Welcome and role overview
- [ ] Team introduction
- [ ] Review job description and expectations
- [ ] Discuss 90-day onboarding plan
- [ ] Set first week goals
- [ ] Explain communication preferences

**2:30 PM - Workspace and Technology Setup**
- [ ] Set up workstation/equipment
- [ ] Computer login and password setup
- [ ] Email setup and overview
- [ ] Software access and training (basic)
- [ ] Phone system overview
- [ ] Security and access credentials

**4:00 PM - Meet the Team**
- [ ] Introduction to immediate team members
- [ ] Tour of relevant work areas
- [ ] Introduction to key contacts in other departments

**4:30 PM - First Day Wrap-Up**
- [ ] Check in with manager
- [ ] Review tomorrow's schedule
- [ ] Answer questions
- [ ] Provide after-hours contact info
- [ ] Confirm arrival time for Day 2

### First Day Checklist by Role

**Field Staff (Installation Technicians, Lead Installers):**
- [ ] Safety orientation overview

- [ ] Vehicle and equipment overview
- [ ] Meet crew members

## First Week Schedule

### Day 2: Role Foundation

**Morning:**
- Review Day 1 information and answer questions
- Deep dive into department/team operations
- Introduction to key processes and workflows
- Shadow experienced team member (2 hours)

**Afternoon:**
- Complete required online training modules
- Role-specific software training
- Begin reading materials (manuals, procedures)
- End-of-day check-in with manager

### Day 3: Technical Training

**Morning:**
- Technical training specific to role (see Role Training section)
- Hands-on practice with tools/systems
- Q&A with subject matter experts

**Afternoon:**
- Continue technical training
- Safety training (field staff)
- Customer service training overview
- End-of-day check-in with mentor

### Day 4: Application

**Morning:**
- Begin supervised work activities
- Apply training in real scenarios
- Manager observation and feedback

**Afternoon:**
- Continue supervised work
- Complete any outstanding paperwork
- Review training progress
- End-of-day check-in with manager

### Day 5: Integration

**Morning:**
- Continue supervised work
- Increase independence level
- Complete first week training requirements

**Afternoon:**
- First week review meeting with manager
- Self-assessment and feedback
- Set goals for Week 2
- Team welcome activity (if applicable)

### First Week Completion Checklist

**Administrative:**
- [ ] All paperwork completed and filed
- [ ] Benefits enrollment submitted
- [ ] Payroll setup confirmed
- [ ] System access verified and working
- [ ] Email signature configured
- [ ] Company directory updated

**Training:**
- [ ] Company orientation completed
- [ ] Employee handbook reviewed
- [ ] Basic safety training completed
- [ ] Role-specific Day 1-5 training completed
- [ ] Key software/systems introduced

**Integration:**
- [ ] Met all immediate team members
- [ ] Met key contacts in other departments
- [ ] Understands reporting structure
- [ ] Knows how to get help/ask questions
- [ ] Understands first 30-day expectations

## Mentorship Program

### Program Overview

Every new employee is assigned a mentor to provide guidance, support, and insider knowledge during the onboarding period and beyond. The mentor relationship is crucial to successful integration and long-term success.

### Mentor Selection Criteria

Mentors are selected based on:
- Minimum 1 year with the company
- Strong performance record
- Positive attitude and cultural fit
- Good communication skills
- Willingness to mentor
- Similar or relevant role experience
- Availability to commit time

### Mentor Responsibilities

**Before New Hire Starts:**
- Review new hire's background and role
- Prepare for first meeting
- Clear time in schedule for mentoring

**First Week:**
- Daily check-ins (minimum 15 minutes)
- Lunch together at least twice
- Available for questions throughout the day
- Help navigate office/team dynamics
- Share unwritten rules and tips

**Weeks 2-4:**
- Check-ins at least 3x per week
- Provide feedback on progress
- Connect new hire with helpful resources
- Support during challenging moments
- Share practical knowledge and shortcuts

**Days 30-60:**
- Weekly check-ins
- Help troubleshoot problems
- Provide encouragement and feedback
- Connect with broader network
- Support goal achievement

**Days 60-90:**
- Bi-weekly check-ins
- Support continued development
- Provide honest feedback for 90-day review
- Celebrate successes
- Transition to peer relationship

### Mentee Responsibilities

- Be proactive in asking questions
- Come prepared to check-ins
- Be receptive to feedback
- Follow through on mentor's suggestions
- Respect mentor's time
- Maintain confidentiality
- Provide feedback on the mentorship

### Mentor-Mentee Meeting Guide

**First Meeting Agenda:**
1. Get to know each other
2. Discuss expectations for relationship
3. Share mentor's career journey
4. Discuss mentee's goals
5. Establish communication preferences
6. Schedule regular check-ins

**Ongoing Meeting Topics:**
- Current challenges or concerns
- Recent wins and successes
- Questions about company/role
- Feedback on performance
- Career development discussions
- Personal well-being check-in

### Mentor Recognition

Successful mentors are recognized through:
- Mentor certificate after completion
- Consideration in performance reviews
- Mentor appreciation events
- Leadership development opportunities

## Onboarding Evaluation

### New Hire Feedback Surveys

**Week 1 Survey:**
- First day experience
- Orientation effectiveness
- Workspace and equipment readiness
- Initial training quality
- Mentor introduction

**30-Day Survey:**
- Training program effectiveness
- Manager support quality
- Mentor relationship
- Team integration
- Clarity of expectations
- Areas for improvement

**90-Day Survey:**
- Overall onboarding experience
- Preparedness for role
- Suggestions for improvement
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

### Manager Evaluation

Managers complete evaluations at:
- **30 days:** Progress assessment, training completion
- **60 days:** Performance evaluation, goal progress
- **90 days:** Comprehensive review, permanent status decision

### Mentor Feedback

Mentors provide input on:
- New hire engagement level
- Cultural fit observations
- Areas of strength
- Development opportunities
- Onboarding process feedback

### Onboarding Program Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| 90-Day Retention | 95%+ | New hires retained at 90 days |
| Time to Productivity | Per role | Days to meet baseline KPIs |
| Training Completion | 100% | Required training completed on time |
| New Hire Satisfaction | 4.5+/5 | Average survey score |
| Manager Satisfaction | 4.5+/5 | Manager feedback scores |
| Mentor Utilization | 100% | New hires with active mentors |

### Continuous Improvement

The onboarding program is reviewed quarterly based on:
- Survey feedback analysis
- Retention and performance data
- Manager and mentor input
- Industry best practices
- New hire suggestions

Improvements are documented and implemented for future cohorts.
