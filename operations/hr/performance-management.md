## Performance Management Overview

### Philosophy

At Tri-State Aquatic Solutions, we believe that effective performance management is about ongoing development, not just annual evaluation. Our approach focuses on:

- **Continuous Feedback** - Regular conversations, not annual surprises
- **Clear Expectations** - Transparent standards and measurable goals
- **Development Focus** - Building strengths and addressing gaps
- **Fair Recognition** - Rewarding contributions appropriately
- **Growth Opportunities** - Pathways for advancement

### Performance Management Cycle

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Goal Setting | January/February | Set annual goals, align with company objectives |
| Quarterly Check-ins | April, July, October | Review progress, adjust goals, provide feedback |
| Mid-Year Review | June/July | Formal progress assessment |
| Year-End Review | December/January | Comprehensive evaluation, development planning |
| Compensation Review | January/February | Merit increases, bonus payouts |

### Key Participants

**Employee:**
- Self-assessment and reflection
- Goal achievement and tracking
- Seeking feedback and development
- Active participation in reviews

**Manager:**
- Setting clear expectations
- Providing regular feedback
- Conducting formal reviews
- Supporting development
- Making compensation recommendations

**HR:**
- Process administration
- Training and support
- Consistency oversight
- Documentation management

## Goal Setting Process

### Goal Setting Principles

**SMART Goals:**
- **S**pecific - Clear and well-defined
- **M**easurable - Quantifiable outcomes
- **A**chievable - Realistic and attainable
- **R**elevant - Aligned with role and company objectives
- **T**ime-bound - Clear deadline

### Annual Goal Setting Process

**Timeline:**
- **January Week 1:** Company communicates annual objectives
- **January Week 2:** Managers cascade objectives to teams
- **January Weeks 2-3:** Managers and employees draft individual goals
- **January Week 4:** Goals finalized and documented
- **February 1:** Goal period begins

### Goal Categories

Each employee should have goals in multiple categories:

**1. Performance Goals (2-3 goals)**
- Related to core job responsibilities
- Focus on quality, productivity, efficiency
- Measurable outcomes

*Example:* "Maintain installation quality score of 97% or higher on all assigned projects"

**2. Professional Development Goals (1-2 goals)**
- Skill building and learning
- Certifications or training
- Career advancement preparation

*Example:* "Complete CPO certification by June 30"

**3. Team/Company Contribution Goals (1 goal)**
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Process improvement
- Mentoring or knowledge sharing

*Example:* "Mentor two new technicians through their first 90 days"

### Goal Adjustment Process

Goals may be adjusted during the year when:
- Business priorities significantly change
- Employee's role or responsibilities change
- External factors make goals unattainable or irrelevant
- Goals are achieved early and should be replaced

**Adjustment Procedure:**
1. Employee or manager identifies need for adjustment
2. Discussion of rationale and new proposed goals
3. Documentation of change with both signatures
4. HR notification for records

## Compensation Review Process

### Compensation Philosophy

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions is committed to:
- Competitive compensation that attracts and retains talent
- Fair pay that reflects performance and contribution
- Internal equity across similar roles
- Transparent compensation practices

### Annual Compensation Review Cycle

**Timeline:**
| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Budget Planning | November | Compensation budget determined |
| Performance Reviews | December-January | Evaluations completed |
| Manager Recommendations | January | Managers submit increase recommendations |
| Calibration | Late January | Leadership reviews for consistency |
| Approval | Early February | Final decisions made |
| Communication | February | Increases communicated to employees |
| Effective Date | March 1 | New compensation takes effect |

### Merit Increase Guidelines

Merit increases are based on performance ratings:

| Performance Rating | Typical Merit Increase Range |
|--------------------|------------------------------|
| 5 - Exceptional | 5-8% |
| 4 - Exceeds Expectations | 3-5% |
| 3 - Meets Expectations | 2-3% |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0-1% |
| 1 - Does Not Meet | 0% |

*Actual increases depend on budget availability, market conditions, and internal equity.*

### Factors Affecting Compensation Decisions

**Performance:**
- Overall performance rating
- Goal achievement
- KPI performance
- Demonstrated growth

**Market:**
- External market data
- Role competitiveness
- Local labor market conditions

**Internal Equity:**
- Pay relative to peers in similar roles
- Experience and tenure differentials
- Skills and certifications

**Budget:**
- Company financial performance
- Departmental allocation
- Overall compensation budget

### Bonus Programs

**Annual Performance Bonus:**
- Eligibility: Full-time employees with 6+ months tenure
- Target: Based on role (0-15% of salary)
- Factors: Individual performance (70%) + Company performance (30%)
- Timing: Paid in March with merit increases

**Sales Commission:**
- Eligibility: Sales Consultants
- Structure: Base + Commission (3-5% of contract value)
- Payment: Monthly following contract signing

**Project Completion Bonus:**
- Eligibility: Field staff on designated projects
- Amount: Varies by project complexity
- Criteria: On-time, on-budget, quality completion

**Referral Bonus:**
- Eligibility: All employees
- Amount: $500-1,000 for successful hires
- Payment: After new hire completes 90 days

### Compensation Review Meeting

Managers should:
1. Schedule private meeting with employee
2. Explain performance rating connection
3. Communicate new compensation
4. Discuss any bonus payout
5. Address questions
6. Provide written confirmation

**Do:**
- Be direct and clear
- Explain rationale
- Listen to concerns
- Thank for contributions

**Don't:**
- Compare to other employees
- Make promises about future increases
- Discuss budget limitations in detail
- Rush the conversation

## Promotion Criteria

### Promotion Philosophy

Promotions at Tri-State Aquatic Solutions are based on:
- Demonstrated performance in current role
- Readiness for next-level responsibilities
- Business need for the position
- Alignment with career development goals

### General Promotion Requirements

**All Promotions Require:**
- Minimum 12 months in current position (exceptions for exceptional performance)
- Performance rating of 3 or higher in most recent review
- No active disciplinary actions
- Completion of required training for current role
- Manager recommendation

### Role-Specific Promotion Paths

#### Field Operations Career Ladder

```
Pool Installation Technician
         |
         v
Lead Installer / Foreman
         |
         v
Project Manager / Operations Supervisor
         |
         v
Operations Manager / Director
```

**Technician to Lead Installer Requirements:**
- 2+ years as Installation Technician
- Performance rating 4+ in most recent review
- All technical certifications complete (OSHA 30, CPO)
- Demonstrated leadership qualities
- Training/mentoring experience
- No safety violations in past 12 months
- Supervisor recommendation

**Lead Installer to Project Manager Requirements:**
- 2+ years as Lead Installer
- Performance rating 4+ in most recent review
- Project management training completed
- Customer communication excellence
- Budget management understanding
- Multiple successful complex project completions
- Manager recommendation and interview

#### Sales Career Ladder

```
Sales Consultant
      |
      v
Senior Sales Consultant
      |
      v
Sales Manager
      |
      v
Director of Sales
```

**Sales Consultant to Senior Sales Consultant:**
- 2+ years as Sales Consultant
- Consistent quota achievement (100%+ for 8+ quarters)
- Customer satisfaction scores 4.5+
- Mentoring contribution
- Advanced product knowledge
- Manager recommendation

#### Customer Service Career Ladder

```
Customer Service Representative
            |
            v
Senior Customer Service Rep / Team Lead
            |
            v
Customer Service Manager
            |
            v
Office Manager
```

**CSR to Senior CSR/Team Lead:**
- 18+ months as CSR
- Performance rating 4+
- All KPIs consistently at target
- Training ability demonstrated
- Problem resolution excellence
- Manager recommendation

### Promotion Process

**Step 1: Identification**
- Manager identifies promotion-ready employee OR
- Employee expresses interest in advancement

**Step 2: Assessment**
- Review against promotion criteria
- Gap analysis if not ready
- Development plan if needed

**Step 3: Recommendation**
- Manager submits promotion recommendation to HR
- Include justification and supporting evidence
- Proposed compensation adjustment

**Step 4: Review**
- HR reviews for consistency and equity
- Leadership approval for management roles
- Budget confirmation

**Step 5: Decision**
- Approval or denial with feedback
- If approved, determine effective date
- Prepare communication plan

**Step 6: Communication**
- Manager communicates promotion to employee
- Discuss new responsibilities and expectations
- HR processes paperwork
- Team announcement

### Promotion Compensation

Promotions typically include:
- Title change
- Pay increase (typically 8-15% depending on role change)
- Updated job description
- New goals and expectations

## Performance Improvement Plans

### When to Use a PIP

A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is appropriate when:
- Employee has received feedback but hasn't improved
- Performance gaps are significant but correctable
- There is a genuine desire to help employee succeed
- Clear, measurable improvement is possible

A PIP is NOT appropriate when:
- Issues warrant immediate termination
- Employee has already demonstrated unwillingness to improve
- Performance gaps cannot be reasonably corrected
- Behavior issues are severe (harassment, violence, theft)

### PIP Process

**Step 1: Documentation**
- Gather evidence of performance issues
- Review previous feedback and coaching
- Consult with HR

**Step 2: PIP Development**
- Identify specific performance gaps
- Define clear improvement expectations
- Set measurable goals and timeline
- Determine support and resources

**Step 3: PIP Meeting**
- Meet with employee (HR present recommended)
- Present performance concerns with examples
- Review PIP document
- Allow employee response
- Obtain signature (acknowledges receipt, not agreement)

**Step 4: Monitoring**
- Weekly check-ins during PIP period
- Document progress (positive and negative)
- Provide ongoing feedback
- Adjust support as needed

**Step 5: Conclusion**
- Meet at PIP end date
- Review performance against goals
- Determine outcome:
  - Successful completion
  - Extended PIP
  - Termination

### PIP Best Practices

**For Managers:**
- Be specific and objective about performance gaps
- Focus on behaviors and outcomes, not personality
- Provide genuine support and resources
- Document all conversations and progress
- Be consistent in monitoring
- Maintain professionalism and respect

**For HR:**
- Ensure consistency with previous cases
- Review for legal compliance
- Support manager throughout process
- Maintain documentation
- Be present for key meetings

**Timeline Guidelines:**
- Typical PIP duration: 30-60 days
- Check-ins: Weekly minimum
- Maximum one extension: 30 additional days

## Recognition Programs

### Recognition Philosophy

Recognition reinforces behaviors and achievements that drive our success. We believe in recognizing contributions at all levels, both formally and informally.

### Informal Recognition

**Day-to-Day Recognition:**
- Verbal praise and thank you
- Written notes or emails
- Public acknowledgment in meetings
- Small tokens of appreciation

**Manager Toolkit:**
- Thank you cards (provided by HR)
- Spot gift cards ($10-25 for exceptional efforts)
- Team celebration for milestones
- Featured in company communications

### Formal Recognition Programs

**Employee of the Month**
- Nomination by managers or peers
- Selection criteria:
  - Exceptional performance
  - Embodied company values
  - Went above and beyond
  - Positive impact on team/customers
- Recognition:
  - Certificate and trophy
  - $100 bonus
  - Premium parking spot for month
  - Photo display and announcement
  - Entered in Employee of Year consideration

**Employee of the Year**
- Selected from monthly winners
- Year-end leadership vote
- Recognition:
  - Plaque and trophy
  - $500 bonus
  - Extra PTO day
  - Company-wide announcement

**Safety Champion Award**
- Quarterly recognition
- Criteria:
  - Zero safety incidents
  - Safety leadership
  - Identifies/corrects hazards
  - Promotes safety culture
- Recognition:
  - Certificate
  - $50 bonus
  - Safety gear item of choice

**Customer Service Excellence Award**
- Based on customer feedback/surveys
- Monthly recognition
- Recognition:
  - Certificate
  - $50 bonus
  - Featured in newsletter

**Perfect Attendance Award**
- Quarterly and annual
- Criteria: No unscheduled absences
- Recognition:
  - Quarterly: $50 bonus
  - Annual: Extra PTO day

### Team Recognition
