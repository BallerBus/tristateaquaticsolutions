# Pool Code Compliance Guide

## Tri-State Aquatic Solutions - Code Requirements

This document outlines the building codes, safety requirements, and compliance standards for residential swimming pool installations in Pennsylvania and Delaware.

## Barrier Requirements

Pool barriers are required by code to prevent unsupervised access by children. These requirements are non-negotiable and must be met for permit approval and final inspection.

### Height Requirements

| Jurisdiction | Minimum Height | Measurement Point |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Pennsylvania | 48 inches | Outside of barrier |
| Delaware | 48 inches | Outside of barrier |
| IRC (Model Code) | 48 inches | Outside of barrier |

**Note:** Some townships may have stricter requirements. Always verify with local building department.

### Opening Limitations

**Maximum Opening Size: 4 inches**
- No opening in the barrier shall allow passage of a 4-inch sphere
- Applies to:
  - Space between fence pickets
  - Space under the fence
  - Decorative cutouts
  - Chain link mesh (2.25" max opening for chain link)

### Gate Requirements

**Self-Closing:**
- Gates must close and latch automatically from any open position
- Spring hinges or hydraulic closers acceptable
- Must function properly in all weather conditions

**Self-Latching:**
- Latch must engage automatically when gate closes
- Latch release mechanism must be:
  - Minimum 54 inches above grade (on outside of gate), OR
  - On the pool side of the gate with no opening within 18 inches that could reach the release

**Opening Direction:**
- Gates must open outward, away from the pool
- Exception: Sliding gates

**No Hold-Open Devices:**
- Gates cannot have mechanisms to hold them open
- Exception: Gates with self-closing devices that are always engaged

### Barrier Design Prohibitions

**Climbable Features Prohibited:**
- No horizontal rails or decorative elements spaced to allow climbing
- If using horizontal rails, space must be 45 inches or greater
- Chain link fence: Must have top rail, no horizontal bars below

**Objects Near Barrier:**
- No permanent structures within 4 feet of barrier that could be used for climbing
- Trees, furniture, play equipment must not provide climbing access

### Using the House as a Barrier

If the house wall serves as part of the pool barrier:

**Door Requirements:**
- Self-closing device required
- Self-latching device at 54 inches minimum
- Audible alarm required (sounds when door opens)
- Alarm must:
  - Sound for minimum 30 seconds
  - Have minimum 85 dB at 10 feet
  - Have override capability for resident use
  - Automatically reset

**Window Requirements:**
- Windows within barrier must not open more than 4 inches, OR
- Must have locks that prevent opening, OR
- Must be alarmed

**Dual Protection Option:**
- Some jurisdictions allow door alarm OR self-closing/latching
- Others require both - verify locally

### Removable Mesh Barriers

Approved for use as secondary barrier when:
- Mesh is non-climbable
- Bottom secured to deck
- Meets 48-inch height requirement
- Has self-closing, self-latching gate
- Meets ASTM F1908 standard

**Not approved as sole barrier** - use only as additional protection

## Electrical Code (NEC Article 680)

The National Electrical Code Article 680 governs swimming pool electrical installations. This is one of the most complex and strictly enforced sections of the electrical code.

### Bonding Requirements (NEC 680.26)

**Purpose:** Create equipotential bonding to prevent electric shock by ensuring all conductive surfaces are at the same electrical potential.

**Required Bonded Components:**
1. Pool reinforcing steel (rebar)
2. Metal parts of pool structure
3. Metal fittings within 5 feet of pool (handrails, ladders, diving boards)
4. Metal parts of equipment (pump motors, filter tanks, heaters)
5. Metal conduit and junction boxes
6. Metal pipes within 5 feet of pool
7. Metal parts of electrical equipment associated with pool
8. Metal fencing within 5 feet of pool

**Bonding Conductor Requirements:**
- Minimum #8 AWG solid copper
- Insulated, covered, or bare
- Connected with listed clamps or exothermic welding

**Equipotential Bonding Grid:**
- Required around perimeter of pool
- Extends minimum 3 feet horizontally beyond inside wall
- Minimum #8 AWG solid copper
- Tied to reinforcing steel at minimum 4 points

### Grounding Requirements (NEC 680.23-680.25)

**Equipment Grounding:**
- All equipment must have equipment grounding conductor
- Sized per NEC 250.122 (minimum #12 AWG)
- Green or green with yellow stripe

**Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) Protection:**

| Equipment | GFCI Required? | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Pool pump motor | Yes | Required by NEC 680.21(C) |
| Pool lighting (120V) | Yes | Required by NEC 680.23(A)(3) |
| Outlets within 20 ft | Yes | Required by NEC 680.22(A) |
| Heaters | Varies | Check manufacturer requirements |
| Automation systems | Yes | All associated equipment |

### Clearances

**Overhead Power Lines (NEC 680.8):**
| Voltage | Horizontal from pool | Vertical over pool |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 0-750V | 10 feet | 22.5 feet |
| Over 750V | 10 feet | 25 feet |
| Communication lines | 10 feet | 10 feet |

**Receptacle Locations (NEC 680.22):**
- No receptacles within 6 feet of pool (inside edge)
- 15A/125V or 20A/125V receptacles: 6-20 feet distance - GFCI required
- 240V receptacles: Minimum 10 feet from pool

**Lighting Fixture Locations (NEC 680.22(B)):**
- No fixtures directly over pool
- Fixtures 5-12 feet from pool: Must be GFCI protected or 12 feet above maximum water level
- Fixtures over 12 feet from pool: Standard rules apply

### Underwater Lighting (NEC 680.23)

**Standard (120V) Underwater Lights:**
- Listed for swimming pool use
- GFCI protected
- Installed in approved niche
- Cord length: Allows light to be placed on deck for relamping (minimum 12 feet typical)
- Fixture at least 18 inches below normal water level

**Low Voltage (12V/15V) Underwater Lights:**
- Listed transformer required
- Transformer minimum 4 feet from pool
- Much simpler installation requirements

### Disconnect Requirements (NEC 680.12)

**Equipment Disconnect:**
- Required for all pool equipment
- Within sight of equipment
- Minimum 5 feet horizontal from inside edge of pool
- Accessible and lockable
- Rated for equipment load

### Wiring Methods (NEC 680.21)

**Approved Methods:**
- Rigid metal conduit (RMC)
- Intermediate metal conduit (IMC)
- Rigid PVC conduit (Schedule 40 or 80)
- Type MC cable (limited locations)

**Underground Installation:**
- PVC conduit: Minimum 18 inches cover
- Rigid metal: Minimum 6 inches cover under concrete
- Direct burial cable: Minimum 18 inches cover

## Plumbing Code Requirements

### Main Drain Requirements

**Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act (VGB):**
Federal law requires all pool drains to be compliant with anti-entrapment standards.

**Single Main Drain:**
- Must have VGB-compliant cover (ASME/ANSI A112.19.8)
- Safety Vacuum Release System (SVRS) or equivalent required
- OR automatic pump shut-off device
- OR gravity drainage system
- OR drain disabled

**Dual Main Drains:**
- Preferred solution
- Must be separated by minimum 3 feet (center to center)
- Both drains must have VGB-compliant covers
- Designed so one drain can handle full flow if other is blocked

**Drain Cover Requirements:**
- Listed to ASME/ANSI A112.19.8
- Properly sized for flow rate
- Manufacturer's maximum flow rate not exceeded
- Dome-shaped or other anti-entrapment design

### Return Line Requirements

**Number of Returns:**
- Sufficient for proper circulation
- Generally: One return per 10,000-15,000 gallons
- Positioned for optimal circulation

**Location:**
- Opposite skimmer for proper circulation
- Below water line (typically 12-18 inches)
- Adjustable eyeball fittings for directing flow

### Skimmer Requirements

**Number of Skimmers:**
- One skimmer per 500-800 square feet of surface area
- Minimum one skimmer per pool

**Installation:**
- Recessed into pool wall
- Water level at mid-point of skimmer opening
- Weir door functional
- Equalizer line connected (if below skimmer)

### Fill Line Requirements

**Backflow Prevention:**
- Air gap (preferred) - 1 inch minimum above flood rim
- Anti-siphon device
- Reduced pressure backflow preventer
- Required by plumbing code to protect water supply

**Auto-Fill Systems:**
- Must have backflow prevention
- Overflow protection required
- Accessible shutoff valve

### Equipment Pad Plumbing

**Pump:**
- Unions for service access
- Check valves where required
- Strainer basket accessible

**Filter:**
- Unions for service access
- Pressure gauge (0-60 PSI typical)
- Air relief valve
- Drain port accessible

**Heater (if applicable):**
- Minimum flow requirements met
- Bypass option recommended
- Unions for service access

## ADA Considerations

While private residential pools are not required to meet ADA standards, understanding these requirements can be valuable for customers who want accessible features or for commercial applications.

### Pool Entry Options (ADA 2010 Standards)

**Primary Entry:**
1. Sloped entry (zero-depth entry)
2. Pool lift
3. Transfer wall
4. Transfer platform
5. Pool stairs (with specific dimensions)

**Large Pools (over 300 linear feet of wall):**
- Require two means of entry

### Pool Lift Requirements (if installed)

**Seat Requirements:**
- Minimum 16 inches wide
- Seat located over pool deck at raised position
- Submerges to minimum 18 inches below water

**Controls:**
- Operable with one hand
- No tight grasping required
- Located on pool deck
- Maximum 48 inches above deck

**Capacity:**
- Minimum 300 lb weight capacity

### Accessible Pool Stairs (if installed)

**Dimensions:**
- Tread depth: 12 inches minimum
- Riser height: 7 inches maximum
- Width: 36 inches minimum between handrails

**Handrails:**
- Both sides
- 34-38 inches above stair nosing
- Extend beyond top and bottom risers
- Graspable (1.25-2 inch diameter)

### Deck Considerations

**Accessible Route:**
- 36 inches minimum clear width
- Maximum 1:20 slope (not ramp)
- Stable, firm surface
- No abrupt level changes greater than 0.5 inches

**Pool Deck Surface:**
- Slip-resistant when wet
- Maximum gap between pavers: 0.5 inches

## Depth Markers and Safety Signs

### Depth Marker Requirements

**Location:**
- Maximum 25 feet apart around pool perimeter
- At maximum and minimum depth points
- At points where slope changes significantly
- At 5-foot depth point (break point)

**Marking Method:**
- Minimum 4-inch tall numerals
- Contrasting color from deck
- May be on deck or pool wall
- Waterproof and permanent

### Required Safety Signs

**"No Diving" Signs:**
- Required where water depth is less than 5 feet
- Recommended for all residential pools
- International symbol acceptable

**Depth Markers:**
- "SHALLOW" at shallow end
- "DEEP" at deep end
- Numerical depths at regular intervals

### Optional Safety Features

**Recommended for Residential Pools:**
- Life ring or shepherd's hook
- First aid kit near pool area
- Emergency phone/numbers posted
- Pool rules sign

## Compliance Checklist

### Before Permit Application

- [ ] Verify setback requirements for your township
- [ ] Confirm pool location meets all setbacks
- [ ] Plan barrier that meets all requirements
- [ ] Identify any easements or restrictions
- [ ] Verify utility locations (call 811)

### During Construction

- [ ] Rebar properly tied and supported
- [ ] Bonding grid installed
- [ ] All metal components bonded
- [ ] Plumbing pressure tested
- [ ] Main drain VGB compliant
- [ ] Electrical conduit properly installed

### Before Final Inspection

- [ ] Barrier meets height requirements (48" minimum)
- [ ] No openings exceed 4 inches
- [ ] Gate self-closing and self-latching
- [ ] Latch at 54 inches minimum
- [ ] All GFCI protection functional
- [ ] Bonding verified
- [ ] Disconnect properly located
- [ ] Depth markers installed
- [ ] Safety signs in place
- [ ] VGB-compliant drain covers installed
