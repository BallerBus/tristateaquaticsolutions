# Delaware Swimming Pool Permit Guide

## Tri-State Aquatic Solutions - DE Operations

This guide covers the permit requirements for residential swimming pool installations in our Delaware service area, primarily New Castle County and the City of Wilmington.

---

## New Castle County

### Overview
New Castle County handles building permits through the Department of Land Use. Pool permits require both building and electrical permits, with additional requirements for pools in flood zones or near wetlands.

**Office Hours:**

- Closed state holidays

### Permit Requirements

#### Building Permit
**Required Documents:**
1. **Permit Application Form**
   - Property owner information
   - Contractor information (must be licensed in Delaware)
   - Project description and estimated cost

2. **Site Plan Requirements**
   - Property boundaries with dimensions
   - Location of all existing structures
   - Proposed pool location with dimensions
   - Setback distances from all property lines
   - Location of pool equipment
   - Fence/barrier location and details
   - Scale (minimum 1" = 20')

3. **Pool Construction Documents**
   - Pool dimensions (length, width, depth)
   - Construction specifications
   - Cross-section drawing
   - Equipment specifications (pump, filter, heater, etc.)

4. **Barrier/Fence Plan**
   - Fence height (minimum 48 inches)
   - Gate specifications (self-closing, self-latching)
   - Latch height (minimum 54 inches from grade)
   - Material specifications

5. **Electrical Plan**
   - Panel location and amperage
   - Circuit layout
   - GFCI protection
   - Bonding details
   - Equipment disconnect locations

6. **Contractor Documentation**
   - Delaware Contractor License
   - Certificate of Insurance
   - Workers' Compensation certificate

### Zoning Requirements

#### Setback Requirements (Standard Residential Zones)

| Zone | Front Yard | Side Yard | Rear Yard |
|------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| NC10 | Not permitted | 10 feet | 10 feet |
| NC15 | Not permitted | 10 feet | 10 feet |
| NC21 | Not permitted | 15 feet | 15 feet |
| NC40 | Not permitted | 25 feet | 25 feet |

**Notes:**
- Pools are not permitted in front yards
- Setbacks measured from water's edge, not deck
- Corner lots have special requirements (verify with Land Use)
- Check for easements before finalizing location

#### Height Restrictions
- Equipment structures: Maximum 6 feet
- Pool house/cabana: Requires separate permit and zoning review
- Fence: Minimum 48 inches, maximum 6 feet without variance

---

### Special Considerations

#### Flood Zone Properties
If your property is in a FEMA-designated flood zone:
- Additional elevation certificate may be required
- Pool equipment may need elevation above base flood elevation
- Floodplain development permit required
- Additional review time: 2-4 weeks

**To Check Flood Zone Status:**
- Visit FEMA Flood Map Service Center: msc.fema.gov
- Or contact New Castle County Land Use

#### Wetlands
Properties with wetlands require:
- Wetland delineation (if boundary unclear)
- Buffer zone compliance (typically 25-100 feet)
- Possible Army Corps of Engineers permit
- Additional review time: 4-8 weeks

#### Septic Systems
- Pool must be minimum 10 feet from septic tank
- Pool must be minimum 10 feet from drain field
- Health Department review may be required

### Timeline Expectations

**Standard Timeline: 10-20 Business Days**

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Application submission | Day 1 |
| Initial review | 3-5 business days |
| Plan review | 5-10 business days |
| Corrections (if needed) | 3-5 business days |
| Permit issuance | 1-2 business days |

**Expedited Review:**
- Available for additional fee ($150)
- Reduces review time by approximately 50%
- Not available for projects requiring variance

**Factors That Extend Timeline:**
- Flood zone review: Add 2-4 weeks
- Wetland review: Add 4-8 weeks
- Variance required: Add 6-10 weeks
- Incomplete application: Add 1-2 weeks per resubmission

## City of Wilmington

### Overview
The City of Wilmington has its own permitting process separate from New Castle County. Properties within Wilmington city limits must obtain permits through the city's Department of Licenses and Inspections.

### Department of Licenses and Inspections
**Contact Information:**
- Address: 800 N. French Street, Wilmington, DE 19801
- Phone: (302) 576-3030
- Website: www.wilmingtonde.gov

**Office Hours:**
- Monday-Friday: 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM

---

### Permit Requirements

**Required Documents:**
1. Building Permit Application
2. Site Plan (to scale)
3. Pool specifications and construction drawings
4. Barrier/fence plan
5. Electrical permit application
6. Contractor license verification
7. Insurance certificates

**Setback Requirements (City of Wilmington):**
- Side yards: 5 feet minimum (verify by zone)
- Rear yard: 10 feet minimum
- Not permitted in front yards
- Additional setbacks may apply in historic districts

**Note:** City lots are typically smaller than county lots. Careful planning required to meet setbacks.

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue 1: Small Lot Size
**Problem:** Property cannot meet setback requirements
**Solutions:**
- Consider smaller pool design
- Apply for variance (additional time and cost)
- In-ground spa instead of full pool

### Issue 2: High Water Table
**Problem:** Groundwater encountered during excavation
**Solutions:**
- Dewatering during construction
- Hydrostatic relief valve installation
- Fiberglass or vinyl liner pool (vs. concrete)
- Pool design with minimal deep end

### Issue 3: Flood Zone Location
**Problem:** Property is in FEMA flood zone
**Solutions:**
- Elevate equipment above base flood elevation
- Obtain floodplain development permit
- Work with floodplain administrator early

### Issue 4: Wetland Buffer
**Problem:** Pool location encroaches on wetland buffer
**Solutions:**
- Relocate pool away from wetlands
- Obtain wetland permit (if minor encroachment)
- Professional wetland delineation to establish actual boundary

### Issue 5: Underground Utilities
**Problem:** Utilities conflict with pool location
**Solutions:**
- Call Miss Utility (811) before design finalization
- Relocate pool away from utilities
- Utility relocation (contact utility company)

### Issue 6: Incomplete Application
**Problem:** Application returned for missing items
**Solutions:**
- Use our checklist before submission
- Pre-application meeting (call Land Use)
- Professional site plan preparation

## Delaware Contractor Requirements

### Licensing
To install pools in Delaware, contractors must be properly licensed:

**General Contractor License:**
- Required for pool construction
- Issued by Delaware Division of Revenue
- Must be current and in good standing

**Electrical License:**
- Licensed electrician required for pool electrical work
- Or general contractor must subcontract to licensed electrician

## Miss Utility (Before You Dig)

**Delaware One Call / Miss Utility**
- Phone: 811 (nationwide)
- Website: www.missutility.net
- Required: 2 business days before excavation (Delaware law)

**What Gets Marked:**
- Electric lines
- Gas lines
- Water lines
- Sewer lines
- Communication lines
- Cable/fiber lines

**What Does NOT Get Marked:**
- Private utilities (propane, well, septic)
- Irrigation systems
- Invisible dog fences

## Inspection Process (Delaware)

### Required Inspections

1. **Footing/Pre-Pour Inspection**
   - Before any concrete is poured
   - Verifies excavation and rebar placement
   - 24-hour notice required

2. **Electrical Rough-In**
   - Before backfill or concrete
   - Verifies conduit and bonding
   - Must pass before proceeding

3. **Electrical Final**
   - After equipment installation
   - Tests GFCI protection
   - Verifies bonding and grounding

4. **Barrier/Fence Inspection**
   - After fence installation
   - Verifies height and gate compliance
   - Must pass before pool can be filled

5. **Final Inspection**
   - After pool is complete and filled
   - Overall code compliance
   - Certificate of Compliance issued

### Scheduling Inspections
- Phone: (302) 395-5400 (New Castle County)
- Online: Many inspections can be scheduled online
- Required notice: 24-48 hours
