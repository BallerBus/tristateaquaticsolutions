# Pennsylvania Swimming Pool Permit Guide

## Tri-State Aquatic Solutions - PA Operations

This guide covers the permit requirements for residential swimming pool installations in our Pennsylvania service area, including Montgomery County, Delaware County, and Chester County.

---

## Montgomery County

### Overview
Montgomery County does not issue pool permits directly. All permits are handled at the township/municipality level. Each township has its own building department and specific requirements.

### Major Townships We Serve

#### Lower Merion Township
**Building & Planning Department**
- Address: 75 E. Lancaster Avenue, Ardmore, PA 19003
- Phone: (610) 645-6200
- Website: www.lowermerion.org

**Permit Requirements:**
- Building Permit Application
- Plot Plan (3 copies) showing:
  - Pool location with setbacks from all property lines
  - Distance from house and other structures
  - Fence/barrier location
  - Equipment pad location
- Pool construction drawings (manufacturer specifications)
- Barrier/fence details (height, gate specifications)
- Electrical permit (separate application)
- Plumbing permit (if applicable)

**Setback Requirements:**
- Rear yard: 10 feet minimum from property line
- Side yard: 10 feet minimum from property line
- From dwelling: 10 feet minimum
- From septic systems: 10 feet minimum

**Fees (2024):**
- Building permit: $150 base + $5 per $1,000 of construction cost
- Electrical permit: $75
- Plan review: $50
- Certificate of Occupancy: Included

**Timeline:**
- Plan review: 10-15 business days
- Permit issuance: Same day after approval
- Total process: 2-4 weeks

## Required Documents Checklist

### For All PA Pool Permits

1. **Completed Permit Application**
   - Property owner information
   - Contractor information (Tri-State Aquatic Solutions)
   - Project description
   - Estimated construction cost

2. **Site Plan/Plot Plan**
   - Property boundaries
   - Existing structures (house, shed, etc.)
   - Proposed pool location with dimensions
   - Setbacks from all property lines (clearly labeled)
   - Location of pool equipment
   - Fence/barrier location
   - Scale noted on drawing

3. **Pool Construction Documents**
   - Pool dimensions and depth
   - Construction specifications
   - Materials to be used
   - Equipment specifications (pump, filter, heater)

4. **Barrier/Fence Plan**
   - Fence height (minimum 48" in PA)
   - Gate specifications (self-closing, self-latching)
   - Latch height (54" minimum from ground)
   - Materials specification

5. **Electrical Information**
   - Panel amperage
   - Circuit specifications
   - GFCI protection details
   - Equipment bonding plan

6. **Contractor Documentation**
   - PA Home Improvement Contractor License (HIC#)
   - Certificate of Insurance
   - Workers' Compensation certificate

## Timeline Expectations

### Standard Timeline: 2-4 Weeks

**Week 1:**
- Submit permit application with all required documents
- Initial review by building department
- Corrections requested (if any)

**Week 2:**
- Plan review completion
- Permit approval
- Permit pickup/issuance

**Factors That Can Extend Timeline:**
- Incomplete applications (most common cause of delays)
- Stormwater management review required
- Zoning variance needed
- Historic district review
- HOA approval pending

### Tips for Faster Processing

1. **Complete applications** - Submit all required documents on first submission
2. **Accurate site plans** - Use professional survey if possible
3. **Clear drawings** - Ensure setbacks are clearly labeled
4. **Pre-application meetings** - Some townships offer these to review requirements
5. **Electronic submission** - Many townships now accept online applications

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue 1: Setback Violations
**Problem:** Pool location doesn't meet required setbacks
**Solution:**
- Redesign pool location
- Request variance from Zoning Hearing Board (adds 4-8 weeks, additional fees)
- Consider smaller pool dimensions

### Issue 2: Stormwater Management
**Problem:** Project triggers stormwater review requirements
**Solution:**
- Engage licensed engineer for stormwater management plan
- Design rain gardens or infiltration features
- Reduce total disturbed area if possible

### Issue 3: Conservation Easements
**Problem:** Property has easements restricting construction
**Solution:**
- Research deed restrictions before design
- Work with easement holder for approval
- Relocate pool to unrestricted area

### Issue 4: Utility Conflicts
**Problem:** Pool location conflicts with underground utilities
**Solution:**
- PA One Call (811) for utility marking before design
- Relocate pool away from utilities
- May need utility relocation (contact utility company)

### Issue 5: Incomplete Applications
**Problem:** Township returns application for missing items
**Solution:**
- Use our checklist before submission
- Include all required documents
- Pre-application meeting if available

### Issue 6: Historic District
**Problem:** Property in historic overlay district
**Solution:**
- Additional review by Historic Commission
- May have additional aesthetic requirements
- Allow extra 2-4 weeks for review
