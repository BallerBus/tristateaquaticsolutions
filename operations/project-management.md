## Milestone Definitions

### Milestone 1: Contract Execution

**Definition:** Customer has signed contract and initial deposit received.

**Deliverables:**
- Signed contract on file
- Initial deposit cleared
- Customer information packet sent
- Project entered into management system
- Pre-construction meeting scheduled

**Payment Trigger:** Initial deposit (typically 10-15% of contract)

---

### Milestone 2: Permit Approval

**Definition:** All required permits have been approved by local jurisdiction.

**Deliverables:**
- Approved permit documents received
- Permit posted at job site (or ready to post)
- Customer notified of approval
- Construction schedule confirmed
- Material orders finalized

**Payment Trigger:** Second payment (typically 20-25% of contract)

---

### Milestone 3: Excavation Complete

**Definition:** Pool excavation is complete and approved.

**Deliverables:**
- Excavation matches approved plans (within tolerance)
- Photos documented and filed
- Any soil condition issues documented
- Ready for steel and plumbing

**Payment Trigger:** May trigger payment per contract terms

---

### Milestone 4: Steel & Plumbing Complete

**Definition:** Reinforcement steel and plumbing rough-in complete and inspected.

**Deliverables:**
- Steel installation per engineering specifications
- Plumbing pressure tested and holding
- Required inspection passed
- Bonding verified
- Photos documented and filed

**Payment Trigger:** Third payment (typically 25-30% of contract)

---

### Milestone 5: Shell Complete

**Definition:** Gunite/shotcrete applied and curing initiated.

**Deliverables:**
- Shell applied to specifications
- No visible defects
- Curing process started
- Photos documented and filed

**Payment Trigger:** May trigger payment per contract terms

---

### Milestone 6: Tile & Coping Complete

**Definition:** All tile and coping installed and grouted.

**Deliverables:**
- Waterline tile installed
- Coping installed and sealed
- Quality inspection passed
- Photos documented and filed

**Payment Trigger:** May be combined with decking

---

### Milestone 7: Decking Complete

**Definition:** All concrete/paver decking installed.

**Deliverables:**
- Decking matches approved plans
- Proper drainage verified
- Expansion joints installed
- Photos documented and filed

**Payment Trigger:** Fourth payment (typically 15-20% of contract)

---

### Milestone 8: Equipment Operational

**Definition:** All pool equipment installed and functioning.

**Deliverables:**
- Pump, filter, heater, etc. installed
- Electrical inspection passed
- All systems tested and operational
- Automation programmed
- Photos documented and filed

**Payment Trigger:** May be combined with final

---

### Milestone 9: Project Completion

**Definition:** Pool is finished, filled, and ready for use.

**Deliverables:**
- Interior finish applied and cured
- Pool filled to operating level
- Water chemistry balanced
- All final inspections passed
- Customer orientation completed
- All documentation provided
- Warranty registered

**Payment Trigger:** Final payment (remaining balance, typically 10-15%)

## Issue Escalation Procedures

### Level 1: Site Supervisor Resolution

**Types of Issues:**
- Minor schedule adjustments (< 1 day)
- Minor material substitutions (same quality)
- Minor customer questions
- Routine equipment issues
- Weather delays (< 1 day)

**Response Time:** Same day

**Action:** Resolve on-site and document in daily log

---

### Level 2: Project Manager Resolution

**Types of Issues:**
- Schedule delays (1-3 days)
- Customer complaints
- Material delivery problems
- Minor scope clarifications
- Subcontractor performance issues
- Budget variances (< $500)

**Response Time:** Within 4 hours

**Action:**
1. Site Supervisor notifies Project Manager
2. Project Manager assesses situation
3. Solution implemented or escalated
4. Customer notified if impact expected
5. Documentation updated

---

### Level 3: Operations Manager Resolution

**Types of Issues:**
- Schedule delays (> 3 days)
- Significant customer complaints
- Major design or engineering issues
- Subcontractor failures
- Budget variances ($500-$2,500)
- Safety incidents (no injury)
- Code compliance questions

**Response Time:** Within 2 hours

**Action:**
1. Project Manager notifies Operations Manager
2. Operations Manager reviews with team
3. Remediation plan developed
4. Customer communication coordinated
5. Executive notification if needed
6. Full documentation completed

---

### Level 4: Executive Resolution

**Types of Issues:**
- Major customer disputes
- Legal or liability concerns
- Budget variances (> $2,500)
- Safety incidents with injury
- Regulatory violations
- Media or reputation issues
- Contract disputes

**Response Time:** Immediate

**Action:**
1. Operations Manager notifies Executive
2. All work may be paused pending review
3. Legal consultation if needed
4. Formal remediation plan developed
5. Customer communication from leadership
6. Full documentation and incident report

---

### Escalation Documentation

All escalations Level 2 and above must be documented with:
- Date and time of issue identification
- Description of issue
- Immediate actions taken
- Escalation timeline
- Resolution implemented
- Customer communication log
- Lessons learned (Level 3+)

## Change Order Process

### When Change Orders Are Required

- Any work not included in original contract
- Customer-requested modifications
- Site conditions requiring different approach
- Upgrades or downgrades to specifications
- Schedule acceleration requests
- Design changes

### Change Order Workflow

```
1. Change Identified
        ↓
2. Impact Assessment (24-48 hours)
   - Cost impact
   - Schedule impact
   - Material requirements
   - Labor requirements
        ↓
3. Change Order Document Prepared
   - Detailed description
   - Cost breakdown
   - Schedule impact
   - Terms and conditions
        ↓
4. Customer Presentation
   - Walk through changes
   - Explain impacts
   - Answer questions
        ↓
5. Customer Decision
   - Approve → Proceed to Step 6
   - Reject → Document and continue original scope
   - Negotiate → Return to Step 2
        ↓
6. Signed Change Order Received
        ↓
7. Payment Collected (if required upfront)
        ↓
8. Work Scheduled and Executed
        ↓
9. Change Order Closed
   - Work verified complete
   - Documentation updated
   - Final payment collected
```

### Change Order Pricing

| Change Type | Markup |
|-------------|--------|
| Materials | Cost + 20% |
| Labor | Hourly rate + 15% |
| Subcontractor | Cost + 15% |
| Equipment | Cost + 10% |
| Expedite fee | Case by case |

### Change Order Documentation

Every change order must include:
- Change order number
- Original contract reference
- Detailed scope of change
- Itemized cost breakdown
- Schedule impact statement
- Both party signatures
- Date of execution

## Delay Handling

### Types of Delays

#### Excusable Delays (Not Our Fault)
- Weather (documented conditions)
- Customer-caused delays
- Permit delays beyond normal
- Force majeure events
- Utility company delays
- Material shortages (market-wide)

#### Non-Excusable Delays (Our Responsibility)
- Crew scheduling issues
- Equipment breakdowns
- Material ordering errors
- Subcontractor performance
- Planning errors

### Delay Response Protocol

#### Day 1-2 of Delay

1. Identify root cause
2. Estimate duration
3. Document with photos/records
4. Notify customer verbally
5. Attempt mitigation measures

#### Day 3+ of Delay

1. Formal written notification to customer
2. Updated schedule provided
3. Mitigation plan documented
4. Consider acceleration options
5. Review contract provisions

### Delay Communication Template

```
Subject: Schedule Update - [Project Name]

Dear [Customer],

I'm writing to inform you of a schedule adjustment for your pool project.

SITUATION:
[Description of what occurred]

CAUSE:
[Explanation of cause]

IMPACT:
- Original completion: [Date]
- New estimated completion: [Date]
- Delay duration: [X days]

MITIGATION:
[What we're doing to minimize impact]

NEXT STEPS:
[Immediate actions and timeline]

We understand this is frustrating and appreciate your patience.
Please call me directly with any questions.

Sincerely,
[Project Manager Name]
[Phone Number]
```

### Delay Mitigation Strategies

| Delay Type | Mitigation Options |
|------------|-------------------|
| Weather | Weekend work, extended hours when clear |
| Materials | Source alternatives, expedite shipping |
| Labor | Add crew members, overtime |
| Inspection | Pre-schedule, communicate with inspector |
| Customer | Flexible scheduling, off-hours work |

### Delay Documentation Requirements

All delays must be documented with:
- Date delay began
- Cause of delay
- Weather records (if applicable)
- Photos of conditions
- Communication log with customer
- Mitigation attempts
- Date delay resolved
- Lessons learned

### Customer Compensation for Delays

| Delay Type | Compensation |
|------------|-------------|
| Excusable | None required |
| Non-Excusable (1-7 days) | Goodwill gesture at management discretion |
| Non-Excusable (8-14 days) | $100-250 credit or upgrade |
| Non-Excusable (15+ days) | Per contract terms, negotiate |

## Project Closeout Procedures

### Closeout Checklist

- [ ] All work complete per contract
- [ ] All inspections passed and documented
- [ ] All change orders executed and closed
- [ ] Final payment collected
- [ ] Warranty documentation provided
- [ ] Customer orientation completed
- [ ] As-built drawings provided (if applicable)
- [ ] All permits finalized
- [ ] Customer satisfaction survey sent
- [ ] Project file archived

### Post-Project Review

Within 7 days of closeout:
1. Review project timeline vs. planned
2. Analyze budget performance
3. Document lessons learned
4. Recognize team performance
5. Update templates/procedures if needed
