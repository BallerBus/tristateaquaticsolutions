## Job Site Safety Requirements

### General Site Safety Rules

1. **Daily Safety Briefing**
   - Conducted at start of each work day
   - All personnel must attend
   - Review day's hazards and precautions
   - Document attendance

2. **Site Access Control**
   - Secure perimeter with fencing/barriers
   - Post "Construction Zone - No Unauthorized Entry" signs
   - Control access points
   - Brief homeowner on restrictions

3. **Housekeeping**
   - Keep work areas clean and organized
   - Store materials properly
   - Clear walkways and access routes
   - Remove debris daily

4. **Weather Awareness**
   - Monitor conditions throughout day
   - Stop work during lightning (30 minutes after last strike)
   - Secure materials before storms
   - Know heat illness warning signs

### Excavation Safety

#### OSHA Compliance (29 CFR 1926 Subpart P)

**Before Excavation:**
- [ ] Utility locate completed (call 811, wait for marks)
- [ ] Soil classified by competent person
- [ ] Protective systems planned
- [ ] Access/egress planned
- [ ] Adjacent structures evaluated

**During Excavation:**
- [ ] No personnel in excavation during active digging
- [ ] Competent person on-site
- [ ] Spoil pile minimum 2 feet from edge
- [ ] No heavy equipment near edge
- [ ] Continuous monitoring for hazards

**Excavation Protection Requirements:**

| Depth | Protection Required |
|-------|-------------------|
| < 4 feet | Visual inspection, evaluate hazards |
| 4-8 feet | Sloping, shoring, or shield required |
| > 8 feet | Engineered protective system |

**Prohibited:**
- Working under suspended loads
- Standing under excavator swing area
- Entering excavation without safe access
- Working alone in excavation

### Trench/Deep Excavation Safety

For pool excavations exceeding 4 feet:

1. **Competent Person Designation**
   - Trained to recognize hazards
   - Authority to stop work
   - Present whenever work in excavation

2. **Cave-in Protection**
   - Sloping (1.5:1 ratio minimum for Type C soil)
   - Shoring if sloping not feasible
   - Continuous monitoring for changes

3. **Access and Egress**
   - Ladder or steps within 25 feet of workers
   - Ladder extends 3 feet above edge
   - Protected from falling materials

4. **Atmospheric Testing**
   - Required if potential for hazardous atmosphere
   - Before entry each day
   - Continuous if conditions warrant

### Hot Work Safety

For welding, cutting, or other spark-producing work:

1. **Hot Work Permit** required
2. **Fire watch** during and 30 minutes after
3. **Fire extinguisher** within 25 feet
4. **Clear area** of combustibles (35-foot radius)
5. **No hot work** near open excavation if gas risk

### Electrical Safety

1. **Ground Fault Protection**
   - GFCI on all temporary power
   - Test before each use
   - Replace damaged cords immediately

2. **Lockout/Tagout**
   - Before any electrical work
   - Only qualified electricians on electrical systems
   - Verify de-energized before contact

3. **Overhead Lines**
   - Maintain minimum 10-foot clearance
   - Use spotters near power lines
   - Lower antenna/attachments when moving equipment

4. **Temporary Power**
   - Proper rated extension cords
   - No daisy-chaining
   - Protect from water and traffic
   - Inspect daily

### Confined Space Entry

Pool excavations may constitute confined spaces.

**Confined Space Indicators:**
- Large enough to enter
- Limited or restricted entry/exit
- Not designed for continuous occupancy

**If Confined Space Conditions Exist:**
- Entry permit required
- Atmospheric testing required
- Attendant required outside
- Rescue plan in place

### Silica Exposure Control

Gunite/shotcrete and concrete cutting create respirable crystalline silica.

**Controls Required:**
1. Wet methods when cutting/grinding
2. Dust collection equipment when applicable
3. Respiratory protection (N95 minimum)
4. Limit exposure duration
5. Decontamination procedures

**Medical Surveillance:**
- Initial baseline for exposed workers
- Every 3 years with ongoing exposure
- Per OSHA Table 1 requirements

## Equipment Operation

### General Equipment Safety

1. **Only Trained Operators**
   - Training documented on file
   - Equipment-specific certification where required
   - No unauthorized operation

2. **Pre-Operation Inspection**
   - Walk-around visual inspection
   - Check fluid levels
   - Test safety devices
   - Report defects immediately
   - Do not operate defective equipment

3. **Safe Operation**
   - Wear seatbelt if equipped
   - No riders unless seat provided
   - Operate at safe speeds
   - Follow manufacturer guidelines
   - Stay alert and focused

4. **Shut Down Procedure**
   - Lower attachments to ground
   - Set parking brake
   - Turn off engine
   - Remove key when leaving
   - Secure against unauthorized use

### Excavator Operation

**Pre-Operation:**
- [ ] 360-degree walk-around
- [ ] Check hydraulic lines and connections
- [ ] Verify tracks/undercarriage condition
- [ ] Check mirrors and cameras
- [ ] Test all controls
- [ ] Verify swing area clear

**During Operation:**
- Establish swing zone barriers
- No personnel in swing zone
- Use spotter for blind spots
- Watch for utilities (even after locate)
- Maintain safe distance from excavation edge
- Three-point contact entering/exiting

**Special Procedures:**
- Slope work: Never exceed equipment limits
- Loading trucks: Keep bucket low while swinging
- Travel: Bucket forward, boom low

### Skid Steer/Compact Equipment

**Pre-Operation:**
- [ ] Walk-around inspection
- [ ] Test backup alarm
- [ ] Check tire/track condition
- [ ] Verify lift capacity for planned loads
- [ ] Ensure seat belt functions

**During Operation:**
- Always use seat belt and restraint bar
- Keep bucket/forks low while traveling
- Watch for overhead obstructions
- Use caution on slopes
- No riders

### Concrete/Gunite Equipment

**Pump Trucks:**
- Set on level, stable ground
- Deploy outriggers fully
- Establish exclusion zone around boom
- Check hose connections
- Clear communication with nozzle operator

**Hose Handling:**
- Never kink high-pressure hose
- Secure all connections
- Bleed pressure before disconnecting
- Wear proper PPE
- Clear area before starting flow

### Power Tools

**General Requirements:**
- Use tools only for intended purpose
- Inspect before each use
- Replace damaged tools
- Use correct blade/bit for material
- Secure work before cutting

**Specific Tools:**

| Tool | Key Safety Requirements |
|------|------------------------|
| Circular saw | Guard functional, proper blade, secure work |
| Angle grinder | Guard in place, correct wheel, face shield |
| Hammer drill | Secure SDS bit, handle controls, dust control |
| Chop saw | Guard functional, material secured |
| Tile saw | Water flowing, proper blade, eye protection |

### Vehicle Safety

**Company Vehicles:**
- Valid driver's license required
- No cell phone use while driving
- Follow all traffic laws
- Secure loads properly
- Pre-trip inspection (lights, tires, mirrors)
- Report accidents immediately

**Towing:**
- Verify vehicle rated for load
- Check hitch and safety chains
- Test brake lights
- Adjust mirrors
- Allow extra stopping distance

## PPE Requirements

### Minimum Site PPE

The following is required for ALL personnel on active construction sites:

| PPE | Standard | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Hard hat | ANSI Z89.1 Type I or II | When overhead hazards present |
| Safety glasses | ANSI Z87.1 | At all times |
| Safety footwear | ASTM F2413 (steel/composite toe) | At all times |
| High-visibility vest | ANSI Class 2 minimum | When traffic present |
| Work gloves | Task-appropriate | Recommended |

### Task-Specific PPE

| Task | Additional PPE Required |
|------|------------------------|
| Excavation (operating equipment) | None beyond minimum |
| Working in excavation | Hard hat required |
| Steel tying | Leather gloves, eye protection |
| Gunite application | Respirator (N95+), goggles, ear protection |
| Tile cutting | Face shield, hearing protection, dust mask |
| Concrete pouring | Rubber boots, long sleeves, chemical gloves |
| Welding | Welding helmet, leather gloves, leather jacket |
| Electrical work | Insulated gloves (rated), non-conductive tools |
| Chemical handling | Chemical gloves, goggles, per SDS |

### PPE Standards

**Eye Protection:**
- ANSI Z87.1 rated
- Side shields for impact hazards
- Face shields for splash/grind hazards
- Welding shade per application

**Hearing Protection:**
- Required when noise > 85 dBA
- NRR 25+ recommended
- Plugs or muffs acceptable
- Consistent use required

**Respiratory Protection:**
- Minimum N95 for dust
- Half-face with P100 for extended dust exposure
- Full-face for chemical exposure
- Fit testing required annually

**Gloves:**
- Task-matched material
- Leather for handling materials
- Chemical-resistant for chemicals
- Cut-resistant when specified
- Never wear near rotating equipment

**Fall Protection:**
- Required above 6 feet (general industry standard)
- Full body harness for fall arrest
- Inspect before each use
- Anchor must support 5,000 lbs

### PPE Maintenance

- Inspect before each use
- Replace damaged PPE immediately
- Clean per manufacturer instructions
- Store properly when not in use
- Never modify PPE

### PPE Provision

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions provides:
- Hard hats (replace when damaged)
- Safety glasses (replace when needed)
- High-visibility vests
- Hearing protection
- Respiratory protection
- Task-specific PPE

Employee provides:
- Safety footwear
- Work gloves (basic)

## Incident Reporting

### Reporting Requirements

**All incidents must be reported immediately**, including:
- Injuries (any severity)
- Near misses
- Property damage
- Equipment damage
- Vehicle accidents
- Safety hazard observations

### Reporting Process

```
1. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
   - Ensure scene is safe
   - Provide first aid if trained
   - Call 911 if emergency
   - Secure scene if investigation needed
        ↓
2. NOTIFY SUPERVISOR
   - Immediately for injuries
   - Same day for near misses/damage
   - Provide basic information
        ↓
3. COMPLETE INCIDENT REPORT
   - Within 24 hours
   - Full details as known
   - Witness statements
   - Photos if applicable
        ↓
4. INVESTIGATION
   - Root cause analysis
   - Contributing factors
   - Corrective actions identified
        ↓
5. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
   - Implement fixes
   - Communicate lessons learned
   - Update procedures if needed
        ↓
6. FOLLOW-UP
   - Verify corrective actions effective
   - Close report
```

### OSHA Reporting Requirements

**Immediately (within 8 hours):**
- Fatality
- In-patient hospitalization
- Amputation
- Loss of an eye

**Within 7 days:**
- Report on OSHA Form 300

**Annually:**
- Post OSHA 300A summary (Feb 1 - Apr 30)
- Electronic submission if required

### Near Miss Reporting

Near misses are equally important as incidents.

**Definition:** An event that could have resulted in injury, illness, or damage but did not.

**Examples:**
- Tool dropped from height (no one hit)
- Trip/slip recovered without falling
- Vehicle near-collision
- Equipment malfunction caught before failure
- Utility strike (no injury)

**Reporting:** Same process as incidents, use "Near Miss" category

### Workers' Compensation

If an employee is injured:

1. **Provide immediate first aid**
2. **Seek medical attention** if needed
3. **Report to supervisor** immediately
4. **Complete incident report** same day
5. **File WC claim** if medical treatment beyond first aid
6. **Follow up** on return to work status
7. **Document** all communications

- Emergency contact list posted
- Communication device (cell phone)
- Eyewash (if chemicals in use)

### Emergency Response Procedures

#### Medical Emergency

```
1. ASSESS SCENE SAFETY
   - Remove yourself from danger
   - Do not become a victim
        ↓
2. CALL FOR HELP
   - Call 911
   - Provide: Location, nature of emergency, number of victims
   - Stay on line until released
        ↓
3. PROVIDE FIRST AID (if trained)
   - Control bleeding
   - CPR if needed and trained
   - Do not move victim unless in danger
        ↓
4. GUIDE EMS TO LOCATION
   - Send someone to street
   - Clear access path
        ↓
5. NOTIFY SUPERVISOR
   - As soon as practical
   - Provide available information
        ↓
6. DOCUMENT
   - Witness names
   - What happened
   - Actions taken
```

#### Fire Emergency

```
1. ALERT
   - Yell "FIRE!" to alert others
   - Activate alarm if available
        ↓
2. EVACUATE
   - Everyone leaves work area
   - Designated meeting point
        ↓
3. CALL 911
   - From safe location
   - Provide address and information
        ↓
4. EXTINGUISH (if safe)
   - Only if small and contained
   - Only if you have clear exit
   - PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep
   - If in doubt, GET OUT
        ↓
5. ACCOUNT FOR PERSONNEL
   - Headcount at meeting point
   - Report missing persons to fire department
        ↓
6. DO NOT RE-ENTER
   - Until fire department clears
```

#### Severe Weather

**Thunderstorm/Lightning:**
- Suspend outdoor work at first sign
- 30-30 rule: If thunder follows lightning by 30 seconds or less, take shelter
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder to resume
- Lower equipment booms/heights
- Seek shelter in vehicle or building (not under trees)

**Tornado/High Winds:**
- Monitor weather alerts
- Know shelter locations
- Stop work, secure materials
- Move to lowest floor, interior room
- Stay away from windows

**Extreme Heat:**
- Monitor heat index
- Provide shade and water
- Frequent breaks
- Know signs of heat illness
- Buddy system

| Heat Index | Work/Rest Guidelines |
|------------|---------------------|
| < 91°F | Normal work, stay hydrated |
| 91-103°F | Light duty, extra breaks, shade |
| 103-115°F | Reduce activity, mandatory breaks |
| > 115°F | Stop work, emergency only |

#### Excavation Emergency (Cave-in)

```
1. DO NOT ENTER EXCAVATION
   - Rescue attempt may cause additional collapse
   - You may become a victim
        ↓
2. CALL 911
   - Request technical rescue team
   - Provide location and situation
        ↓
3. SHUT DOWN EQUIPMENT
   - Prevent vibration
   - No vehicle movement near excavation
        ↓
4. IF VICTIM VISIBLE AND CONSCIOUS
   - Maintain verbal contact
   - Provide reassurance
   - Do not enter
        ↓
5. CLEAR AREA
   - Keep everyone back
   - Prevent further collapse
        ↓
6. WAIT FOR RESCUE TEAM
   - Only trained rescuers enter
   - Provide information to responders
```

#### Utility Strike

**Gas Line:**
1. Evacuate area immediately
2. Do not use phones near leak
3. Do not start vehicles
4. Call 911 from safe distance
5. Call gas company emergency line
6. Do not attempt repair

**Electrical:**
1. Assume line is energized
2. Do not touch victim if in contact
3. Call 911
4. Call utility emergency line
5. Keep everyone 35+ feet away
6. Wait for utility to de-energize

**Water:**
1. Shut off equipment
2. Call water utility
3. Document damage
4. Protect excavation from flooding

#### Chemical Spill/Exposure

```
1. REMOVE FROM EXPOSURE
   - Move to fresh air
   - Remove contaminated clothing
        ↓
2. DECONTAMINATE
   - Skin: Flush with water 15+ minutes
   - Eyes: Flush with water 15+ minutes
        ↓
3. CALL FOR HELP
   - 911 if serious
   - Poison Control if minor
        ↓
4. REFER TO SDS
   - Identify substance
   - Follow specific instructions
        ↓
5. CONTAIN SPILL (if safe)
   - Use appropriate absorbent
   - Prevent spread to drains
        ↓
6. REPORT
   - Notify supervisor
   - Document incident
```
