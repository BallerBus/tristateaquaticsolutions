## Standard Service Protocols

### Tier 1 - Essential Care Protocol

**Time Target:** 20-25 minutes

**Step 1: Water Testing (5 minutes)**
1. Collect water sample from elbow depth
2. Test pH using appropriate kit
3. Test free chlorine level
4. Test total alkalinity
5. Record all readings in service app

**Step 2: Chemical Adjustment (5 minutes)**
1. Calculate required chemical additions
2. Add pH adjuster if needed (target: 7.4-7.6)
3. Add chlorine as needed (target: 2-4 ppm)
4. Add alkalinity increaser if needed (target: 80-120 ppm)
5. Broadcast chemicals away from returns

**Step 3: Surface Cleaning (8 minutes)**
1. Skim entire surface with leaf net
2. Remove large debris from floor if visible
3. Clear pool perimeter of organic material
4. Empty skimmer basket(s)
5. Empty pump strainer basket

**Step 4: Basic Equipment Check (3 minutes)**
1. Verify pump is running
2. Check filter pressure (record reading)
3. Note any visible leaks or issues
4. Ensure timer/automation functioning
5. Check water level

**Step 5: Documentation (2 minutes)**
1. Enter all chemical readings
2. Log chemicals added with quantities
3. Note any concerns or observations
4. Take photo if unusual conditions
5. Mark service complete

---

### Tier 2 - Complete Care Protocol

**Time Target:** 35-45 minutes

**All Tier 1 steps, plus:**

**Step 6: Extended Water Testing (3 minutes)**
1. Test calcium hardness (monthly)
2. Test cyanuric acid (CYA) - monthly
3. Test TDS (quarterly)
4. Test phosphates if algae concerns
5. Check salt level (salt pools)

**Step 7: Comprehensive Cleaning (15 minutes)**
1. Brush walls starting at tile line
2. Brush steps and benches
3. Brush floor toward main drain
4. Vacuum debris as needed
5. Clean tile line (spot cleaning)
6. Brush around fittings and lights

**Step 8: Equipment Inspection (5 minutes)**
1. Record filter pressure
2. Check for suction/return flow issues
3. Inspect visible plumbing
4. Check equipment pad for leaks
5. Verify heater/heat pump status
6. Inspect salt cell (salt pools)
7. Test automation functions

**Step 9: Monthly Filter Service (when scheduled)**
1. Note filter pressure before cleaning
2. Backwash sand/DE filters OR
3. Remove and hose cartridge elements
4. Reassemble properly
5. Record post-clean pressure

**Step 12: Premium Communication**

### One-Time Service Protocols

**Green Pool Recovery Protocol:**

1. Assessment and documentation
2. Remove large debris
3. Verify equipment functionality
4. Shock treatment calculation (30 ppm target)
5. Brush entire pool
6. Run filter 24/7
7. Return in 24-48 hours
8. Repeat shock if needed
9. Clarifier treatment when chlorine stable
10. Final brush and vacuum
11. Balance chemistry
12. Document recovery with before/after photos

**Pool Opening Protocol:**

1. Remove and store winter cover
2. Remove plugs from returns and skimmers
3. Reinstall eyeball fittings and skimmer baskets
4. Fill pool to proper level
5. Prime and start pump
6. Inspect all equipment
7. Clean filter
8. Test water chemistry
9. Shock and balance water
10. Brush and vacuum pool
11. Program automation/timers
12. Customer walkthrough and handoff

**Pool Closing Protocol:**

1. Clean pool thoroughly
2. Balance chemistry (lower chlorine, pH, alkalinity)
3. Add winterizing chemicals
4. Lower water level below tile line
5. Drain equipment and plumbing
6. Install winter plugs
7. Disconnect pump and filter
8. Drain heater (if applicable)
9. Cover equipment
10. Install safety/winter cover
11. Document closing completion
12. Schedule spring opening

## Post-Service Documentation

### Required Documentation Per Visit

**Water Chemistry Log:**
- Date and time of service
- All chemical readings (numeric values)
- Chemicals added (product and quantity)
- Target vs. actual levels

**Service Activity Log:**
- Services performed (checklist)
- Time on site
- Equipment readings (filter pressure, salt level)
- Before/after photos (if conditions warrant)

**Observation Notes:**
- Equipment concerns
- Water quality issues
- Customer requests
- Safety hazards
- Recommended follow-up

### Photo Documentation Requirements

**Always Photograph:**
- Green or discolored water
- Equipment issues or damage
- Unusual debris or conditions
- Completed repair work
- Before/after for major services
- Access issues or safety concerns

**Photo Standards:**
- Clear, well-lit images
- Include reference points for scale
- Timestamp visible in metadata
- Upload same day to customer record

### Service Report Delivery

**Standard Delivery (Tier 1 & 2):**
- Email summary within 24 hours
- Include chemical readings
- Note any concerns or recommendations
- Accessible via customer portal

**Premium Delivery (Tier 3):**
- Same-day detailed email report
- Include photos of service
- Personal notes from technician
- Direct contact information
- Proactive suggestions

## Quality Verification

### Self-Inspection Checklist

Before leaving every job, verify:

**Pool Condition:**
- [ ] Water clear and debris-free
- [ ] Chemical readings in acceptable range
- [ ] Tile line clean
- [ ] No floating debris
- [ ] Water level correct

**Equipment Status:**
- [ ] Pump running normally
- [ ] Filter pressure appropriate
- [ ] No visible leaks
- [ ] Automation functioning
- [ ] Heater status correct

**Site Condition:**
- [ ] No tools or supplies left behind
- [ ] Deck area clean
- [ ] Gate secured
- [ ] Equipment pad organized
- [ ] No trip hazards created

### Quality Audit Program

**Random Quality Audits:**
- 10% of service visits audited
- Lead technician or manager visits
- Unannounced arrival after service
- Checklist evaluation
- Customer feedback collection

**Quality Metrics Tracked:**
- Chemical accuracy (readings vs. targets)
- Cleanliness standards met
- Documentation completeness
- Time on site vs. standard
- Customer satisfaction scores

**Audit Scoring:**

| Rating | Score | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Excellent | 95-100 | Exceeds all standards |
| Good | 85-94 | Meets all standards |
| Acceptable | 75-84 | Minor improvements needed |
| Needs Improvement | 65-74 | Training required |
| Unacceptable | <65 | Immediate corrective action |

### Corrective Action Process

**For Quality Issues:**
1. Document specific deficiency
2. Review with technician same day
3. Identify root cause
4. Provide additional training if needed
5. Re-audit within 2 weeks
6. Progressive discipline if repeated

## Customer Sign-Off

### When Sign-Off Required

**Always Required:**
- Major equipment repairs (>$300)
- Renovation or construction work
- Pool opening/closing
- Any warranty service
- Work with change orders

**Optional but Recommended:**
- First visit to new customer
- Unusual service conditions
- Customer complaint follow-up
- Premium tier customers

### Sign-Off Methods

**In-Person Signature:**
1. Walk customer through completed work
2. Explain findings and recommendations
3. Answer questions
4. Collect signature on work order
5. Provide copy to customer

**Electronic Signature:**
1. Complete service documentation
2. Send summary to customer
3. Request electronic approval
4. Store signed document
5. Confirm receipt

**Photo Verification (when customer unavailable):**
1. Document completed work with photos
2. Timestamp and location verify
3. Send completion notification
4. Follow up for any concerns
5. Note customer unavailable in system

### Post-Service Follow-Up

**24-Hour Follow-Up (repairs and issues):**
- Confirm equipment operating correctly
- Verify no new concerns
- Address any questions
- Document follow-up completion

**Satisfaction Survey (automated):**
- Sent 24-48 hours after service
- Simple rating system
- Open comment field
- Trigger alert for low scores

## Emergency Service Procedures

### Emergency Classification

**Priority 1 - Immediate (respond within 4 hours):**
- Complete equipment failure (no filtration)
- Major leak (losing >1 inch/day)
- Electrical hazard
- Safety cover failure
- Main drain issue

**Priority 2 - Urgent (respond within 24 hours):**
- Green pool (health hazard)
- Pump failure
- Heater failure (commercial/rental)
- Partial equipment failure

**Priority 3 - Standard (next available):**
- Minor equipment issues
- Cosmetic concerns
- Non-critical repairs
- Scheduled maintenance issues

### Emergency Response Protocol

1. Receive emergency call/notification
2. Classify priority level
3. Contact available technician
4. Dispatch with ETA to customer
5. Assess situation on-site
6. Stabilize if possible (temporary fix)
7. Quote permanent repair
8. Complete repair or schedule
9. Document thoroughly
10. Follow up within 24 hours
