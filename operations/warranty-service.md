## Warranty Terms

### Standard Warranty Coverage

#### Structural Warranty

| Component | Coverage Period | What's Covered |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Pool Shell (Gunite/Concrete) | 25 Years | Structural integrity, water loss from shell failure |
| Pool Shell (Fiberglass) | Lifetime (structural) | Structural defects, osmotic blistering |
| Steel Reinforcement | 25 Years | Structural failure due to steel |
| Plumbing (Underground) | 10 Years | Leaks, pipe failure |

#### Surface Warranty

| Component | Coverage Period | What's Covered |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Plaster/Pebble Finish | 3-5 Years* | Delamination, structural cracking |
| Tile | 3 Years | Detachment, manufacturer defects |
| Coping | 3 Years | Cracking (non-cosmetic), detachment |
| Decking | 2 Years | Structural cracking, settling |

*Varies by finish type selected

#### Equipment Warranty

| Equipment | Coverage Period | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Pumps | 3 Years (mfg) | Manufacturer warranty, registered |
| Filters | 2-3 Years (mfg) | Manufacturer warranty |
| Heaters | 2-5 Years (mfg) | Varies by manufacturer |
| Salt Cells | 3-5 Years (mfg) | Pro-rated after year 1 |
| Automation | 3 Years (mfg) | Manufacturer warranty |
| Lighting | 1-3 Years (mfg) | Manufacturer warranty |
| Labor on Equipment | 1 Year (company) | Installation labor |

### Warranty Exclusions

The following are NOT covered under warranty:

#### General Exclusions
- Normal wear and tear
- Cosmetic issues not affecting function
- Damage from improper water chemistry
- Damage from freezing (failure to winterize)
- Damage from external sources (trees, vehicles, etc.)
- Acts of nature (earthquake, flood, lightning)
- Unauthorized modifications or repairs
- Commercial use (unless specified in contract)
- Neglect or lack of maintenance
- Calcium/scale buildup

#### Specific Exclusions

**Surface Finish:**
- Staining from metals or organics
- Etching from low pH
- Scaling from high calcium
- Color variation within normal range
- Crazing (hairline surface cracks)
- Normal surface roughness development

**Equipment:**
- Damage from power surges
- Improper chemical handling
- Operating without water flow
- Pest damage (rodents chewing wires)

**Deck/Hardscape:**
- Hairline/shrinkage cracks
- Color variation
- Surface wear
- Staining
- Settling from soil conditions

### Warranty Validation Requirements

Warranty is valid only when:

1. **Registration Complete**
   - Equipment registered with manufacturers within 30 days
   - Pool registered with Tri-State within 30 days

2. **Proper Maintenance Documented**
   - Water chemistry logs available (if disputed)
   - Winterization performed by qualified party
   - No evidence of neglect

3. **No Unauthorized Modifications**
   - Original installation intact
   - No third-party repairs without approval
   - Equipment not tampered with

4. **Timely Reporting**
   - Issues reported within 30 days of discovery
   - Structural issues reported immediately

## Claim Handling Process

### Claim Categories

| Category | Priority | Response Target | Resolution Target |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Emergency (leak, safety) | Urgent | 4 hours | 24-48 hours |
| Equipment Failure | High | 24 hours | 3-5 business days |
| Surface Issue | Medium | 48 hours | 10 business days |
| Cosmetic/Minor | Standard | 5 business days | 20 business days |

### Warranty Claim Process

```
1. CLAIM RECEIVED
   Customer contacts: Phone/Email/Portal
   Information collected:
   - Customer/address
   - Description of issue
   - When noticed
   - Photos if available
        ↓
2. CLAIM LOGGED
   Assigned claim number
   Categorized and prioritized
   Initial response sent
        ↓
3. INITIAL ASSESSMENT
   Review:
   - Warranty status
   - Installation date
   - Previous claims
   - Maintenance history
   Decision: Schedule inspection or request more info
        ↓
4. ON-SITE INSPECTION
   Technician dispatched
   Inspection performed
   Photos and documentation
   Preliminary determination
        ↓
5. COVERAGE DETERMINATION
   Analyze findings against warranty terms
   Categories:
   □ Covered - proceed with repair
   □ Partially covered - discuss options
   □ Not covered - provide estimate
   □ Manufacturer warranty - coordinate
        ↓
6. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION
   Explain findings and determination
   Discuss resolution options
   Obtain approval if cost involved
        ↓
7. REPAIR SCHEDULED
   Parts ordered if needed
   Service appointment set
   Customer confirmed
        ↓
8. REPAIR COMPLETED
   Work performed
   Quality verified
   Customer sign-off
        ↓
9. CLAIM CLOSED
   Documentation completed
   Warranty tracking updated
   Follow-up scheduled
```

### Coverage Determination Guidelines

**APPROVE coverage when:**
- Issue matches covered component
- Within warranty period
- No evidence of exclusion causes
- Proper maintenance evident
- No unauthorized modifications

**PARTIALLY APPROVE when:**
- Issue partially due to covered defect
- Normal wear contributed
- Customer shares responsibility
- Component at end of normal life

**DENY coverage when:**
- Outside warranty period
- Clear exclusion cause
- Evidence of neglect
- Unauthorized modification
- Customer-caused damage

### Denial Communication

When denying a claim, always:

1. Explain finding clearly
2. Reference specific warranty provision
3. Provide evidence/photos
4. Offer paid repair option with estimate
5. Explain appeal process
6. Document all communication

## Service Scheduling

### Service Categories and Response Times

| Category | Description | Target Response |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Emergency | Active leak, safety hazard, no heat in freezing weather | 4 hours |
| Urgent | Equipment down, pool unusable | 24 hours |
| Priority | Reduced function, upcoming event | 2-3 business days |
| Standard | Routine warranty, non-urgent | 5-7 business days |
| Scheduled | Pre-planned service, opening/closing | Customer preferred date |

### Scheduling Process

#### Inbound Call/Request

1. **Gather Information**
   - Customer name and address
   - Contact phone and email
   - Description of issue
   - Urgency level
   - Schedule preferences

2. **Check Customer Record**
   - Warranty status
   - Equipment inventory
   - Previous service history
   - Open work orders

3. **Categorize and Schedule**
   - Assign priority
   - Check technician availability
   - Match skills to issue
   - Confirm with customer

4. **Create Work Order**
   - Document all information
   - Attach relevant history
   - Note special instructions
   - Assign technician

#### Appointment Confirmation

Send/call confirmation including:
- Date and time window
- Technician name (if known)
- Description of planned service
- Any preparation needed by customer
- Contact for changes

**Confirmation Template (Text):**
```
Tri-State Service Confirmation:
Date: [Date]
Window: [Time range]
Service: [Brief description]

Please ensure pool area is accessible.
Questions? Call [Number]
Reply CONFIRM or call to reschedule.
```

### Day-of Service Protocol

**Morning:**
- Technician receives route and work orders
- Reviews each job's requirements
- Loads required parts and equipment
- Confirms arrival windows with customers (for AM appointments)

**Arrival:**
- Text customer 15-30 minutes before arrival
- Present company ID
- Confirm issue with customer
- Review scope of work

**During Service:**
- Perform diagnosis
- Communicate findings to customer
- Obtain approval for any additional work
- Complete repairs
- Test system operation
- Clean work area

**Departure:**
- Review completed work with customer
- Explain any maintenance recommendations
- Obtain signature on work order
- Collect payment if applicable
- Provide copy of service record

### Scheduling Policies

**Appointment Windows:**
- Morning: 8 AM - 12 PM
- Afternoon: 12 PM - 5 PM
- Specific time: +$50 precision appointment fee

**Cancellation/Reschedule:**
- Customer: 24-hour notice preferred
- Company: Will notify ASAP and priority reschedule

**No-Show (Customer):**
- Attempt contact on-site
- Leave door hanger with contact info
- Charge trip fee if pattern develops

**Weekend/Holiday:**
- Emergency only
- 1.5x standard labor rate

## Parts Ordering

### Parts Inventory Management

#### Truck Stock (Minimum)

Each service vehicle carries:

**Pump/Motor Parts:**
- Shaft seals (common sizes)
- Capacitors (common sizes)
- Gasket/O-ring assortment
- Pump baskets (2-3 types)
- Impellers (common)

**Filter Parts:**
- Cartridge filters (common sizes)
- DE filter grids (common)
- Multiport gaskets
- Pressure gauges

**Plumbing:**
- PVC fittings assortment
- Valve parts (common)
- Unions and connectors
- Hose sections
- Clamps

**Electrical:**
- Timers (mechanical/digital)
- Relays and contactors
- Wire connectors
- Fuses

**Miscellaneous:**
- Light bulbs/LED replacements
- Skimmer baskets
- Weir doors
- Eyeball fittings
- Lubricants and sealants

### Parts Ordering Process

#### Standard Parts Order

```
1. Technician identifies need
   - From service call diagnosis
   - From truck stock depletion
        ↓
2. Order request submitted
   - Part description
   - Part number (if known)
   - Equipment model/serial
   - Customer and job info
   - Urgency level
        ↓
3. Parts coordinator processes
   - Verify part number
   - Check local inventory
   - Check vendor availability
   - Place order
        ↓
4. Order tracking
   - Confirm ship date
   - Track delivery
   - Notify when received
        ↓
5. Parts received
   - Verify correct parts
   - Assign to job
   - Schedule installation
```

#### Emergency Parts

For urgent needs:
1. Check all local inventory (shop, other trucks)
2. Contact local suppliers for same-day availability
3. Will-call pickup if available
4. Consider overnight shipping
5. Customer notified of any delay

### Manufacturer Warranty Parts

For equipment under manufacturer warranty:

1. **Confirm Warranty Status**
   - Check installation date
   - Verify registration
   - Review warranty terms

2. **Contact Manufacturer**
   - Submit warranty claim per their process
   - Provide photos and documentation
   - Obtain authorization

3. **Parts Sourcing**
   - Manufacturer ships part (most common)
   - Or authorize local purchase with credit

4. **Labor Coverage**
   - Manufacturer labor allowance (if any)
   - Our labor warranty (first year)
   - Customer responsible for labor (after first year)

### Parts Tracking

All parts orders tracked with:
- Job number
- Customer name
- Part description
- Part number
- Vendor/source
- Order date
- Expected arrival
- Cost
- Status (ordered/shipped/received/installed)

### Return Parts Process

Unused parts return:
- Must be in original packaging
- Within 30 days of purchase
- With receipt/packing slip
- Restocking fee may apply

Defective parts:
- Document defect with photos
- Contact vendor for RA
- Return per vendor process
- Track credit receipt
