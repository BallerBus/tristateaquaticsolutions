## Pricing at Target Margins

| Package | Cost | @ 35% Margin | @ 40% Margin | @ 45% Margin |
|---------|------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Basic (T1)** | $82 | $126 | $137 | $149 |
| **Basic (T2)** | $87 | $134 | $145 | $158 |
| **Standard (T2)** | $119 | $183 | $198 | $216 |

### Recommended Prices (Rounded, Market-Aligned)

| Package | Price | Effective Margin | $/hr Revenue |
|---------|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Essential Opening** | **$225** | 64% on T1 / 61% on T2 | $225/hr |
| **Complete Opening** | **$350** | 66% on T2 / 63% on T3 | $233-280/hr |
| **Swim-Ready Opening** | **$525** | 51% on T3 | $175/hr (but 2 visits) |

> **Why these are higher than the cost formula suggests:** Your rough draft had market rates of $250-550. Your costs are LOW relative to market. This means the market supports prices well above your cost-based floor. **Don't leave money on the table** — price to market, not to cost-plus. Your cost analysis just confirms you have healthy margins at market rates.
