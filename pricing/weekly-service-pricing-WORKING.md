## ⚠️ Questions to Resolve

### 1. In-ground vs gallon pricing — which applies?
You have two structures:
- **By type:** In-ground $125, above-ground $150
- **By gallons:** 0-30K $125, 31-60K $150, 60K+ $180

Above-ground pools are almost always under 30K gallons, so they'd be $125 on the gallon scale but $150 on the type scale. **Pick one system.** Gallon-based is more defensible — it ties price to actual work/chemicals required.

**Suggestion:** Drop the type distinction, go gallon-only:

| Pool Size | Service Only | With Chemicals |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| 0–30K gal | $125/week | $150/week |
| 31–60K gal | $150/week | $175/week |
| 60K+ gal | $180/week | $205/week |

Above-ground pools naturally fall into the 0-30K tier. Simpler for quoting, easier to justify to customers ("bigger pool = more work & chemicals").

### 2. Above-ground priced higher than in-ground — INTENTIONAL ✅
Above-ground pools have inferior equipment that's more time-consuming to service. Cheap pumps, flimsy filters, harder access, more troubleshooting. The $25/week premium is justified by the extra labor time per visit.
