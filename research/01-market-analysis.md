## Competition Landscape

### Major Competitors

**Anthony & Sylvan Pools** (Warminster, PA)
- National brand, Forbes "Best Pool Installation" 5 years
- Pricing: 20'x40' ~$61,000 base; 18'x40' freeform w/spa ~$100,000
- **CRITICAL WEAKNESS**: 173 BBB complaints in 3 years, only 56 resolved
- Documented issues: warranty disputes, 19-day waits for repairs, structural failures

**Carlton Pools** (Delaware County)
- 50+ years, family-owned
- Custom concrete/gunite
- Retail stores in West Chester and Collegeville

**Scott Payne Custom Pools** (Main Line/Ardmore)
- Concrete/gunite and fiberglass
- 3D design services

**Ted's Pools** (Chester County)
- Regional presence in Main Line

### Market Gaps Identified
1. **Post-sale service gap** - Anthony & Sylvan's poor service creates opportunity
2. **Local trust deficit** - National companies perceived as impersonal
3. **Fiberglass specialists underrepresented** - Most focus on gunite
4. **Mid-market value opportunity** - Between premium gunite and commodity vinyl

---

## Seasonal Patterns

- **Peak Selling Season**: Late May to early June
- **Best Construction Conditions**: Fall (softer, drier ground)
- **Lead Time**: Spring installations must be booked 12+ months in advance
- **Strategy**: Sell in fall/winter for spring installation

## Plunge Pool Opportunity

### Market Growth
- 7.1% CAGR (portable pool market to reach $2.55B by 2035)

### Dimensions
- Cocktail pools: 9-13' x 5-7' (4' depth max)
- Standard plunge: 8-12' wide x 12-20' long

### Pricing
- Professional installation: $30,000-$50,000
- Position as "affordable luxury" vs full-size pools ($85,000+)

### ROI for Homeowners
- Average pool ROI: 5-7%
- Listing price premium: ~8%
- Pittsburgh PA shows 11.5% home value increase with pools
