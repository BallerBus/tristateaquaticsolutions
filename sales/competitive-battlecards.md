# Competitive Battlecards
## Tri-State Aquatic Solutions

**Created:** February 2026
**Purpose:** Competitive intelligence for sales positioning
**Note:** This document is for internal use only. Never disparage competitors directly to customers.

---

## Competitive Positioning Philosophy

**Our Approach:**
- Lead with our strengths, not their weaknesses
- Encourage customers to ask the same questions of all companies
- Let quality differences speak for themselves
- Focus on what we DO, not what they DON'T do

**When Competitors Come Up:**
1. Acknowledge them professionally
2. Share differentiating questions to ask
3. Provide references who can speak to our experience
4. Win on trust, communication, and value

## 1. Anthony Sylvan Pools

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 1946 (75+ years)
- **Headquarters:** Doylestown, PA
- **Coverage:** National (14 states, 30+ locations)
- **Volume:** 1,500+ pools annually
- **Types Offered:** Fiberglass, vinyl, concrete
- **BBB Rating:** A+ (but 173+ complaints in 3 years)
- **Google Reviews:** 3.5 stars average
- **Yelp:** 2.3 stars

### Their Strengths
| Strength | How to Acknowledge |
|----------|-------------------|
| 75+ year heritage | "They absolutely have history and brand recognition" |
| National scale | "They have significant resources and reach" |
| Google Guaranteed | "They've invested in the certification process" |
| Multiple pool types | "They offer variety if you want all options under one roof" |
| Large showrooms | "Their showrooms can help visualize features" |

### Their Vulnerabilities
| Issue | Evidence | What Customers Say |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Warranty disputes | Frequent BBB complaints | "Denied our claim and blamed us" |
| Communication breakdown | Review pattern | "Designer disappeared for weeks" |
| Post-sale neglect | Consistent theme | "Once they have your money..." |
| Material substitution | Customer reports | "Not what we saw in the showroom" |
| High-pressure sales | Review mentions | "Felt rushed to sign" |
| Project manager turnover | Industry knowledge | "Different person every call" |

### Questions for Customers to Ask Them
1. "Who will be my single point of contact throughout the project?"
2. "Can I reach a decision-maker directly, or do I go through customer service?"
3. "Will the exact materials I see in my contract match the showroom samples?"
4. "What is your warranty claim process, and what typically gets denied?"
5. "How many projects will my crew be working on simultaneously?"

### Our Differentiators vs. Anthony Sylvan
| They Offer | We Offer |
|------------|----------|
| Customer service department | Owner's direct phone number |
| Project manager (may change) | Same person, start to finish |
| National warranty process | Personal warranty response |
| Volume-driven scheduling | Boutique attention to each project |
| Generic showroom experience | Custom on-site consultation |
| Corporate communication protocols | 24-hour response guarantee |

### Winning Talk Track
> "Anthony Sylvan has been around a long time - there's no arguing with that. What I'd encourage you to do is look at recent customer experiences, not just the company history. Read reviews from the last 1-2 years, especially around communication, warranty service, and follow-through. Then talk to our recent customers and compare. The difference in experience will speak for itself."

## 6. DIY/Kit Pool Companies

### Examples
- Pool Warehouse
- In The Swim
- Doughboy/Intex (above ground)
- Latham Pool Products (through contractor networks)

### Their Appeal
| Appeal | Reality Check |
|--------|--------------|
| Lower upfront cost | Hidden costs add up significantly |
| "You can do it yourself" | Most homeowners can't, actually |
| Control over process | Means responsibility for mistakes |
| Perceived savings | Doesn't account for time, tools, permits |

### Their Vulnerabilities
| Issue | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Permit complexity | Most require licensed installer anyway |
| Equipment/tool needs | Thousands in rentals/purchases |
| Learning curve | Costly mistakes are common |
| No warranty coverage | Manufacturer void if self-installed |
| Financing not available | Banks want professional installation |
| Inspection failures | Common without professional knowledge |
| Time investment | 200+ hours for homeowner |
| Safety liability | Improper installation risks |

### Questions for DIY Customers to Consider
1. "Who will pull the permits, and are you comfortable with inspections?"
2. "What happens if something goes wrong - who's responsible?"
3. "How will you handle excavation, electrical, plumbing, and gas?"
4. "What's your time worth over the 200+ hours this will take?"
5. "Will your homeowner's insurance cover a self-installed pool?"

### Our Position vs. DIY
> "I understand the appeal of saving money - that's smart thinking. Here's what I've seen: most DIY projects either don't get completed, cost more than expected, or result in problems down the road. The warranty is voided, the insurance situation is complicated, and the time investment is massive. For most families, professional installation actually delivers better value when you account for everything. Let me show you what a properly installed pool costs versus what DIY actually costs when you add everything up."

## 7. General Contractors Adding Pool Services

### The Scenario
Homeowner is doing a major renovation; GC offers to "add a pool" to the project.

### Their Appeal
| Appeal | Reality Check |
|--------|--------------|
| One project manager | Jack of all trades, master of none |
| Bundled pricing | Margins often built in |
| Convenience | Pool expertise typically lacking |
| Existing relationship | Pool is afterthought, not specialty |

### Their Vulnerabilities
| Issue | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Pool as add-on, not core competency | Subcontracted to lowest bidder |
| Limited pool knowledge | Can't advise on design, features |
| No manufacturer relationships | Generic product sourcing |
| Warranty gaps | Who services pool-specific issues? |
| No ongoing relationship | GC moves to next project |
| Limited portfolio | Few pool projects to reference |

### Questions to Ask General Contractors
1. "How many pools have you personally overseen?"
2. "Who specifically will build the pool - your team or a subcontractor?"
3. "What's your warranty on pool-specific issues?"
4. "Who do I call in 3 years if I have a pool problem?"
5. "Can you show me pools you've completed in the last year?"

### Our Position vs. GC Pool Add-Ons
> "I get it - it seems convenient to bundle everything with your renovation. Here's the risk: pool building is highly specialized. A GC who's great at kitchens and additions isn't necessarily great at pools. They typically subcontract it out, and you lose control over who's actually doing the work. After the project, who do you call for pool-specific issues? We're pool specialists. This is all we do. You get dedicated expertise, manufacturer relationships, and a partner for the life of your pool."

## Quick Objection Responses by Competitor

### "Anthony Sylvan has been around 75 years"
> "They have, and that heritage means something. My question for you is: what's your experience going to be like today? Read recent reviews, not company history. Then compare with our references."

### "Blue Haven is the biggest fiberglass builder"
> "Volume doesn't equal attention. They have scale. We have focus. You'll be one of thousands of their projects. You'll be one of ours. The experience difference matters."

### "Ted's does luxury pools"
> "They do beautiful concrete work. The question is: do you want a 6-month project with higher lifetime maintenance, or a 3-week fiberglass install with lower costs for decades?"

### "This local guy is way cheaper"
> "Price matters, but so does longevity. Will they be here in 10 years? Are they properly licensed and insured? Cheaper today can mean expensive problems tomorrow."

### "I can get a kit pool online for half the cost"
> "The kit is half the cost. The installation isn't included. Neither is the excavation, the permits, the electrical, the plumbing, or the warranty. When you add it all up, professional installation often costs the same - with far less risk."
