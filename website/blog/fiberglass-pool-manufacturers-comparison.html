Fiberglass Pool Manufacturers: Latham vs Thursday Pools vs Others

An honest comparison of the major fiberglass pool manufacturers. Quality, warranties, design options, and which brands deliver the best value for Pennsylvania homeowners.

When shopping for a fiberglass pool, you'll encounter a confusing landscape of manufacturer names, dealer claims, and warranty promises. Everyone says they're "the best." So how do you actually compare them?

As pool installers who work with multiple manufacturers, we have an insider view that most homeowners don't get. We see how pools hold up over time, which warranty claims get honored, and which designs actually work for Pennsylvania properties.

This guide cuts through the marketing to give you an honest assessment of the major fiberglass pool manufacturers available in the Mid-Atlantic region.

The Fiberglass Pool Market Landscape

The fiberglass pool industry has consolidated significantly over the past decade. Several smaller manufacturers have been acquired by larger players, while a few independents maintain strong positions.

The Big Picture

  • Latham Pool Products: The industry giant, owning multiple brands including Blue Hawaiian, Trilogy, and Narellan
  • Thursday Pools: Innovative independent known for unique designs
  • Leisure Pools: Australian-based manufacturer with strong US presence
  • San Juan Pools: Oldest fiberglass manufacturer in North America
  • Viking Pools: Major player with extensive dealer network

Each manufacturer has genuine strengths and real limitations. Let's examine them honestly.

Latham is the 800-pound gorilla of fiberglass pools. They've grown through acquisitions, now owning brands including Blue Hawaiian, Trilogy, Narellan, and others. This gives them manufacturing facilities across North America and the largest selection of models.

What Latham Does Well

  • Manufacturing consistency: Their scale allows for quality control systems smaller manufacturers can't match
  • Model selection: Over 100 pool shapes and sizes across their brands
  • Warranty coverage: Lifetime structural, up to 15 years on surface
  • Financial stability: Publicly traded company with resources for long-term support
  • Innovation: Led industry in color development and tanning ledge integration

Latham Limitations

  • Premium pricing: Typically 10-15% higher than competitors for similar-sized pools
  • Brand confusion: Multiple brand names can complicate comparison shopping
  • Corporate feel: Large company processes can feel impersonal

Popular Latham Models for PA Homeowners

  • Mediterranean (16' x 35'): Classic rectangle with full-length bench, ideal for traditional Main Line properties
  • Olympia (14' x 32'): Freeform design with tanning ledge, popular for family use
  • Gemini (12' x 25'): Compact geometric for smaller Chester County lots

Our Assessment

Latham represents a safe, premium choice. You're paying more, but you're getting consistent quality, strong warranty backing, and a company that will be around in 20 years. Best for homeowners who prioritize peace of mind over price.

Thursday Pools is the most innovative manufacturer in the fiberglass space. They focus on designs that genuinely work for how families use pools today, not just scaled versions of old shapes.

What Thursday Pools Does Well

  • Innovative design: Features like full-width tanning ledges, integrated spillover spas, and sport-specific pools
  • Color technology: Proprietary gel coat formulations with excellent UV resistance
  • Beach entries: Best-in-class gradual entry designs
  • Quality construction: Hand-laid fiberglass, not sprayed
  • Responsive company: Smaller size means more flexibility and better communication

Thursday Pools Limitations

  • Smaller selection: About 30 models compared to Latham's 100+
  • Single manufacturing facility: Longer lead times during peak season
  • Limited dealer network: Fewer authorized installers

Standout Thursday Pools Models

  • Wellspring (16' x 35'): Stunning beach entry design, perfect for families with young children
  • Imagine (14' x 31'): Classic rectangle with full-length tanning ledge
  • Grace (10' x 20'): Plunge pool with spa-like proportions, ideal for small yards
  • Spirit (16' x 36'): Sport pool designed for lap swimming and water exercise

Best For

Thursday Pools is our top recommendation for homeowners who want something distinctive. Their beach entry and sport pool designs genuinely outperform competitors. If design innovation matters more than having 100 options, Thursday is often the right choice.

How to Choose the Right Manufacturer

Step 1: Define Your Priorities

Ask yourself what matters most:

  • Design uniqueness: Thursday Pools leads here
  • Model selection: Latham offers the most choices
  • Value: Leisure and San Juan balance quality and price
  • Budget: Viking delivers basic pools at lower cost
  • Warranty confidence: Latham's financial stability provides security

Step 2: Consider Your Installer

This matters more than most homeowners realize. A great installer working with a good pool will outperform a mediocre installer with a premium pool. Ask your installer:

  • Which manufacturers do you work with most?
  • How many pools have you installed from each?
  • Have you had warranty issues with any manufacturer?
  • Which manufacturers do you recommend and why?

Step 3: See Pools in Person

Photos don't tell the whole story. Visit completed pools when possible:

  • Check surface quality and color consistency
  • Look at how steps and ledges actually function
  • Ask owners about their experience with the manufacturer
  • Compare 2-3 year old pools to new ones (does the surface hold up?)

Step 4: Read the Warranty Carefully

Not all lifetime warranties are equal. Check:

  • What's actually covered vs. excluded?
  • Is the warranty transferable if you sell?
  • What's the claims process?
  • Are there maintenance requirements that could void coverage?

The Installation Matters Most

Even the best pool shell can fail if improperly installed. Backfill material, leveling, plumbing connections, and proper drainage all determine long-term success. Choose a great installer first, then work with them on manufacturer selection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which fiberglass pool manufacturer has the best warranty?

Thursday Pools and Latham both offer lifetime structural warranties, which are the strongest in the industry. For surface warranties, Latham leads with up to 15 years on gelcoat fading, while Thursday Pools offers 10 years. The key difference is transferability - check if the warranty transfers to new owners if you sell your home.

Is Latham Pools the same as other pool brands?

Latham is actually a parent company that owns multiple pool brands including Blue Hawaiian, Cardinal Systems, and others. They manufacture pools under various names but use consistent manufacturing standards. When comparing, focus on the specific model and features rather than brand name alone.

What makes Thursday Pools different from other manufacturers?

Thursday Pools specializes in innovative designs with distinctive features like integrated tanning ledges, unique color options, and sport-oriented shapes. Their manufacturing process uses hand-laid fiberglass and a proprietary gel coat. They're particularly known for their Beach Entry pools and the Graceline series.

Are cheaper fiberglass pool brands worth considering?

Entry-level manufacturers can save $5,000-$10,000 on the shell, but you may sacrifice warranty coverage, color selection, and build quality. The pool shell is the one part of your pool you can never upgrade later. For a 25+ year investment, most homeowners find premium manufacturers worth the extra cost.

How do I choose between similar pool models from different manufacturers?

Compare: 1) Warranty terms and what's actually covered, 2) Gelcoat thickness and quality, 3) Color options and fade resistance, 4) Specific features like tanning ledge size or step design, 5) Manufacturer reputation and years in business, and 6) Your installer's experience with that manufacturer. Often the installation quality matters more than the manufacturer.

Do fiberglass pool manufacturers sell directly to homeowners?

No. All major fiberglass pool manufacturers sell exclusively through authorized dealer-installers. This ensures proper installation, which is critical for warranty coverage. Be wary of anyone selling factory-direct pools - proper installation requires specialized training and equipment.
