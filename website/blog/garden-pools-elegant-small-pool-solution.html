Garden Pools: The Elegant Small Pool Solution

Where landscape artistry meets refreshing functionality. Garden pools create a refined outdoor retreat that enhances your property year-round, not just during swimming season.

For homeowners who see their backyard as an extension of their living space, a standard pool may feel too utilitarian. A garden pool offers something different: a water feature that is designed from the start to be part of the landscape, not just sitting in it.

Garden pools blend the cooling functionality of a plunge pool with thoughtful landscape design, creating an outdoor space that is beautiful in every season, not just when you are swimming.

What Is a Garden Pool?

A garden pool is a small, design-forward pool that is conceived as an integrated element of your garden and landscape, rather than a standalone recreational feature. The pool becomes the centerpiece of an outdoor composition that includes plantings, natural materials, lighting, and hardscaping.

Think of a garden pool as the intersection of pool design and landscape architecture. The water is just one element of a larger outdoor living environment.

Landscape-First Design

The pool is designed to complement your garden, architecture, and outdoor living goals, not the other way around.

Natural Materials

Natural stone coping, organic shapes, and integrated plantings create a cohesive, refined aesthetic.

Four-Season Beauty

Even when not in use, a garden pool serves as a stunning landscape focal point throughout the year.

Sophisticated Entertaining

Creates an intimate, elegant setting for outdoor dining and gathering rather than a recreational zone.

Garden Pool vs Plunge Pool: What Is the Difference?

Garden pools and plunge pools share similar dimensions, but the design philosophy is different:

Aspect Garden Pool Plunge Pool
Design Focus Landscape integration, visual beauty Functional water feature
Surroundings Natural stone, plantings, garden beds Standard decking or patio
Shape Often organic or naturalistic Typically geometric (rectangular)
Water Features Common (fountains, spillovers, bubblers) Optional
Off-Season Presence Remains a garden centerpiece Covered and dormant
Cost $30,000 - $55,000 $25,000 - $45,000

In practical terms, a garden pool is a plunge pool that has been elevated through design, materials, and landscaping to become an integrated outdoor feature rather than a standalone fixture.

Garden Pool Design Ideas

The Courtyard Retreat

Position a compact rectangular garden pool in the center of a walled or hedged courtyard. Surround it with stone pavers, container plantings, and low garden walls for seating. This creates a private, enclosed retreat that feels like a European garden. Works beautifully in Main Line properties with existing garden rooms or walled areas.

The Natural Oasis

Use an organic, freeform pool shape surrounded by native plantings, natural boulders, and ornamental grasses. A small waterfall or bubbler adds the sound of moving water. This style blends seamlessly with naturalistic landscapes and feels like a discovered woodland pool.

The Modern Reflecting Pool

A sleek, geometric pool with dark interior finish that doubles as a reflecting pool when not in use for swimming. Minimal plantings, clean lines, and contemporary materials like bluestone or porcelain pavers. Ideal for modern and transitional architecture.

The Terrace Garden Pool

On sloped properties, build the garden pool into a terraced level with surrounding garden beds at different elevations. This creates a dramatic, multi-level outdoor space where the pool is framed by cascading plantings and stone retaining walls.

The Spa Garden

Combine a heated garden pool with aromatic plantings (lavender, rosemary, jasmine), subtle lighting, and comfortable seating to create a spa-like wellness garden. The emphasis is on relaxation and sensory experience rather than recreation.

Landscaping Integration

The landscaping around a garden pool is what transforms it from a simple pool into a garden feature. Here are key considerations for our region:

Plants That Work Near Pools

  • Ornamental grasses: Karl Foerster feather reed grass, Blue Fescue, and Switchgrass provide movement and texture without dropping excessive debris
  • Evergreen structure: Boxwood, Japanese Holly, and dwarf conifers provide year-round form and green
  • Perennials: Lavender, Russian Sage, Coneflower, and Black-Eyed Susan add seasonal color with minimal maintenance
  • Groundcovers: Creeping Thyme between pavers or Pachysandra for shade areas

Plants to Avoid Near Pools

  • Large deciduous trees that drop heavy leaf loads
  • Fruit-bearing trees or shrubs
  • Plants with aggressive root systems
  • Plants that attract large numbers of bees (near the pool edge)

Pennsylvania-Specific Tip

For garden pools in our region, choose plants rated for USDA Hardiness Zones 6b-7a. Native Pennsylvania plants offer low-maintenance beauty and support local ecosystems. See our Pool Landscaping Ideas for Pennsylvania for more detailed plant recommendations.

Hardscape Materials

The materials you choose for the pool surround and garden paths set the tone:

  • Natural bluestone: Classic for the Main Line, weathers beautifully, non-slip surface
  • Limestone: Warm, elegant, pairs well with formal garden designs
  • Natural fieldstone: Ideal for rustic or naturalistic garden pool designs
  • Premium pavers: Wide range of colors and patterns, durable, consistent look
  • Pea gravel paths: Budget-friendly, natural appearance, good for garden pathways leading to the pool

Year-Round Appeal

One of the strongest arguments for a garden pool over a standard plunge pool is its four-season presence. A well-designed garden pool remains a beautiful feature even when the water is covered for winter.

Spring

Early bulbs and flowering shrubs bring the garden to life around the pool. Open the pool for use as the weather warms, typically in April or May with a heater.

Summer

Peak season. The pool is the cooling centerpiece of your outdoor entertaining space. Surrounding plantings are in full bloom.

Fall

Ornamental grasses reach their peak. Fall foliage creates a stunning backdrop. Heated garden pools extend the season well into October or November.

Winter

Evergreen plantings, structural garden elements, and the pool's architectural form continue to provide visual interest. Outdoor lighting transforms the garden into a winter scene. Some homeowners with premium heating systems use their garden pools year-round for cold-plunge wellness routines.

Sizing for Garden Pools

Garden pools follow similar sizing to plunge pools, with the landscape component requiring additional space:

Garden Pool Size Pool Dimensions Total Garden Area Needed
Intimate 10' x 6' ~400 sq ft (for pool + surrounds + plantings)
Standard 12' x 7' ~500-600 sq ft
Generous 14' x 8' ~600-800 sq ft

The garden area surrounding the pool is as important as the pool itself. Budget at least 3-5 feet of plantable space on at least two sides of the pool for meaningful landscape integration.

Garden Pool Costs

Garden pools cost somewhat more than a basic plunge pool because of the enhanced landscape design and premium materials:

Component Cost Range
Pool Shell & Installation $25,000 - $40,000
Natural Stone Coping & Patio $5,000 - $12,000
Landscape Design & Plantings $3,000 - $10,000
Water Features (optional) $1,500 - $5,000
Landscape Lighting $1,500 - $4,000
Total Garden Pool Project $30,000 - $55,000

The additional investment in landscape design, materials, and plantings typically adds $5,000-$15,000 compared to a basic plunge pool. However, the result is a cohesive outdoor feature that adds significant curb appeal and property value.

Garden Pools Are Perfect For:

  • Homeowners who value design and aesthetics as much as functionality
  • Properties where the outdoor space is visible from main living areas
  • Those who entertain outdoors and want an elegant focal point
  • Garden enthusiasts who want their pool to enhance, not replace, their landscape
  • Main Line and Delaware properties with established garden character

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a garden pool?

A garden pool is a small, thoughtfully designed pool that is integrated into the surrounding landscape as a central garden feature. Unlike standard pools, garden pools are designed to complement and enhance the garden environment with natural materials, plantings, and a cohesive aesthetic.

How is a garden pool different from a plunge pool?

While similar in size, garden pools emphasize landscape integration and visual design. Plunge pools focus primarily on the water feature itself. Garden pools incorporate natural stone, surrounding plantings, water features, and organic shapes to create a cohesive outdoor living space.

How much does a garden pool cost?

Garden pools typically cost $30,000-$55,000 including the pool, landscaping integration, natural stone or premium coping, and plantings. The landscape design component adds $5,000-$15,000 beyond a basic plunge pool installation.

Can a garden pool be used year-round in Pennsylvania?

With a heater, garden pools can be used from April through October, and some homeowners use them year-round. Even in winter when the pool is closed, a well-designed garden pool remains a beautiful landscape focal point with evergreen plantings and structural garden elements.
