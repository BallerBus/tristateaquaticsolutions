How Long Does Fiberglass Pool Installation Take?

One of the biggest advantages of fiberglass pools is the fast installation timeline. Here's a detailed breakdown of what to expect, what affects your schedule, and how to plan for a smooth installation in the Main Line area.

If you're considering a fiberglass pool for your Main Line home, you've probably heard that installation is much faster than concrete. But exactly how long will your backyard be a construction zone? And when do you need to start planning to be swimming by summer?

This guide breaks down the fiberglass pool installation timeline in detail, from the moment you sign a contract to the moment you take your first swim.

The Quick Answer

2-4 Weeks

Typical fiberglass pool installation from excavation to swim-ready

Once your fiberglass pool shell arrives on site, most installations are complete within 2-4 weeks. However, the total timeline from signing a contract to swimming includes additional phases:

PhaseTimeline
Contract to permit approval2-6 weeks
Pool manufacturing & delivery4-8 weeks
Excavation to completion2-4 weeks
Total timeline8-16 weeks

The good news? The 2-4 week installation phase is when your backyard is actively disrupted. The pre-installation phases happen mostly in the background while you go about your normal life.

Day-by-Day Installation Timeline

Here's what a typical fiberglass pool installation looks like in the Main Line area:

Day 1

Layout and Excavation Begin

The crew marks the exact pool location and begins excavation. For a standard pool, excavation typically takes 1-2 days depending on soil conditions and access.

  • Pool location staked and confirmed
  • Excavator arrives and digs begin
  • Soil hauled away or stockpiled
Days 2-3

Excavation Complete & Base Prep

Excavation is finalized and the hole is prepared to receive the pool. A gravel or sand base is installed for proper drainage and leveling.

  • Hole graded to exact specifications
  • Gravel base installed and compacted
  • Plumbing rough-in begins
Day 4

Pool Delivery and Set

The most exciting day! Your fiberglass shell arrives via crane truck and is carefully lowered into the excavated hole. This process takes just a few hours.

  • Crane positions pool shell
  • Shell leveled precisely
  • Initial positioning confirmed
Days 5-6

Backfill and Plumbing

The pool is backfilled with gravel while simultaneously filling with water to balance pressure. Plumbing connections are completed.

  • Backfill installed in lifts
  • Water added simultaneously
  • Plumbing lines connected
Days 7-8

Electrical and Equipment

Electrical connections are made and pool equipment (pump, filter, heater, controls) is installed and connected.

  • Equipment pad poured or set
  • Pump, filter, heater installed
  • Electrical connections completed
Days 9-12

Decking and Coping

Coping is installed around the pool edge, and concrete, paver, or other decking is constructed. This is often the longest phase.

  • Coping stones or cantilever forms installed
  • Deck poured or pavers laid
  • Cleanup of work area
Days 13-14

Final Details and Startup

Final inspections, water balancing, and owner orientation. You're ready to swim!

  • Final township inspection
  • Water chemistry balanced
  • Owner training on equipment
  • First swim!

The Pre-Installation Phase

Before excavation begins, several things need to happen. Understanding this timeline helps you plan for summer swimming.

Step 1: Contract and Design (1-2 weeks)

After your initial consultation, you'll finalize pool selection, features, and placement. Once you sign the contract and submit your deposit, the wheels start turning.

Step 2: Permits (2-6 weeks)

Step 3: Pool Manufacturing and Delivery (4-8 weeks)

Your fiberglass pool shell is manufactured at the factory and transported to the area. Lead times vary by season and manufacturer, with spring being the busiest time.

Pro Tip: Order Early

To be swimming by Memorial Day, sign your contract by mid-February at the latest. This accounts for manufacturing lead times and ensures you're in the spring installation queue before it fills up.

What Affects Your Installation Timeline?

Several factors can speed up or slow down your installation:

Weather

Rain is the biggest delay factor. Excavation and concrete work can't proceed in heavy rain, and wet conditions can delay the schedule by days or even weeks during particularly wet springs.

Site Conditions

  • Rock: Subsurface rock can add 1-3 days to excavation
  • High water table: May require dewatering, adding 1-2 days
  • Access limitations: Narrow access or steep slopes can slow delivery
  • Tree roots: Large roots may need to be addressed

Project Complexity

  • Standard pool: 2-3 weeks
  • Pool with spa: 3-4 weeks
  • Pool with extensive decking: 3-4 weeks
  • Pool with outdoor kitchen/features: 4-6 weeks

Season

Peak season (March-June) means crews are busy and scheduling is tighter. Fall installations often proceed faster due to better availability, though weather becomes a factor.

Fiberglass vs Concrete: Timeline Comparison

One of the biggest advantages of fiberglass is the dramatically shorter installation time:

PhaseFiberglassConcrete
Excavation1-2 days2-3 days
Pool structure1 day (set)3-4 weeks (pour + cure)
Tile/FinishN/A (built-in)2-3 weeks
Plumbing/Electric2-3 days1-2 weeks
Decking3-5 days5-10 days
Fill/Startup1-2 days1-2 days
Total2-4 weeks3-5 months

Why the Massive Difference?

The concrete pool curing period alone takes 28 days. Add in the time to build the rebar structure, spray gunite/shotcrete, apply the interior finish, and install tile - it's simply a much more time-intensive process than setting a pre-manufactured fiberglass shell.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does fiberglass pool installation take from start to finish?

A typical fiberglass pool installation takes 2-4 weeks from excavation to swim-ready. The actual construction work takes about 2 weeks, with additional time for permits (1-2 weeks before) and final inspections. Weather delays can extend this timeline.

Why is fiberglass faster to install than concrete?

Fiberglass pools arrive as pre-manufactured shells that simply need to be set in place, while concrete pools must be built from scratch on-site. Concrete requires a 28-day curing period alone, plus weeks of tile work and finishing. Fiberglass skips all of this.

What is the fastest a fiberglass pool can be installed?

Under ideal conditions with expedited permits and simple site conditions, some fiberglass pools can be swim-ready in as little as 10-14 days. However, 3-4 weeks is a more realistic expectation that accounts for typical variables.

What delays fiberglass pool installation the most?

The most common delays are weather (especially rain), permit processing times, and unexpected site conditions like rock or high water tables. Scheduling conflicts during peak season and utility coordination can also cause delays.

When should I order a pool to be swimming by Memorial Day?

To swim by Memorial Day, sign your contract by early February and aim for April installation. This accounts for manufacturer lead times (4-8 weeks), permit processing (2-4 weeks), and a buffer for weather delays during Pennsylvania's unpredictable spring.
