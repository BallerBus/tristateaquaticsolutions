How to Choose the Right Pool Size for Your Family

Finding the perfect pool size balances family needs, yard space, budget, and future use. Here's how to make the right decision for your Main Line or Chester County home.

Pool size affects everything from installation cost to ongoing maintenance expenses to daily enjoyment. Too small and you'll wish you'd gone bigger. Too large and you're heating water nobody swims in. Let's find the sweet spot for your family.

Common Pool Sizes Explained

Plunge Pools (10-14 feet)

Size: 150-250 sq ft | Depth: 4-5 feet typical

Perfect for cooling off, relaxation, and small gatherings. Ideal for smaller yards or as a secondary feature. Not suitable for swimming laps but great for exercise when combined with jets.

Best for: Couples, small families, limited space, budget-conscious buyers.

Small Pools (14-24 feet)

Size: 250-400 sq ft | Depth: 3-6 feet

Good for families who want swimming capability without dominating the yard. Accommodates casual swimming, play, and small gatherings. Many fiberglass options in this range include built-in seating and tanning ledges.

Best for: Families of 2-4, moderate yards, those prioritizing yard preservation.

Medium Pools (24-34 feet)

Size: 400-550 sq ft | Depth: 3.5-8 feet

The most popular size range. Accommodates multiple swimmers, games, and entertaining. Usually includes varied depth zones - shallow end for play, deeper area for diving/swimming. Good balance of capacity and manageability.

Best for: Families of 3-6, regular entertaining, versatile use.

Large Pools (35+ feet)

Size: 550-800+ sq ft | Depth: 3-8+ feet

Full-size pools for larger families, serious swimmers, or frequent large gatherings. Can accommodate lap swimming, diving, and parties. Requires larger yards and higher maintenance budgets.

Best for: Large families, frequent entertainers, exercise swimming, estate properties.

Key Factors in Pool Size Selection

1. How Will You Use the Pool?

  • Relaxation only: Plunge pool or small pool sufficient
  • Family play: Medium pool with shallow area
  • Exercise/laps: Minimum 35-40 feet length
  • Diving: Minimum 8 feet deep in designated area
  • Entertaining: Larger pool with tanning ledge
  • Kids' activities: Graduated depths, play features

2. Yard Size and Setbacks

Township setback requirements limit where pools can be placed:

  • Property line setbacks: Typically 10-15 feet
  • House setbacks: Usually 10+ feet
  • Easement restrictions: Must stay clear
  • Deck space: Plan for adequate patio area

Rule of Thumb

Your usable pool area (pool + surrounding deck) should be no more than 50-60% of your available backyard space after setbacks. This leaves room for lawn, landscaping, and other features.

3. Budget Considerations

Pool costs scale with size, but not linearly:

  • Installation: Roughly $200-$300 per square foot for quality fiberglass
  • Heating: Larger pools cost 30-50% more to heat
  • Chemicals: Scale roughly with volume
  • Electricity: Larger pumps use more power

Frequently Asked Questions

What size pool do I need for a family of 4?

For a family of 4, a pool of 400-500 square feet (approximately 30x15 feet) provides comfortable swimming space. This allows multiple people to swim, play, and lounge comfortably. Smaller families may be happy with 300-400 sq ft; larger families or frequent entertainers should consider 500+ sq ft.

How big should my backyard be for a pool?

As a general rule, you need a backyard at least 1.5-2 times the pool size to accommodate decking, setbacks, and equipment. A 400 sq ft pool typically needs a backyard of at least 1,200-1,500 sq ft usable area after accounting for setback requirements.

What is a good pool size for exercise?

For lap swimming, you need at least 35-40 feet in length. Standard lap pools are 40-50 feet long and 8-10 feet wide. If space is limited, a swim-in-place system with current jets can provide exercise swimming in a much smaller pool (12-15 feet).

Are larger pools more expensive to maintain?

Yes, larger pools cost more to heat, require more chemicals, and use more electricity for filtration. A pool that's twice as large typically costs 30-50% more to maintain annually. However, the cost per square foot decreases with size, so bigger pools offer better value per unit area.

What pool size is best for small backyards?

For small backyards, consider plunge pools (10-14 feet) or cocktail pools (12-16 feet) which provide refreshment and relaxation in compact spaces. These pools are 200-300 sq ft and can fit in backyards as small as 600-800 sq ft usable area.
