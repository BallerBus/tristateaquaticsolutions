Lap Pools for Main Line Homes: Design and Cost Guide

Everything you need to know about building a lap pool in Chester, Delaware, or Montgomery County. Dimensions, costs, design options, and how to fit a lap pool on your property.

Lap pools represent the intersection of fitness and luxury - a dedicated swimming space for serious exercise that also adds stunning aesthetics to Main Line properties. Whether you're a competitive swimmer, triathlete, or someone who simply prefers swimming to running, a lap pool transforms your backyard into a personal training facility.

This guide covers everything you need to know about building a lap pool in the Philadelphia suburbs, from ideal dimensions to realistic costs to creative solutions for challenging properties.

Why Choose a Lap Pool?

Health and Fitness Benefits

  • Low-impact exercise: Swimming is easier on joints than running or cycling
  • Full-body workout: Engages every major muscle group
  • Cardiovascular health: Excellent for heart health without impact stress
  • Consistent access: No driving to the gym or dealing with crowded lanes
  • Year-round swimming: With heating, swim 7-9 months per year in PA

Property Benefits

  • Narrow profile: Fits properties where traditional pools won't
  • Clean aesthetics: Sleek, modern appearance
  • Multi-use: Many designs incorporate lounging areas
  • Property value: Appeals to fitness-focused buyers

The Swimmer's Math

A typical gym membership costs $50-$100/month, plus travel time and scheduling hassles. Over 20 years, that's $12,000-$24,000 just in fees - not counting the time value. A lap pool pays for itself while adding property value and eliminating excuses.

Sizing and Dimensions

Length Considerations

Pool length determines your swimming experience more than any other dimension.

Short Course (25 Yards)

Length: 75 feet Standard: Competition Turns per 500: 20 Best for: Serious swimmers

Residential Long (50+ feet)

Length: 50-60 feet Standard: Excellent home Turns per 500: 25-30 Best for: Regular exercise

Residential Standard (40+ feet)

Length: 40-50 feet Standard: Good home pool Turns per 500: 30-38 Best for: Fitness swimming

Compact with Jets

Length: 20-35 feet Standard: Swim-in-place Turns per 500: None (endless) Best for: Limited space

Width Options

  • Single lane (8 feet): Minimum for comfortable swimming, one person at a time
  • Wide single (10 feet): More comfortable stroke width, slight passing room
  • Double lane (12-14 feet): Two swimmers side by side, or one with plenty of room
  • Multi-use (16+ feet): Lap swimming plus lounging/play areas

Depth

  • Ideal lap depth: 4-4.5 feet consistent throughout
  • Flip turn minimum: 3.5 feet (4+ preferred)
  • Maximum useful: 5 feet (deeper provides no benefit for laps)

Our Recommendation

For most Main Line homeowners who want serious exercise capability, aim for at least 40 feet long and 10 feet wide with 4 feet of consistent depth. This provides a genuinely useful swimming experience without requiring an oversized property.

Lap Pool Types

Fiberglass Lap Pools

Several manufacturers offer dedicated lap pool designs.

  • Advantages: Fast installation, smooth surface, low maintenance
  • Limitations: Maximum length around 40-45 feet due to transport
  • Cost: $75,000-$95,000 installed (40' model)
  • Best for: Homeowners wanting quick installation and easy maintenance

Concrete Lap Pools

Custom-built on-site to exact specifications.

  • Advantages: Any length, any width, any shape, ultimate customization
  • Limitations: Longer installation, higher maintenance
  • Cost: $100,000-$175,000 depending on size
  • Best for: Longer pools (50+ feet), specific design requirements

Above-Ground Lap Pools

Prefabricated units that sit on your property.

  • Advantages: Lower cost, removable, no excavation
  • Limitations: Less attractive, limited size, not ideal for serious swimming
  • Cost: $15,000-$40,000
  • Best for: Budget-conscious or temporary installations

Heating for Lap Pools

If you're building a lap pool for exercise, heating is not optional - it's essential. Swimming in cold water is neither comfortable nor conducive to a good workout.

Heating Options

  • Heat pump: Most efficient for regular use, $3,500-$5,000, ideal for lap pools
  • Gas heater: Fast heating, $2,500-$4,000, higher operating costs
  • Solar: $4,000-$8,000, only effective in warmer months

Pool Covers

A pool cover dramatically reduces heating costs and is especially valuable for lap pools:

  • Solar cover: $200-$500, basic heat retention
  • Safety cover: $3,000-$6,000, manual operation
  • Automatic cover: $12,000-$20,000, one-button operation (ideal for daily swimmers)

The Automatic Cover Investment

For serious lap swimmers, an automatic cover is worth every penny. You'll use your pool more when opening it takes 30 seconds instead of 10 minutes. The cover also cuts heating costs by 50-70% and keeps debris out. Over 20 years of daily use, the convenience and savings far exceed the upfront cost.

Compact Alternatives

If your property can't accommodate a traditional lap pool, consider these alternatives.

Swim Current Systems

Powerful jets create a current you swim against, turning even a short pool into an endless swimming experience.

  • Retrofit systems: $3,000-$8,000, add to existing pools
  • Built-in systems: $5,000-$12,000, integrated during construction
  • Brands: Endless Pools, Badu, Fastlane

Swim Spas

Self-contained units combining swim current with spa features.

  • Size: Typically 12-20 feet long
  • Cost: $20,000-$50,000 installed
  • Advantages: Compact, portable, year-round use, spa features
  • Limitations: Different swimming feel, smaller exercise space

Plunge Pool with Jets

Small pool (10' x 20') with swim jet system.

  • Cost: $50,000-$75,000
  • Best for: Smaller properties, multi-use (relaxation + exercise)

Design Considerations for Main Line Properties

Fitting a Lap Pool on Your Lot

Main Line properties range from compact village lots to multi-acre estates. Lap pools' narrow profile makes them surprisingly adaptable.

Side Yard Installation

Many Main Line homes have unused side yards 15-20 feet wide. A 10-foot-wide lap pool with 2.5-foot setbacks on each side fits perfectly while leaving the main backyard for other uses.

Property Line Orientation

Running a lap pool parallel to your longest property line often maximizes pool length while meeting setback requirements.

Integration with Landscape

The clean lines of lap pools complement modern and traditional Main Line architecture. Natural stone or bluestone decking connects the pool to the property's established aesthetic.

Privacy Considerations

Lap swimmers typically wear minimal clothing and may swim at early hours. Consider:

  • Screening: Hedge, fence, or trellis for privacy
  • Orientation: Position to minimize visibility from neighbors
  • Pool house: Structure for changing and storage

Aesthetic Integration

  • Vanishing edge: Dramatic effect especially on sloped lots
  • Integrated spa: Add relaxation to your exercise pool
  • Sun shelf: One end can include a tanning ledge without shortening swim lanes
  • Water features: Subtle features can mask street noise during swims

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a lap pool be?

A minimum length of 40 feet is recommended for serious lap swimming. Olympic pools are 50 meters (164 feet), but residential lap pools are typically 40-75 feet. Shorter pools work with swim jets or current systems that allow swimming in place. Width should be at least 8 feet for single-lane swimming.

How much does a lap pool cost in Pennsylvania?

Lap pools in Pennsylvania typically cost $75,000-$150,000 installed. A basic 40-foot fiberglass lap pool runs $75,000-$95,000. Custom concrete lap pools range from $100,000-$150,000+. Costs increase with length, width, and features like heating systems for year-round use.

Can I fit a lap pool on a narrow lot?

Yes. Lap pools are ideal for narrow lots because of their slim profile. A lap pool as narrow as 8 feet wide can fit where traditional pools cannot. The long, narrow shape often works perfectly along property lines or in side yards where wider pools wouldn't fit.

What depth is best for lap swimming?

For lap swimming, a consistent depth of 4-4.5 feet throughout is ideal. This depth allows proper swimming strokes without hitting bottom on flip turns, while being shallow enough to touch bottom if needed. Deeper water isn't necessary unless you plan competitive diving.

Should I heat my lap pool?

If you plan to swim regularly for exercise, heating is highly recommended. An unheated pool in Pennsylvania is only comfortable for 3-4 months. With heating, you can extend the season to 7-9 months (April-November). Heat pumps are most efficient for lap pools since they run frequently to maintain consistent temperature.

What is a swim-in-place pool or swim spa?

Swim-in-place systems use powerful jets to create a current you swim against, allowing endless swimming in a small pool. Swim spas combine this with spa features in a compact unit (typically 12-20 feet long). They cost $20,000-$50,000 and fit where traditional lap pools cannot, though the swimming experience differs from pool swimming.
