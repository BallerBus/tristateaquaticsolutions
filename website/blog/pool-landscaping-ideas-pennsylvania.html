Pool Landscaping Ideas for Pennsylvania Properties

The landscaping around your pool transforms a backyard feature into a complete outdoor living environment. Explore plants, hardscaping, and design strategies perfect for Main Line homes and Pennsylvania's climate.

A pool is just the beginning. The landscaping around your pool creates the atmosphere, provides privacy, and determines how your outdoor space functions for your family. The right landscaping transforms a backyard pool into a private resort.

For Pennsylvania homeowners, landscaping choices must account for our four distinct seasons, freeze-thaw cycles, and the practical realities of pool ownership. This guide helps you create a beautiful, functional poolscape that thrives in our climate.

Planning Your Pool Landscape

Key Considerations

  • Sun and shade: Consider how shade patterns change throughout the day and seasons
  • Wind protection: Strategic planting can block prevailing winds
  • Views: What do you want to see from the pool? What do you want to hide?
  • Privacy: Sight lines from neighbors and public areas
  • Maintenance: How much time do you want to spend on upkeep?
  • Debris: Minimize plants that drop leaves, seeds, or flowers into the pool

The Pool-Friendly Zone

Divide your pool area into zones for planning purposes:

  • Immediate zone (0-4 feet): Keep clear of plants - deck and safety area
  • Near zone (4-10 feet): Low-growing, low-debris plants only
  • Mid zone (10-20 feet): Medium shrubs and ornamental grasses
  • Far zone (20+ feet): Trees and larger specimens

Pool-Friendly Plants for Pennsylvania

The best poolside plants share these characteristics: minimal debris, no aggressive root systems, salt/chlorine tolerance, and suitability for Pennsylvania's Zone 6b-7a climate.

Recommended Plants

Blue Fescue

Compact ornamental grass with striking blue-gray color. Stays neat, no reseeding, drought tolerant once established.

Full Sun Low Water 1-2' tall

Fountain Grass

Graceful arching plumes, movement in breeze. Annual in PA but worth replanting for dramatic effect.

Full Sun 3-4' tall Annual

Daylilies

Tough, colorful, and low maintenance. Hundreds of varieties, bloom all summer. Deer resistant.

Sun/Part Shade Perennial Many Colors

Boxwood

Classic evergreen for structure and formality. Dense growth, neat appearance, winter interest.

Sun/Shade Evergreen Low Debris

Yucca

Dramatic architectural plant, extremely drought tolerant. Adds tropical feel, deer proof.

Full Sun Low Water Evergreen

Arborvitae

Excellent for privacy hedges. Dense evergreen foliage, many sizes available, fast growing.

Full Sun Evergreen Privacy

More Great Choices

  • Knock Out Roses: Disease resistant, continuous bloom, minimal maintenance
  • Catmint: Lavender-blue flowers, deer resistant, long bloom time
  • Juniper: Low-growing evergreen groundcover, many varieties
  • Black-Eyed Susan: Native, tough, yellow summer blooms
  • Sedums: Succulent texture, drought tolerant, interesting foliage
  • Lavender: Fragrant, drought tolerant, pollinators love it

Plants to Avoid Near Pools

  • Deciduous trees close by: Maples, oaks, sweet gums - too much debris
  • Fruit trees: Dropped fruit attracts insects and stains deck
  • Cottonwoods/Willows: Aggressive roots, messy seeds
  • Pine trees: Needles clog filters, sap damages gelcoat
  • Bamboo: Invasive, nearly impossible to remove
  • Bee-attracting plants too close: Create conflicts with swimmers

Hardscaping Options

Hardscaping includes all non-plant elements: decking, patios, walkways, walls, and structures. For Pennsylvania pools, durability through freeze-thaw cycles is essential.

Cool Deck Tip

Lighter colored deck materials stay cooler in sun. Textured finishes provide better slip resistance when wet. Consider how hot the surface will get when choosing - bare feet and summer sun don't mix well with dark materials.

Additional Hardscape Features

  • Retaining walls: Manage grade changes, create planting beds
  • Seat walls: Built-in seating around fire pits or pool edges
  • Pergolas: Shade structures, support for climbing plants
  • Outdoor kitchen: Grill, prep space, bar seating
  • Fire features: Fire pits, fire bowls, linear fire tables

Creating Privacy

Privacy is a top priority for most Main Line pool owners. Effective screening combines multiple elements for year-round coverage.

Privacy Solutions

Layered Privacy Approach

The most effective privacy combines:

  • Layer 1: Fence or wall as base barrier (often required by code)
  • Layer 2: Evergreen hedge for year-round screening (arborvitae, holly)
  • Layer 3: Ornamental grasses for height and movement
  • Layer 4: Pergola or shade structure for overhead privacy

Privacy Plant Options

  • Green Giant Arborvitae: Fast growing (3-4 ft/year), 40-60 ft tall potential
  • Emerald Green Arborvitae: Slower, narrower, better for tight spaces
  • Holly: Dense, thorny (deters climbing), some varieties evergreen
  • Skip Laurel: Broad-leaved evergreen, tolerates shade
  • Privet: Fast growing hedge, semi-evergreen in PA

Creating Outdoor Living Spaces

Modern pool design integrates the pool into a complete outdoor living environment.

Popular Features

  • Covered pavilion: Protection from sun and rain, extends usable season
  • Outdoor kitchen: Built-in grill, refrigerator, sink, bar seating
  • Fire pit area: Gathering space for cooler evenings
  • Lounge areas: Comfortable seating zones around the pool
  • Dining area: Table and chairs for poolside meals
  • Pool house/cabana: Changing room, bathroom, storage

Think About Traffic Flow

Design pathways that make sense: from house to pool, from pool to bathroom, from outdoor kitchen to seating. Clear circulation paths keep the space functional and prevent trampling through planted areas.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Phase the work: Install pool first, add landscaping over time
  • Start with smaller plants: They'll grow, and cost 1/3 of mature specimens
  • DIY what you can: Planting and mulching are homeowner-friendly
  • Buy at end of season: Nurseries discount plants in fall
  • Focus on impact zones: Invest most in areas you see/use most

Frequently Asked Questions

What plants are best around a pool in Pennsylvania?

The best poolside plants for Pennsylvania are low-debris evergreens like juniper and boxwood, ornamental grasses like blue fescue and fountain grass, and colorful perennials like daylilies and black-eyed susans. Avoid trees that drop leaves, seeds, or sap like maples, oaks, and fruit trees.

How far should landscaping be from a pool?

Keep trees at least 15-20 feet from the pool edge to minimize debris and root intrusion. Shrubs should be at least 4-6 feet from the pool. Ground cover and low plants can be closer, but keep 2-3 feet clear for foot traffic and cleaning access. Consider mature plant size, not just planting size.

What is the best pool deck material for Pennsylvania?

Concrete pavers and natural stone are the most popular and durable options for Pennsylvania pools. Poured concrete is more affordable but can crack in freeze-thaw cycles. Travertine looks beautiful but requires sealing. Wood decking is not recommended near pools due to rot, splinters, and maintenance.

How much does pool landscaping cost?

Basic pool landscaping (plants, mulch, some hardscape) typically costs $5,000-$15,000. Moderate landscaping with privacy screening, larger plant specimens, and professional design runs $15,000-$35,000. Extensive landscaping with outdoor kitchen, fire features, and premium materials can exceed $50,000-$100,000.

How do I create privacy around my pool?

Privacy options include evergreen hedges like arborvitae or holly, fence/trellis with climbing plants, ornamental grass screens, pergolas with fabric panels, or strategic placement of large planters. The best approach combines multiple elements for layered privacy that looks natural and provides year-round screening.
