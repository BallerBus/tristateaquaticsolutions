Pool Water Features: Waterfalls, Fountains, and Bubblers

Transform your backyard pool into a resort-style oasis with stunning water features. Complete guide to waterfalls, fountains, deck jets, and bubblers for Pennsylvania pools.

Water features transform a standard swimming pool into a captivating backyard centerpiece. Beyond aesthetics, they create soothing sounds, help cool the water, and provide endless entertainment for families. For Main Line Pennsylvania homeowners, water features add that resort-quality touch that makes your pool truly special.

Whether you're planning a new pool or upgrading an existing one, understanding your water feature options helps you make informed decisions that match your style, budget, and maintenance preferences.

Types of Pool Water Features

Waterfalls

Cost: $2,000-$25,000+ | Impact: High visual and auditory

Waterfalls range from elegant sheer descent features to dramatic natural rock formations. They create the most immersive resort atmosphere and the soothing sound of falling water masks neighborhood noise beautifully.

Fountains & Deck Jets

Cost: $500-$3,500 each | Impact: Playful and elegant

Deck jets shoot arcing streams of water from the pool deck into the pool. Multiple jets create stunning visual patterns, especially when combined with LED lighting. Perfect for entertaining and family fun.

Bubblers

Cost: $500-$2,000 each | Impact: Playful and interactive

Bubblers (also called gushers) push water up from the pool floor, typically in tanning ledges or shallow areas. Kids love playing in the gentle spray, and they add visual interest without overwhelming sound.

Laminar Jets

Cost: $1,500-$3,500 each | Impact: Dramatic and modern

Laminar jets produce smooth, glass-like streams of water that arc gracefully into the pool. With fiber optic lighting, the stream itself glows in any color - stunning after dark. The epitome of modern pool design.

Waterfalls: Creating Natural Beauty

Waterfalls are the most impactful water feature you can add to a pool. They range from architectural modern designs to naturalistic rock formations that look like they've been there for centuries.

Sheer Descent Waterfalls

Sheer descent (or sheet) waterfalls produce a smooth, unbroken curtain of water. They're mounted in raised walls, spillover spas, or dedicated water feature walls. The clean lines complement modern and contemporary pool designs.

  • Standard width: 12" to 48" (larger custom available)
  • Installation cost: $2,000-$5,000 including wall construction
  • Best for: Modern designs, formal landscapes, geometric pools
  • Pros: Clean look, adjustable flow, LED lighting options
  • Cons: Requires raised wall or beam, wind can disrupt sheet

Rock Waterfalls

Natural rock waterfalls create a grotto-like atmosphere, transforming your backyard into a tropical retreat. They range from modest boulder arrangements to elaborate multi-tier cascades with caves and grottos.

  • Size range: 3-foot accent features to 15-foot+ dramatic installations
  • Installation cost: $5,000-$25,000+ depending on size and complexity
  • Best for: Freeform pools, natural landscapes, tropical themes
  • Pros: Dramatic impact, natural appearance, can include slides or grottos
  • Cons: Higher cost, more maintenance (algae, debris), requires skilled installation

Spillover Spas

A raised spa that spills into the main pool combines two features in one. The spillover edge acts as a continuous waterfall while providing the therapeutic benefits of a spa. This is one of the most popular configurations for Main Line properties.

  • Installation cost: $15,000-$35,000 (for spa with spillover feature)
  • Best for: Families wanting both spa and water feature
  • Pros: Dual functionality, continuous water movement, elegant appearance
  • Cons: Higher initial cost, spa maintenance required

Design Tip: Sound Considerations

The sound of a waterfall depends on drop height and water volume. Higher drops and more volume create louder sounds - great for masking traffic noise but potentially overwhelming near outdoor living areas. Discuss sound preferences during design to ensure the right balance.

Deck Jets and Fountains

Deck jets are among the most versatile and affordable water features. Installed flush with the pool deck, they shoot adjustable arcs of water into the pool. Multiple jets can be choreographed for stunning visual displays.

How Deck Jets Work

Each deck jet connects to its own dedicated plumbing line with an adjustable nozzle. The arc height and angle can be fine-tuned after installation. Most homeowners install 4-8 jets for visual impact, though some designs use 12 or more.

Deck Jet Configurations

  • Paired opposing: Jets on opposite sides create crossing arcs
  • Corner clusters: Multiple jets from corners for dramatic corner effect
  • Linear array: Row of jets along one side for consistent water curtain
  • Surrounding: Jets around perimeter shooting inward

Cost Breakdown

  • Individual deck jet: $150-$400 for the fixture
  • Installation per jet: $300-$600 including plumbing
  • Control system: $500-$2,000 for programmable controls
  • LED lighting add-on: $100-$300 per jet

A typical 6-jet installation runs $3,000-$6,000 including all components and installation. Adding LED lighting brings the total to $4,000-$8,000.

Bubblers: Fun for Sun Shelves

Bubblers (also called gushers or pop-ups) are installed in the pool floor, typically in shallow tanning ledges or beach entries. They push water up in a gentle bubbling spray that's perfect for children and creates visual interest in shallow areas.

Ideal Locations for Bubblers

  • Tanning ledges: The most popular location - adds movement to lounging areas
  • Beach entries: Creates interactive play area for young children
  • Shallow step areas: Adds interest to pool entry zones
  • Baja shelves: Combines with in-pool seating areas

Bubbler Specifications

  • Water depth: Work best in 6-12 inches of water
  • Height: Adjustable from 6 inches to 2+ feet
  • Quantity: Usually installed in groups of 2-4
  • Cost per bubbler: $500-$2,000 installed

Bubblers are particularly popular with families because they create a safe, engaging play area for young children while parents relax nearby on tanning ledge loungers.

Laminar Jets: Modern Elegance

Laminar jets represent the pinnacle of water feature technology. They produce perfectly smooth, glass-like streams of water that maintain their shape from source to pool. With integrated fiber optic lighting, the stream itself glows with vibrant color.

How Laminar Jets Differ

Unlike standard jets that produce turbulent, splashing streams, laminar jets use special nozzle technology to create laminar (non-turbulent) flow. The result is a crystalline arc that looks almost solid - especially dramatic at night with lighting.

Laminar Jet Options

  • Standard laminar: Clear stream, can add LED lighting in housing
  • Fiber optic laminar: Light travels through the water stream itself
  • Color-changing: Programmable RGB lighting for any color
  • Jumping jets: Programmed to turn on/off in sequences

Installation Considerations

  • Wind sensitivity: Laminar streams are affected by wind more than turbulent jets
  • Power requirements: Fiber optic systems need dedicated electrical
  • Automation recommended: Best controlled through pool automation systems
  • Professional installation: Precise alignment critical for proper appearance

LED Lighting for Water Features

Lighting transforms water features from daytime attractions to nighttime showpieces. Modern LED systems offer color-changing capabilities, programmable shows, and smartphone control.

Lighting Options

  • Underwater spotlights: Illuminate waterfalls from below ($300-$800 each)
  • In-stream lighting: LEDs mounted in sheer descent channels
  • Fiber optic: Light travels through laminar streams
  • Landscape lighting: Accent lighting on rock features

Control Systems

Modern pool automation systems allow complete control of water features and lighting from your smartphone. Program sunset shows, party modes, or simple on/off schedules. Popular systems include Pentair IntelliCenter, Jandy iAquaLink, and Hayward OmniLogic.

Combining Multiple Features

The most impressive pools combine multiple water features for layered visual and auditory impact. Here are popular combinations:

Classic Combination: Spillover Spa + Deck Jets

A raised spillover spa provides continuous background water sound while deck jets add playful, adjustable accents. This combination suits both formal entertaining and family fun.

Natural Combination: Rock Waterfall + Bubblers

A naturalistic rock waterfall combined with bubblers on a tanning ledge creates a resort feel. The waterfall establishes the tropical atmosphere while bubblers add interactive elements for children.

Modern Combination: Sheer Descent + Laminar Jets

For contemporary designs, a sheer descent waterfall paired with color-changing laminar jets creates a sophisticated, architecturally stunning presentation, especially dramatic at night.

Water Features for Fiberglass Pools

Fiberglass pools work beautifully with most water features, though some considerations apply:

Best Features for Fiberglass

  • Deck jets: Install in surrounding deck, not pool shell
  • Bubblers: Many fiberglass pools come with pre-installed ports
  • Sheer descents: Mount in separate raised wall, not shell
  • Laminar jets: Install in deck, arc into pool
  • Spillover spas: Fiberglass spas pair perfectly

Considerations for Fiberglass

Unlike concrete pools, you cannot drill into fiberglass shells after manufacturing without voiding warranties. Plan water features during the design phase. For existing fiberglass pools, deck-mounted features (jets, laminars) are the best retrofit options.

Winterization in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania's freezing winters require proper winterization of all water features. This is typically included in professional pool closing services.

Winterization Process

  • Drain all lines: Water feature plumbing must be completely drained
  • Blow out with air: Compressed air removes residual water
  • Antifreeze optional: Some installers add pool antifreeze as backup
  • Protect exposed components: Cover or remove vulnerable parts

Spring Startup

Water features are tested and adjusted during spring pool opening. Jets may need realignment, and all lighting should be checked before the season begins.

Maintenance Requirements

Well-designed water features add minimal maintenance burden. Here's what to expect:

Weekly Maintenance

  • Check that all features operate properly
  • Clear debris from around rock waterfalls
  • Verify jets are aligned correctly

Monthly Maintenance

  • Clean jet nozzles if calcium buildup appears
  • Check lighting operation
  • Inspect waterfall surfaces for algae (rock features)

Seasonal Maintenance

  • Professional winterization (fall)
  • Spring startup and adjustment
  • Annual inspection of pumps and plumbing

Pro Tip: Water Quality Benefits

Water features actually help maintain water quality by improving circulation and aeration. Running waterfalls or fountains during evening hours also helps cool the pool through evaporation - particularly welcome during hot Pennsylvania summers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do pool water features cost?

Pool water feature costs vary widely: Deck jets and bubblers cost $500-$2,000 each, sheer descent waterfalls $2,000-$5,000, rock waterfalls $5,000-$25,000+, and laminar jets $1,500-$3,500 each. Most Pennsylvania homeowners spend $3,000-$15,000 total on water features.

Do water features increase pool maintenance?

Water features add minimal maintenance when properly designed. The main considerations are: additional plumbing to winterize, potential for more debris if waterfall includes landscaping, and keeping fountain heads clean. The water circulation actually helps with pool health.

Can I add water features to an existing pool?

Yes, many water features can be retrofitted to existing pools. Deck jets, bubblers, and some fountains are relatively easy to add. Larger features like rock waterfalls or sheer descents may require more extensive work and access to the pool's plumbing system.

Do water features cool the pool?

Yes, water features can help cool pool water through evaporation and aeration. Running waterfalls or fountains during evening hours when air is cooler than water can lower water temperature by 2-4 degrees. This is especially beneficial during hot Pennsylvania summers.

What water features work best with fiberglass pools?

Fiberglass pools pair excellently with deck jets, bubblers, laminar jets, and spillover spas. Sheer descent waterfalls can be incorporated into raised walls. Large rock waterfalls typically require a separate raised bond beam structure rather than attaching directly to the fiberglass shell.
