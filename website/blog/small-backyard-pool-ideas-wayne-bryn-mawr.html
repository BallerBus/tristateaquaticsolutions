Small Backyard Pool Ideas for Wayne and Bryn Mawr Homes

Think you don't have room for a pool? Think again. From plunge pools to cocktail pools to compact fiberglass designs, there are beautiful options that fit even the most modest Main Line backyards.

Many Wayne and Bryn Mawr homeowners assume their backyards are too small for a pool. While it's true that you won't fit an Olympic-sized lap pool in a townhouse patio, the pool industry has evolved to offer beautiful, functional pools for spaces once considered too compact.

Today's small pool designs are intentional, not compromised. They're engineered to maximize every square foot while delivering the cooling, relaxation, and aesthetic benefits that make pools desirable in the first place.

Main Line Setback Requirements

Most Main Line townships require pools to be at least 10 feet from rear property lines and 5-10 feet from side property lines. Lower Merion, Radnor, and Tredyffrin all have specific zoning requirements that affect how much of your yard can be used for a pool. We'll help you navigate these during planning.

Small Pool Types for Main Line Homes

Plunge Pools

The smallest category of inground pools, plunge pools are designed for cooling off, lounging, and relaxation rather than lap swimming. They've become extremely popular in urban and suburban settings.

10-20' Typical Length
6-10' Typical Width
$45-65K Price Range
  • Perfect for cooling off after yard work or a hot day
  • Often paired with jets for resistance exercise
  • Can be heated for year-round use
  • Minimal water volume means faster heating and lower chemical costs

Cocktail Pools (Spools)

A cocktail pool (or "spool" - spa + pool) is slightly larger than a plunge pool and typically includes spa-like features such as jets, seating, and heating. They're designed for entertaining and relaxation.

12-18' Typical Length
8-12' Typical Width
$55-80K Price Range
  • Combines pool and spa functionality in one unit
  • Built-in seating is standard
  • Often includes water features like bubblers or spillovers
  • Year-round use with heating capability

Compact Fiberglass Pools

Traditional fiberglass pools are available in smaller sizes that work well for modest backyards while still providing a "real pool" experience.

20-28' Typical Length
10-14' Typical Width
$65-85K Price Range
  • Large enough for actual swimming
  • Can include tanning ledges and bench seating
  • Many shape options available
  • Fast installation (2-3 weeks)

Design Strategies for Small Yards

Smart design choices can make a small pool feel larger and your backyard more functional.

Shape Selection

  • Rectangular: Maximizes swimming space and creates clean sightlines that make yards feel larger
  • Kidney or freeform: Can work around landscaping and create varied depths in compact spaces
  • L-shaped: Utilizes corners and creates distinct zones (shallow lounge, deeper swim area)
  • Round or oval: Efficient use of space, but less functional for swimming

Deck Considerations

  • Minimal deck: A narrow coping-only approach maximizes pool size
  • Cantilevered deck: Extends over the pool edge, creating more usable patio without taking pool space
  • Multi-level decking: Creates visual interest and defines spaces in small areas
  • Integrated planters: Add greenery without eating into limited ground space

Visual Tricks for Small Spaces

Use lighter-colored pool finishes (blues, whites) to make the water appear more open. Mirror effects from glass tile or water features can also create a sense of expanded space. Avoid dark finishes, which can make small pools feel like hot tubs.

Features That Work in Small Pools

Tanning Ledge (Baja Shelf)

A shallow (6-8") shelf for lounge chairs in the water. Perfect for cooling off without full immersion. Works great in small pools where swim space is limited.

Swim Jets (Endless Pool Style)

Powerful jets create a current to swim against, enabling lap-swimming exercise in pools too small for actual laps. Transform a plunge pool into a fitness tool.

Spillover Spa

A raised hot tub that spills into the main pool adds luxury without taking ground-level space. Creates beautiful water feature sounds.

Bubblers and Deck Jets

Water features that don't take up pool space but add visual interest and soothing sounds. Great for entertaining ambiance.

Fire Features

Fire bowls or a fire pit near the pool create dramatic evening ambiance and extend pool season into cooler months. Perfect for small outdoor spaces.

Built-in Seating

Underwater benches and loungers provide gathering spots that don't reduce swim area. Multiple people can enjoy the pool without crowding.

Local Considerations for Main Line Small Pools

Township Requirements

Each township has specific requirements that affect small pool installations:

  • Lower Merion: 10' rear setback, 5' side setback, fencing requirements
  • Radnor: 10' setbacks all sides, specific barrier requirements
  • Tredyffrin: Setbacks vary by zone, stormwater considerations
  • Easttown: May require conditional use in some areas

HOA Considerations

Many Wayne and Bryn Mawr neighborhoods have HOA restrictions on pools. Common requirements include:

  • Architectural review and approval
  • Fencing style and material requirements
  • Equipment placement and screening
  • Construction timing and noise restrictions

Access Challenges

Smaller lots often have more challenging access for equipment. We assess:

  • Crane access for fiberglass shell delivery
  • Excavation equipment access
  • Material staging areas
  • Protection for existing landscaping

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the smallest pool size for a backyard?

The smallest practical inground pools are plunge pools, which start at around 10 feet long by 6-8 feet wide. These compact pools typically hold 2,000-5,000 gallons and fit backyards as small as 15' x 20'. Some manufacturers offer cocktail pools as small as 8' x 10'.

How much does a small backyard pool cost?

Small fiberglass pools (under 25 feet) typically cost $50,000-$75,000 installed in the Main Line area. Plunge pools range from $45,000-$65,000. The smaller footprint reduces excavation costs, but equipment and installation labor remain similar to larger pools, so savings aren't proportional to size.

What is a cocktail pool?

A cocktail pool (also called a spool) is a small pool typically 10-16 feet long and 6-10 feet wide, designed for lounging, cooling off, and entertaining rather than lap swimming. Many include spa jets and heating for year-round use. They're ideal for urban and suburban properties with limited space.

Can I put a pool in a 20x20 backyard?

Yes, a 20' x 20' backyard (400 sq ft) can accommodate a small plunge pool or cocktail pool. You'd need to account for setback requirements (typically 5-10' from property lines) and leave some deck space. A 10' x 15' pool would fit while leaving room for patio furniture.

Are small pools easier to maintain?

Small pools have less surface area to clean and less water to treat, but chemistry can fluctuate more quickly in smaller volumes. Overall maintenance time is reduced, but you may need to check chemistry more frequently. Heating and running costs are lower due to smaller water volume.

What's the best pool shape for a small yard?

Rectangular pools maximize usable swimming space in small yards. Geometric shapes (rectangle, square, L-shape) work better with limited space than freeform designs. Corner installations with L-shaped pools can utilize otherwise unusable space effectively.
