Pool Investment vs Other Home Improvements: Where to Spend Your Money

Considering a pool but wondering if the money would be better spent on a kitchen remodel, outdoor kitchen, or other improvement? We compare pools to other major home investments, examining ROI, lifestyle value, and which makes sense for different situations.

When you have $70,000-$100,000 to invest in your home, you have options. A pool is a significant investment - but so is a major kitchen remodel, finished basement, or comprehensive landscaping. How do you decide where to put your money?

This guide compares pools to other major home improvements, looking at both financial returns and lifestyle value. Because sometimes the best investment isn't the one with the highest ROI.

Understanding ROI in Home Improvements

What ROI Actually Means

Return on Investment (ROI) for home improvements measures what percentage of your spending you recover when you sell. An 80% ROI on a $50,000 project means you add $40,000 to your home's value.

Why ROI Isn't Everything

  • You're living there: Unlike stocks, you get to enjoy home improvements daily
  • Lifestyle value: Some improvements transform how you live
  • Time horizon: If you're staying 15+ years, annual enjoyment matters more than resale value
  • Market factors: ROI varies by neighborhood and buyer preferences

The Better Question

Instead of "What has the highest ROI?", ask "What improvement will make the biggest positive difference in my family's daily life?" A pool you use 100+ days per year has more real value than a kitchen remodel you appreciate for 30 minutes at breakfast.

When to Choose the Kitchen

  • Your kitchen is genuinely outdated or dysfunctional
  • You're an avid cook or entertainer indoors
  • Resale value is your primary motivation
  • You already have adequate outdoor living space
  • You're selling within 2-3 years

When to Choose the Pool

  • Your kitchen is adequate (even if not perfect)
  • Your family values outdoor recreation
  • You have children who will use the pool for years
  • You're staying 5+ years to enjoy it
  • You want to create a backyard lifestyle destination

The Smart Play: Both

Pool and outdoor kitchen are often complementary rather than competing investments. Many homeowners install both as part of a complete outdoor living space. If budget requires choosing:

  • Choose pool first if you have children or active family
  • Choose outdoor kitchen first if adult entertaining is your priority
  • Budget for both by starting with a fiberglass pool (lower maintenance cost) and adding outdoor kitchen later

Considerations by Family Type

Families with young children:

  • Pool: Summer recreation, swimming lessons, family activity
  • Basement: Year-round playroom, bad weather option
  • Often best: Pool first, basement can wait

Families with teenagers:

  • Pool: Friends gather, summer hangout, exercise
  • Basement: Gaming, media room, teen space
  • Depends on: Teen preferences and social patterns

Empty nesters:

  • Pool: Exercise, relaxation, grandkid visits
  • Basement: Guest suite, hobby space, home office
  • Consider: Which will you actually use more?

Pool vs Comprehensive Landscaping

The Integration Opportunity

Pool installation often includes significant landscaping as part of the project. A well-designed pool package may accomplish both goals:

  • Pool creates a focal point for landscape design
  • Decking and patios are included in pool projects
  • Many pool contracts include landscape repair/basic planting
  • Professional design integrates pool with existing landscape

Landscaping Without Pool

If you choose landscaping alone, consider:

  • Beautiful to look at, but less interactive
  • Requires ongoing maintenance (you or lawn service)
  • Plants mature over time - best value comes later
  • Doesn't provide recreation or exercise opportunity

Beyond ROI: The Lifestyle Value

What Pools Actually Provide

  • Exercise: Low-impact cardiovascular activity
  • Family time: Unplugged hours together
  • Social hub: Natural gathering space
  • Mental health: Water's calming effect
  • Home staycations: Resort feeling without travel
  • Property transformation: Completely changes your home's feel

Calculating Real Value

Consider this exercise:

  1. Estimate pool use days per year (100-150 typical)
  2. Value each day at $50 (less than a family outing)
  3. That's $5,000-$7,500 in annual "lifestyle value"
  4. Over 15 years: $75,000-$112,500 in enjoyment
  5. This exceeds the pool's cost before considering resale value

The Vacation Comparison

A family vacation might cost $5,000-$10,000 for one week. A pool provides "vacation moments" all summer, every year, for decades. The $85,000 pool cost equals 8-17 family vacations - but delivers value for 25+ years.

Making Your Decision

Choose a Pool If:

  • Your family values outdoor recreation
  • You're staying in the home 5+ years
  • You have children or grandchildren
  • Exercise and health matter to you
  • You want to transform your backyard lifestyle
  • Your neighborhood values pools (Main Line, Delaware)

Choose Other Improvements If:

  • Your kitchen/bath is genuinely non-functional
  • You're selling within 2-3 years
  • No one in your family would use a pool
  • You prefer indoor activities
  • Your property can't accommodate a pool

The Right Answer is Personal

There's no universal "best" investment. A pool is transformative for some families and unused by others. The best investment is the one that:

  1. Your family will actually use and enjoy
  2. Fits your lifestyle and preferences
  3. Makes financial sense for your situation
  4. Enhances how you live, not just how your home looks

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a pool a good investment compared to a kitchen remodel?

It depends on your priorities. Kitchen remodels typically have higher ROI (60-80% cost recovery) but lower enjoyment hours. Pools have lower ROI (40-60%) but provide daily recreation for families. In pool-friendly markets like Main Line PA, pools can significantly enhance property appeal.

How much value does a pool add to a home?

In Main Line PA and Northern Delaware, pools typically add 5-10% to home value. A $85,000 fiberglass pool on a $750,000 home might add $40,000-$60,000 to sale price - a 47-70% cost recovery. Value varies by neighborhood and pool type.

What home improvement has the best ROI?

Garage door replacement (94% ROI), minor kitchen remodel (81%), and manufactured stone veneer (91%) typically have highest ROI. However, ROI isn't everything - these don't provide daily lifestyle enjoyment like pools, outdoor kitchens, or finished basements.

Should I get a pool or finish my basement?

Choose based on how your family spends time. Pools offer outdoor recreation, exercise, and summer entertainment. Finished basements provide year-round indoor space. Costs are similar ($70,000-$100,000), but lifestyle benefits differ significantly.

Is a pool or outdoor kitchen a better investment?

They serve different purposes and are often complementary. Pools cost $70,000-$100,000 with 50-60% ROI. Outdoor kitchens cost $15,000-$50,000 with similar ROI. Many homeowners do both. If choosing one, pools offer more daily use opportunities for families.
