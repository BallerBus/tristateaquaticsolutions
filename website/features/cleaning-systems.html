Cleaning Types

Pool Cleaner Categories

Each type of pool cleaner offers unique advantages for different pool types and budgets.

Robotic Cleaners

Self-contained units with built-in motors and filtration. The most efficient and effective cleaning solution, operating independently from your pool pump.

$400 - $3,000
  • Independent operation - no pump required
  • Energy efficient (low voltage motors)
  • Built-in filtration captures fine debris
  • Cleans floors, walls, and waterline
  • Smart navigation and scheduling

Pressure-Side Cleaners

Powered by water pressure from a dedicated booster pump or return line. Excellent for larger debris like leaves and acorns with built-in debris bags.

$300 - $800
  • Great for heavy debris areas
  • Built-in debris bag
  • Doesn't strain skimmer system
  • Aids water circulation
  • Durable with fewer parts

Suction-Side Cleaners

Connect to your skimmer or dedicated suction line. Most affordable option that uses your existing pump and filter system for debris collection.

$150 - $500
  • Most affordable option
  • Uses existing pump system
  • Simple installation
  • Good for light debris
  • Minimal maintenance

In-Floor Cleaning Systems

Built into the pool during construction with pop-up cleaning heads that rotate and push debris to the main drain. The ultimate invisible cleaning solution.

$6,000 - $10,000+
  • Completely invisible when not in use
  • Automated daily cleaning cycles
  • Improves chemical distribution
  • Enhances water circulation
  • No equipment to store

Considerations

Robotic Cleaner Pros & Cons

Understanding the advantages and limitations of robotic pool cleaners.

Advantages

  • Independent operation doesn't strain your pool pump or filter
  • Energy efficient - uses 60-90% less electricity than other cleaners
  • Cleans floors, walls, and waterline in a single cycle
  • Built-in filtration captures debris as small as 2 microns
  • Smart navigation for efficient, thorough coverage
  • WiFi control and scheduling on premium models
  • No installation required - plug and play operation
  • Reduces chemical usage by improving circulation

Considerations

  • Higher upfront cost than suction or pressure-side cleaners
  • Filter cartridges need regular cleaning and occasional replacement
  • Should be stored out of direct sunlight when not in use
  • Cord length limits reach - may need repositioning for large pools
  • More complex electronics means potential for more repairs
  • Very large debris may need manual removal first
  • Requires storage space for unit and caddy
Premium Option

Built-In In-Floor Cleaning Systems

The ultimate in automated pool cleaning, in-floor systems are integrated during pool construction. Pop-up cleaning heads retract flush with the floor and walls, providing invisible cleaning that operates automatically every day.

  • Completely invisible when not operating - no equipment to remove
  • Automated daily cleaning cycles run on schedule
  • Improves chemical distribution and water quality
  • Reduces dead spots and algae formation
  • Long lifespan with minimal maintenance
  • Works with pool automation systems

Care Tips

Cleaner Maintenance

Keep your pool cleaner performing at its best with regular maintenance.

After Each Use

Rinse robotic cleaners with fresh water. Empty debris bags and filter baskets.

Weekly

Deep clean filters with filter cleaner solution. Check for worn brushes or tracks.

Monthly

Inspect hoses and connections. Lubricate o-rings. Check for wear on moving parts.

Annually

Professional inspection. Replace worn parts (tracks, brushes, wheels). Firmware updates for smart models.

FAQ

Common Questions

What is the best type of pool cleaner?

Robotic cleaners offer the best overall cleaning with independent operation and advanced filtration. They don't rely on your pump system and use less energy. For new construction, in-floor systems provide invisible, comprehensive cleaning built into the pool. The best choice depends on your budget, pool type, and maintenance preferences.

How much do robotic pool cleaners cost?

Entry-level robotic cleaners start at $400-$700. Mid-range models with smart features cost $800-$1,500. Premium models like Dolphin Sigma or Polaris Alpha iQ range from $1,500-$3,000. In-floor systems cost $6,000-$10,000+ installed. Consider the long-term energy and time savings when evaluating cost.

Are robotic pool cleaners worth it?

Yes, robotic cleaners save time and reduce chemical usage by improving filtration. They operate independently from your pool pump, reducing energy costs. Most homeowners find they recoup the investment through time savings and reduced maintenance costs within 2-3 years. The convenience of hands-free cleaning is often the biggest benefit.

How long do robotic pool cleaners last?

Quality robotic cleaners last 5-8 years with proper maintenance. Key factors include rinsing after use, storing out of direct sunlight, and replacing worn parts (brushes, filters, tracks) as needed. Extended warranties are often available, and premium brands like Dolphin and Polaris have strong service networks.

What is an in-floor pool cleaning system?

In-floor cleaning systems feature pop-up heads installed in the pool floor and walls. They rotate in a programmed sequence, directing debris toward the main drain while circulating chemicals evenly. Paramount PCC2000 and PV3 are leading brands. Best installed during new pool construction as they require in-floor plumbing.
