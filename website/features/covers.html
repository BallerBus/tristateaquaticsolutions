Types of Pool Covers

From basic solar blankets to premium automatic covers, find the right protection for your pool.

Solar Covers

$100 - $500

Bubble-style covers that float on the water surface. Heat the pool using solar energy and reduce evaporation by up to 95%.

  • Heats pool 10-15 degrees naturally
  • Reduces evaporation dramatically
  • Cuts chemical usage 35-60%
  • Keeps debris out when in use
  • 3-5 year lifespan
  • NOT a safety cover

Mesh Safety Covers

$1,500 - $4,000

Durable mesh fabric that allows rainwater to drain through while blocking debris and providing child/pet safety.

  • ASTM safety certified
  • Supports significant weight
  • Water drains through
  • Lightweight, easy to handle
  • 10-15 year lifespan
  • Custom fit to any pool shape

Solid Safety Covers

$2,000 - $5,000

Solid vinyl covers that block all light and debris. Keeps pool cleaner but requires a cover pump for rain water.

  • ASTM safety certified
  • Blocks all sunlight (no algae)
  • Pool opens cleaner in spring
  • Requires cover pump
  • 7-12 year lifespan
  • Heavier than mesh covers

Automatic Covers

$12,000 - $25,000+

Motorized covers that open and close at the push of a button. The ultimate in convenience, safety, and energy savings.

  • ASTM safety certified
  • Open/close in under 1 minute
  • Reduces heating costs 50-70%
  • Key-switch or remote operation
  • 8-12 year lifespan
  • Can be automated with pool system

FAQ

Common Questions

How much does a pool cover cost?

Solar covers cost $100-$500. Mesh safety covers cost $1,500-$4,000 installed. Solid safety covers cost $2,000-$5,000 installed. Automatic covers cost $12,000-$25,000+ installed. Prices vary based on pool size and shape.

Are automatic pool covers worth it?

Automatic covers provide unmatched convenience, safety, and energy savings. They reduce heating costs by 50-70%, evaporation by up to 95%, and chemical usage significantly. The high upfront cost is often offset by long-term savings and increased home value.

Do pool covers really save money?

Yes, pool covers save significant money. They reduce heat loss by 50-70%, cutting heating costs substantially. They also reduce water evaporation by up to 95% (saving water and refill costs) and decrease chemical usage by 35-60%.

How long do pool covers last?

Solar covers typically last 3-5 years. Mesh safety covers last 10-15 years with proper care. Solid safety covers last 7-12 years. Automatic cover fabric lasts 8-12 years, though the mechanism can last longer with proper maintenance.

Can pool covers support weight?

Safety covers (mesh, solid, and automatic) are designed to support significant weight and meet ASTM safety standards. They can typically hold several hundred pounds. Solar covers are NOT weight-bearing and should never be walked on.
