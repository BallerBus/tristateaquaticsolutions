Materials

Pool Decking Options

Each material offers unique benefits for durability, aesthetics, comfort, and maintenance.

Most Popular

Concrete Pavers

Interlocking concrete pavers offer endless design possibilities with excellent durability. Individual pieces allow for easy repairs and flexibility during ground movement.

$12 - $20 per sq ft installed
  • Virtually unlimited color and pattern options
  • Individual pavers replaceable if damaged
  • Excellent slip resistance when textured
  • 25-50 year lifespan
  • Low maintenance requirements
Coolest Surface

Travertine

Natural travertine stone stays remarkably cool in summer sun, up to 30 degrees cooler than concrete. Its natural texture provides excellent grip while adding timeless elegance.

$15 - $25 per sq ft installed
  • Naturally stays cool underfoot
  • Non-slip natural texture
  • Elegant, timeless appearance
  • Each piece uniquely beautiful
  • Increases property value
Budget Friendly

Stamped Concrete

Poured concrete with decorative patterns and colors offers the look of pavers or stone at a lower cost. Seamless installation with endless pattern possibilities.

$8 - $15 per sq ft installed
  • Most affordable option
  • Wide variety of patterns and colors
  • Seamless, no joints to maintain
  • Quick installation
  • Can mimic stone, brick, or wood

Natural Stone

Premium flagstone, bluestone, or limestone creates a one-of-a-kind outdoor space. Each stone is unique, providing unmatched natural beauty and character.

$20 - $35+ per sq ft installed
  • Unique, premium appearance
  • Exceptional durability (50+ years)
  • Natural slip resistance
  • Maximum property value increase
  • Environmentally sustainable

Composite Decking

Wood-alternative composite materials offer the warmth of wood without the maintenance. Ideal for elevated pool decks and areas around the pool.

$15 - $30 per sq ft installed
  • Low maintenance - no staining needed
  • Splinter-free and comfortable
  • Won't rot, warp, or crack
  • Cool-touch technology available
  • 25-30 year warranty

Cool Deck Coating

Acrylic coating applied over existing concrete to reduce surface temperature and add texture. An economical way to upgrade an existing deck.

$4 - $8 per sq ft installed
  • Reduces temperature 20-40 degrees
  • Covers existing concrete
  • Adds slip-resistant texture
  • Multiple color options
  • Most affordable upgrade

Planning

Key Considerations

Important factors to evaluate when choosing your pool decking material.

Heat & Comfort

Surface temperature is crucial for barefoot comfort. Lighter colors and certain materials like travertine stay significantly cooler. Consider how much direct sun exposure your deck receives and whether cooling technology is worth the investment for your climate.

Slip Resistance

Pool decks get wet constantly. Look for textured surfaces that provide grip when wet. Most quality pavers, travertine, and properly finished concrete offer good slip resistance. Avoid highly polished surfaces near the water.

Longevity

Quality decking is a significant investment that should last decades. Natural stone and pavers typically outlast stamped concrete. Consider the total cost of ownership including maintenance and potential repairs over 20-30 years.

Design Flexibility

Consider how the deck integrates with your landscape, home architecture, and pool design. Some materials offer more color and pattern options. Think about borders, accents, and how the deck transitions to other outdoor areas.

Care Tips

Deck Maintenance

Keep your pool deck looking beautiful with regular care.

Weekly

Sweep or blow off debris. Rinse with hose to remove pool chemicals and prevent buildup.

Seasonally

Deep clean with appropriate cleaner. Inspect for cracks, loose pavers, or damaged areas.

Every 2-3 Years

Re-seal stamped concrete and travertine. Re-sand paver joints if needed. Power wash carefully.

Professional Check

Annual inspection for settling, drainage issues, and expansion joint maintenance.

FAQ

Common Questions

What is the best material for a pool deck?

The best pool deck material depends on your priorities. Concrete pavers offer durability and design flexibility. Travertine provides natural beauty and stays cool. Stamped concrete is budget-friendly with many pattern options. Natural stone offers premium aesthetics. Consider heat, slip resistance, maintenance, and budget when making your decision.

How much does pool decking cost?

Pool decking costs range from $8-$35+ per square foot installed. Basic stamped concrete costs $8-$15/sq ft. Concrete pavers cost $12-$20/sq ft. Travertine costs $15-$25/sq ft. Premium natural stone costs $20-$35+/sq ft. A typical 500 sq ft deck costs $4,000-$17,500 depending on material choice.

What pool decking stays coolest?

Travertine and light-colored pavers stay coolest, up to 30 degrees cooler than dark concrete. Special cool deck coatings can reduce surface temperature by 20-40 degrees. Textured surfaces and lighter colors reflect more heat. Composite decking with cooling technology is also available for elevated deck areas.

How long does pool decking last?

Well-installed pool decking lasts 20-50+ years. Concrete pavers and natural stone last 25-50 years. Stamped concrete lasts 15-25 years with proper sealing. Travertine lasts 25-40 years. Composite decking lasts 25-30 years. Longevity depends on climate, maintenance, proper installation, and quality of materials.

Should pool decking be sealed?

Most pool decking benefits from sealing. Stamped concrete requires sealing every 2-3 years to maintain color and prevent damage. Pavers can be sealed to enhance color and prevent staining, though it's optional. Travertine and natural stone should be sealed to prevent absorption. Sealing protects against pool chemicals, UV damage, and staining.
