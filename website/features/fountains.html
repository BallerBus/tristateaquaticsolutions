Types of Pool Fountains

From playful deck jets to elegant laminar streams, find the perfect fountain feature for your pool.

Deck Jets

Arcing streams of water that shoot from the pool deck into the water. Creates a playful, splashing effect. Available in adjustable heights and angles.

$300 - $500 per jet

Laminar Jets

Crystal-clear, glass-like streams of water with internal fiber optic lighting. Creates stunning nighttime effects with color-changing LEDs.

$1,500 - $3,000 per jet

Bubbler Fountains

Gentle bubbling water on tanning ledges or beach entries. Safe and fun for kids, creates a soothing spa-like atmosphere.

$400 - $800 per bubbler

FAQ

Common Questions

How much do pool fountains cost?

Pool fountains range from $300-$500 per deck jet to $1,500-$3,000 for laminar jets with LED lighting. Complete fountain systems with multiple jets typically cost $2,000-$5,000+ installed, depending on complexity and features.

What is the difference between deck jets and laminar jets?

Deck jets create a turbulent, splashing arc of water from the deck into the pool. Laminar jets use a special nozzle to produce smooth, glass-like streams that can be illuminated with fiber optic lighting for stunning nighttime effects. Laminars are more expensive but create more dramatic visual effects.

Can fountains be added to an existing pool?

Yes, most fountain types can be retrofitted to existing pools. Deck jets and bubblers are easiest to add, requiring minimal deck work. Laminar jets may require more extensive plumbing. A site visit helps determine the best options for your pool.

Are pool fountains safe for children?

Yes, pool fountains are safe and children love them. Bubblers on tanning ledges provide interactive play in shallow water. Deck jets add excitement to pool play. Always supervise children around water features and pools, following standard pool safety practices.

Do fountains affect pool chemicals?

Fountains improve aeration and circulation, which can help distribute chemicals evenly throughout the pool. However, increased aeration may slightly increase chlorine loss due to off-gassing. Monitor chemical levels and adjust as needed. Most pool owners find the impact minimal.
