Care Guide

Maintenance Tips

Weekly

Rinse rails with fresh water to remove chlorine and mineral deposits.

Monthly

Clean with mild soap and soft cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners.

Seasonally

Check anchor bolts for tightness. Inspect for any corrosion or damage.

Saltwater Pools

Use 316 SS. Rinse more frequently. Apply protective wax annually.

FAQ

Common Questions

What type of pool handrail is best?

Stainless steel (304 or 316 marine grade) is most durable and corrosion-resistant. Powder-coated options offer color choices and stay cooler in the sun. Choose 316 grade for saltwater pools. Standard 304 works well for traditional chlorine pools.

How much do pool handrails cost?

Basic pool ladders cost $200-$400. Standard handrails cost $300-$600 per pair. Designer rails cost $600-$1,200. ADA-compliant rails cost $800-$1,500. Professional installation adds $150-$300 per rail.

Do I need a ladder for my pool?

Pools with vertical walls (fiberglass, vinyl liner) typically need ladders for deep-end entry. Pools with built-in walk-in steps may only need handrails for support. Consider accessibility needs for all swimmers, including children and elderly users.

Are pool handrails required by code?

Requirements vary by location and pool type. Many building codes require handrails on pool steps and ladders. ADA regulations specify requirements for public pools and some residential situations. We ensure all installations meet local code requirements.

How do I maintain pool handrails?

Rinse rails weekly with fresh water to remove chlorine and minerals. Clean with mild soap monthly. Check anchor bolts seasonally. For saltwater pools, use 316 stainless steel, rinse more frequently, and apply protective marine wax annually.
