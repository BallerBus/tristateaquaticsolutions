FAQ

Common Questions

What is the best type of pool heater?

Gas heaters heat fastest and work in any weather, making them ideal for spas and occasional use. Heat pumps are most energy-efficient for regular swimmers. Solar is cheapest to operate but weather-dependent. The best choice depends on your usage patterns, budget, and climate.

How much does pool heating cost to operate?

Gas heaters cost $300-$600/month for regular use. Heat pumps cost $100-$200/month. Solar heating has minimal operating costs ($10-30/month for pump power). Actual costs depend on pool size, desired temperature, and local utility rates.

How long does it take to heat a pool?

Gas heaters can raise pool temperature 1-2 degrees per hour. Heat pumps raise temperature 0.25-0.5 degrees per hour. Heating a pool from 60F to 80F takes approximately 8-12 hours with gas or 24-48 hours with a heat pump.

Can I heat my pool year-round in Pennsylvania?

Gas heaters can heat pools even in cold weather, though costs increase significantly. Heat pumps work best when air temperature is above 50F. With proper heating and a pool cover, you can comfortably extend your season from April through October in the PA/DE region.

How long do pool heaters last?

Gas heaters typically last 5-10 years with proper maintenance and balanced water chemistry. Heat pumps last 10-20 years. Solar systems can last 15-25+ years with minimal maintenance. Regular service extends all heater lifespans.
