How It Works

Salt Chlorine Generation

Salt systems use electrolysis to convert dissolved salt into chlorine, continuously sanitizing your pool.

1

Add Salt

Pool-grade salt is added to your pool water at approximately 3,000-3,500 ppm.

2

Electrolysis

Water passes through the cell where electrical current converts salt to chlorine.

3

Sanitization

The generated chlorine sanitizes the pool, killing bacteria and algae.

4

Recycles

After sanitizing, chlorine reverts back to salt and the cycle repeats.

Maintenance Requirements

Weekly

Test pH and alkalinity. Check salt level monthly.

Monthly

Inspect cell for calcium buildup. Clean if needed.

Seasonally

Deep clean cell with acid wash. Check flow sensor.

FAQ

Common Questions

How much does a saltwater system cost?

Saltwater chlorine generators cost $1,000-$2,500 for the unit plus $300-$600 for professional installation. Cells need replacement every 3-7 years at $400-$900. Initial salt costs approximately $50-$100 depending on pool size.

Is a saltwater pool actually salt water?

Saltwater pools have about 1/10th the salt concentration of ocean water (3,000 ppm vs 35,000 ppm). The water may taste slightly salty but nothing like the ocean. Most people notice the water feels softer and silkier on their skin.

Are saltwater pools easier to maintain?

Yes, saltwater pools require less day-to-day maintenance since chlorine is generated automatically. However, you still need to monitor pH, alkalinity, and salt levels regularly. The cell requires periodic cleaning to remove calcium buildup.

Will salt damage my pool equipment?

Salt can corrode certain metals over time if not properly managed. Use salt-rated equipment, install sacrificial zinc anodes, and avoid splashing saltwater on stainless steel fixtures or natural stone. With proper maintenance, most equipment handles salt well.

Can I convert my existing pool to saltwater?

Yes, most pools can be converted to saltwater. The process involves installing a salt chlorine generator, adding pool-grade salt to the water, and potentially upgrading any equipment that is not salt-compatible. We can assess your pool during a consultation.
