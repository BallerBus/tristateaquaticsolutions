Safety First

Important Safety Requirements

Proper installation and usage guidelines ensure safe enjoyment for your family.

Water Depth Requirements

Slides typically require 3.5-4 feet minimum depth. Diving boards require 7.5-9+ feet. Always follow manufacturer specifications.

Clearance Zones

Adequate clearance around the slide entry point and landing zone. No obstructions in the path of descent or dive.

Adult Supervision

Children should always be supervised when using slides and diving boards. One person at a time on equipment.

Professional Installation

All slides and diving boards should be professionally installed to ensure proper anchoring and safety compliance.

FAQ

Common Questions

How deep does my pool need to be for a slide?

Most pool slides require a minimum water depth of 3.5-4 feet at the landing area. Larger, taller slides may require 4-5+ feet. Always check and follow the manufacturer specifications for your specific slide model.

How deep does a pool need to be for a diving board?

Diving boards require minimum depths of 7.5-9 feet or more, depending on board height and type. The diving envelope (depth, width, and distance) must meet strict safety standards. Most residential pools built today are not designed for diving boards due to liability concerns.

How much does a pool slide cost?

Basic straight pool slides cost $1,000-$3,000. Mid-range slides with curves cost $3,000-$8,000. Large custom rock slides with integrated waterfalls cost $8,000-$15,000+. Professional installation typically adds $500-$2,000 to these prices.

Are pool slides safe?

Pool slides are safe when properly installed and used according to guidelines. Key safety factors include proper water depth, non-slip surfaces, adequate clearance around landing zones, proper deck anchoring, and adult supervision for children.

Can I add a slide to my existing pool?

Yes, slides can be added to most existing pools if water depth requirements are met. The deck must be able to support the slide weight and anchoring, and there must be adequate clearance. A professional site assessment will determine feasibility and the best placement.
