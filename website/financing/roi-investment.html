How Much Does a Pool Add to Home Value?

In affluent markets like Main Line PA and Northern Delaware, a quality pool can increase your home's value by 5-15%. This is significantly higher than the national average because pools are an expected amenity in higher-end neighborhoods.

Real estate data shows that in communities where most homes have pools, not having one can actually reduce your home's value and make it harder to sell. In these markets, a pool is less of a luxury and more of a necessity.

Local Market Insight

In neighborhoods like Gladwyne, Villanova, and Greenville where pools are common, homes with pools sell 7% faster on average. In these areas, buyers expect a pool and factor it into their search criteria.

Factors That Maximize Pool ROI

Make smart decisions to ensure your pool investment delivers the best possible return.

Match Your Market

Invest proportionally to your home's value and neighborhood expectations. A $150,000 pool on a $500,000 home may not return as well as an $80,000 pool on the same home.

Tip: Aim for 7-15% of your home's value as your pool budget.

Choose Quality Materials

Higher quality pools (fiberglass, well-built concrete) last longer, require less maintenance, and appeal more to future buyers than budget options.

Tip: Fiberglass offers the best ROI due to low maintenance costs.

Consider the Full Project

A pool surrounded by a beautiful patio, landscaping, and lighting is worth more than a pool alone. Plan the complete outdoor space together.

Tip: Budget 10-20% extra for landscaping and hardscaping.

Work with Professionals

A professional installation with proper permits and documentation protects your investment and makes selling easier. Cut-rate installations can become liabilities.

Tip: Always get permits - unpermitted pools reduce home value.

Questions

Pool ROI FAQ

How much does a pool increase home value?

In affluent areas like Main Line PA and Northern Delaware, a quality pool installation can increase home value by 5-15%. On a $800,000 home, that could mean $40,000-$120,000 in added value. The exact impact depends on neighborhood pool saturation, pool quality, and overall market conditions.

Is a pool a good investment?

While pools may not provide 100% ROI on the installation cost, they offer significant value beyond dollars. In our Main Line PA and Delaware markets, pools are expected amenities for higher-end homes. The lifestyle benefits - health, family time, entertaining, relaxation - provide daily returns that are harder to quantify but very real.

Does pool type affect ROI?

Yes. Fiberglass pools typically offer the best ROI due to lower long-term maintenance costs and durability. Concrete pools can add more value to luxury homes but require more maintenance investment. The best ROI comes from pools that match your home's value and neighborhood expectations.

Do pools affect insurance costs?

Yes, pools typically increase homeowner's insurance by $50-$150 per year. This is because pools add liability risk. Most insurers require pools to be fenced and may require certain safety features. Factor this ongoing cost into your decision.

Can a pool make a home harder to sell?

In some markets with colder climates or where pools are uncommon, yes. However, in Main Line PA and Northern Delaware, pools are so common in higher-end homes that not having one can make selling harder. Know your specific neighborhood before deciding.
