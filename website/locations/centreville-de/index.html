Common Questions

Centreville Pool FAQ

How does Centreville's rural character affect pool installation?

Centreville's larger lots and rural setting often provide more flexibility for pool placement and design. However, the area's rolling terrain and mature trees require careful site planning. We conduct thorough site assessments to identify the optimal location, taking into account views, drainage, and access for construction equipment.

Can you work with equestrian properties?

Absolutely. Many Centreville properties have horses, and we have experience designing pools that integrate with working farms. We understand concerns about fencing, positioning to minimize impact on pastures, and working around farm schedules during construction.

What permits are required in Centreville?

Centreville is part of New Castle County, so you will need a building permit from the county. Delaware requires a barrier (fence) around the pool. Some properties may have additional deed restrictions. We handle all permitting and research any property-specific requirements.

How quickly can you respond to Centreville service calls?

Centreville is one of the closest communities to our Hockessin headquarters. We can typically respond to urgent issues within 30 minutes, and routine visits can often be scheduled same-day.

Do you work with historic properties?

Yes, we have experience working with Centreville's historic properties. We understand the importance of designing pools that complement historic architecture rather than competing with it. We can work with traditional materials and design elements that honor your property's heritage.
