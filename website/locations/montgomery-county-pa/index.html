Understanding Montgomery County

From Main Line Estates to Suburban Excellence

Montgomery County represents remarkable diversity in residential living. The southern portion includes the prestigious Main Line communities of Lower Merion Township, where historic estates sit on multi-acre lots with century-old trees. Moving north, thriving suburbs like Blue Bell and Lansdale offer newer developments with excellent schools and growing families.

This diversity requires a pool builder who can adapt. We work with equal expertise on estate-scale concrete installations in Gladwyne and efficient fiberglass pools in newer Hatfield developments. Our understanding of each township's permitting requirements means your project proceeds smoothly regardless of location.

With over 60 municipalities in Montgomery County, permit processes vary significantly. We maintain relationships with building departments throughout the county, ensuring efficient approvals whether you are in Lower Merion, Upper Merion, Whitpain, or any other township.

FAQ

Montgomery County Pool Questions

Do permit requirements vary by township?

Yes, significantly. Each of Montgomery County's 60+ municipalities has its own process. We know them all and handle everything.

How long does installation take?

Fiberglass pools install in 2-4 weeks. Concrete pools take 8-12 weeks. Permit timelines vary by township.

Do you serve all of Montgomery County?

We serve the southern and central portions of the county most frequently, from Lower Merion through Blue Bell and Lansdale.

What about historic districts?

Lower Merion and some other townships have historic overlay districts. We work with any applicable requirements.

What maintenance is included?

Every installation includes 1-2 years of complimentary maintenance. We also offer ongoing packages.
