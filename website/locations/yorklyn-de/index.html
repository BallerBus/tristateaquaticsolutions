Common Questions

Yorklyn Pool FAQ

How do you handle Yorklyn's wooded lots?

Many Yorklyn properties have significant tree cover. We carefully assess each site to find pool locations that minimize tree removal while ensuring proper sunlight and drainage. In some cases, strategic tree removal actually improves the health of remaining trees by reducing competition.

Can you work with well water systems?

Yes, many Yorklyn homes are on well water. We understand the considerations for filling and maintaining pools with well water, including potential mineral content and flow rate limitations. We can advise on the best approach for your specific well system.

What about properties near the Auburn Heights Preserve?

Properties adjacent to preserved land may have specific deed restrictions or setback requirements. We research these requirements early in the process and design pools that comply with any restrictions while maximizing your enjoyment of the natural views.

How do you access remote properties for construction?

We are experienced with the access challenges that come with Yorklyn's more rural properties. During our initial site visit, we assess driveways, gates, and potential construction access routes. We use appropriate equipment sizes and plan deliveries to minimize impact on your property.

What is the typical timeline for a Yorklyn pool project?

Fiberglass pools are typically installed in 2-4 weeks from permit approval. Concrete pools take 8-12 weeks. Our proximity in Hockessin means we can often start sooner and complete faster than competitors traveling from further away.
