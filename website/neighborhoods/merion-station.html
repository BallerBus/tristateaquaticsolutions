Understanding Merion Station

Where Golf History Meets Refined Living

Merion Station's character is shaped by its proximity to Merion Golf Club, one of America's most historic golf courses. The neighborhood's residences reflect this pedigree - stately stone homes, manicured gardens, and properties that have been cherished by generations of Philadelphia's finest families.

Pool installation in Merion Station requires sensitivity to this context. Many properties feature historic architecture that demands pool designs to complement rather than compete. Our team understands the importance of working within Lower Merion Township's preservation guidelines while creating outdoor spaces that enhance these exceptional properties.

We've installed pools throughout Merion Station, from intimate courtyard plunge pools hidden within walled gardens to expansive resort-style installations on the larger estates. Each project reflects our commitment to honoring Merion's legacy of excellence.

Common Questions

Merion Station Pool FAQs

How long does Lower Merion Township permitting take?

Typically 4-6 weeks for standard installations. We handle all permit applications and inspections, expediting the process through our established relationships with township officials.

Can you work with mature landscaping?

Absolutely. Many Merion Station properties have established gardens. We use specialized equipment and techniques to minimize impact on mature trees and plantings.

Do you coordinate with landscape architects?

Yes, we frequently collaborate with landscape architects and designers to ensure the pool integrates seamlessly with overall property plans.

What about historic property considerations?

We're experienced with historic properties and understand how to design pools that complement rather than detract from classic architecture and established landscapes.
