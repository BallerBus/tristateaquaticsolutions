Pool Permits in Lower Merion

Lower Merion Township maintains high standards for residential construction, including swimming pool installations. The Building and Planning Department reviews all pool permit applications to ensure compliance with local zoning ordinances, building codes, and safety requirements.

The township's permitting process is designed to protect homeowners and ensure safe, properly constructed pools that enhance property values throughout the community. With proper preparation and documentation, most pool permits are approved within 2-4 weeks.

Documentation

Application Requirements

Lower Merion Township requires the following documentation for pool permit applications.

Required Forms & Plans

Essential documentation for your pool permit application:

  • Pool permit application form (available from township)
  • Site plan showing pool location, setbacks, and utilities
  • Property survey with proposed pool placement
  • Building plans and specifications for pool construction
  • Fence/barrier plan showing safety enclosure

Contractor Documentation

Required credentials and insurance documentation:

  • Valid Pennsylvania contractor license
  • Certificate of liability insurance
  • Workers' compensation insurance certificate
  • Electrical contractor license (for electrical work)
  • Signed contractor agreement with homeowner

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a pool permit in Lower Merion Township?

The typical permit approval timeline is 2-4 weeks. This can vary depending on project complexity and whether variances are required. Peak season (spring) may have slightly longer processing times.

What are the setback requirements for pools in Lower Merion Township?

Pools must be at least 10 feet from all property lines and 10 feet from septic system components. Setbacks from buildings vary by zoning district and specific property conditions.

What fence requirements apply to pools in Lower Merion Township?

A 4-foot minimum height barrier is required around all pools. Gates must be self-closing and self-latching with latches at least 54 inches from grade. Fence openings cannot exceed 4 inches.

How much do pool permits cost in Lower Merion Township?

Pool permits range from $200-$400. Additional permits include electrical ($75-$150) and fence ($50-$100). Total costs typically range from $325-$650 depending on project scope.

Do I need a survey for a pool permit in Lower Merion Township?

Yes, a property survey showing existing structures, property lines, and proposed pool location with setback measurements is required. Many homeowners have surveys from their home purchase that can be used.
