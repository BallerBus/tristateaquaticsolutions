Application Requirements

Here is what New Castle County requires for a pool permit application. Do not worry - we prepare and submit all of this for you.

Building Permit Application

The primary permit application submitted to New Castle County Department of Land Use.

  • Completed application form
  • Property owner information
  • Contractor license details
  • Project description

Site Plan

A detailed drawing showing the proposed pool location on your property.

  • Pool location and dimensions
  • Distance from property lines
  • Location of existing structures
  • Proposed fence/barrier location

Property Survey

May be required depending on property characteristics and pool placement.

  • Certified property boundaries
  • Easement locations
  • Topographic information
  • Required if setbacks are close

Contractor License

Proof that your pool builder is properly licensed in Delaware.

  • Valid Delaware contractor license
  • Insurance certificates
  • Bond information (if required)
  • We provide all documentation

Pool Specifications

Technical details about the pool being installed.

  • Pool dimensions and depth
  • Construction type (fiberglass, concrete, etc.)
  • Equipment specifications
  • Safety features

Electrical Plans

Details for pool equipment electrical connections.

  • Equipment electrical requirements
  • GFCI protection plan
  • Bonding requirements
  • Separate electrical permit needed

Safety Requirements

Fence and Barrier Requirements

Delaware state code requires a safety barrier around all residential in-ground pools. These requirements are strictly enforced in New Castle County to ensure child safety.

We can help coordinate fence installation as part of your pool project, or work with your existing fencing contractor to ensure compliance with all requirements.

  • Minimum 4-foot (48 inches) height
  • Self-closing, self-latching gates
  • Gate latches must be out of reach of small children
  • No openings larger than 4 inches
  • Delaware state code compliant design

Common Questions

New Castle County Permit FAQ

How long does it take to get a pool permit in New Castle County, Delaware?

Pool permits in New Castle County typically take 1-3 weeks to process. This is often faster than neighboring Pennsylvania counties. Our established relationships with the Department of Land Use help expedite the process whenever possible.

What permits are required to build a pool in New Castle County?

You will need a swimming pool building permit ($100-250), an electrical permit ($50-100) for pool equipment, and typically a fence permit ($40-75) for the required safety barrier. We handle all permit applications as part of our pool installation service.

What are the setback requirements for pools in New Castle County?

Pools must typically be at least 10 feet from property lines, 15 feet from septic systems, and 25 feet from wells. Exact requirements may vary based on zoning district and property characteristics. We verify all setbacks during our site evaluation.

Do I need a fence around my pool in Delaware?

Yes, Delaware state code requires a barrier (fence) of at least 4 feet (48 inches) surrounding the pool. Gates must be self-closing and self-latching. This applies to all residential in-ground pools in New Castle County.

How much can I save with Delaware's no sales tax on pool equipment?

Delaware has no sales tax, which can save you thousands on pool materials and equipment. On a typical $75,000 pool project, this represents approximately $4,500-$6,000 in savings compared to building the same pool in Pennsylvania (6% sales tax).
