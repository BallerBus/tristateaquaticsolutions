Safety Barriers

Fence and Barrier Requirements

Willistown Township requires safety barriers around all residential swimming pools in compliance with PA pool safety code. These requirements are designed to prevent unauthorized access and protect children in the community.

Proper barrier installation is verified during the final inspection. Non-compliant barriers will require correction before the permit can be closed and the pool approved for use.

  • Barrier must completely enclose pool area with no gaps
  • Gates must swing outward, away from pool
  • Fence design must prevent climbing (no horizontal rails)
  • Maximum 4-inch gap between fence and ground

48 Inches Minimum Height

Barrier must be at least 4 feet tall measured from ground level

Self-Closing Gates

All access gates must close and latch automatically

Self-Latching Mechanism

Latch must engage automatically when gate closes

PA Pool Safety Code Compliant

All barriers must meet Pennsylvania state pool safety requirements

FAQ

Willistown Township Permit Questions

How long does it take to get a pool permit in Willistown Township?

Standard pool permit applications typically take 2-3 weeks for approval. Projects on larger equestrian properties or those requiring variances may take longer. We recommend submitting applications at least 4-6 weeks before your desired construction start date.

What are the setback requirements for pools in Willistown Township?

Willistown Township requires a minimum 10-foot setback from property lines and 10-foot setback from septic systems. Additional setback requirements may apply for properties in agricultural or conservation districts.

Do I need a fence around my pool in Willistown Township?

Yes, Willistown Township requires a barrier at least 48 inches (4 feet) high around all residential pools in compliance with PA pool safety code. Gates must be self-closing and self-latching to prevent unauthorized access.

How much does a pool permit cost in Willistown Township?

Pool permit fees typically range from $150-300 for the main inground pool permit, plus $75-125 for electrical permits and $50-100 for fence permits. Total permit costs generally range from $275-525 depending on project scope.
