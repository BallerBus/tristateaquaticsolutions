The Reality

The Math Doesn't Work Anymore

You built that pool when the kids were young. It was the center of summer. Now the kids have moved out, the grandchildren visit a few weekends a year, and you are left with a 20x40 concrete pool that demands constant attention and thousands of dollars every season.

At some point, a responsible homeowner has to ask: does this still make sense?

  • $4,500 - $8,000 per year in maintenance Chemicals, equipment repairs, resurfacing, opening and closing. The costs never stop on a pool this old.
  • $50,000+ to renovate Resurface the interior, replace the pump and filter, repair the deck, update the plumbing. And after all that, you still have a 28-year-old pool.
  • Used only 10 - 15 times per summer An honest count. Most pool owners we talk to are surprised when they actually track it.

When you do the math, you are paying roughly

$400 per swim

to maintain a pool that no longer fits your life.

The Modern Alternative

Renovate vs. Replace
