Project Background

These Hockessin homeowners built a striking contemporary home with their architect and were now ready to complete their vision with an equally modern pool that would serve as an architectural element, not just a place to swim.

They had specific requirements: a geometric shape with precise 90-degree corners, a negative edge facing the wooded view, and a grey plaster finish that would complement the home's concrete and steel aesthetic.

After interviewing several builders, they chose us because of our concrete expertise and willingness to work closely with their architect to achieve the exact design specifications they envisioned.

The Challenge & Our Solution

Where architecture meets aquatics

The Challenge

Creating a pool that functions as architectural sculpture while maintaining practical swimming functionality - and coordinating with an architect who had exacting standards.

  • Architect required precise geometric tolerances (corners within 1/8")
  • Grey plaster finish needed to match specific color specification
  • Negative edge on wooded side with complex catch basin engineering
  • All equipment to be hidden from view (no visible boxes or pipes)
  • Coordinate with landscape architect for seamless hardscape integration
  • Longer timeline acceptable but quality non-negotiable

Our Solution

A custom concrete pool built to architectural specifications with meticulous attention to detail at every phase.

  • Custom steel framework for precise 90-degree corners
  • Multiple plaster samples to achieve exact grey tone
  • Engineered negative edge with concealed catch basin
  • Underground equipment vault designed into landscape
  • Weekly coordination meetings with architect throughout build
  • Third-party quality inspections at each phase

Design Philosophy

This pool was designed as an extension of the home's architecture - a reflecting pool that becomes a work of art when not in use, and a functional swimming space when needed.

  • Rectangular geometry mirrors home's clean lines
  • Grey plaster creates dark, reflective water surface
  • Negative edge frames the wooded view like a picture
  • Flush deck-level coping eliminates visual barriers
  • No visible equipment maintains sculptural aesthetic
  • Strategic uplighting transforms pool at night

Key Takeaways

Lessons from architectural pool design

Concrete Enables Custom Design

When architectural precision matters, concrete is the only choice. Fiberglass shells cannot achieve custom shapes, exact dimensions, or specialty finishes like grey plaster.

Collaboration Elevates Results

Working alongside the client's architect from the start ensured the pool became an integral part of the home's design language, not an afterthought.

Timeline Follows Quality

These clients chose a 10-week concrete build over a 4-week fiberglass install because quality and design mattered more than speed. Good things take time.

Details Make the Difference

Hidden equipment, flush coping, and precise corners might add cost and complexity, but they're what transform a pool from functional to exceptional.
