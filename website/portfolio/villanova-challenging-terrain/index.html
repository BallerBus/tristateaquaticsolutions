Project Background

This Villanova family purchased their hillside property specifically for the stunning views. But when they started planning their pool, three different builders told them the slope was simply too severe - they'd need to look elsewhere or settle for a much smaller pool in a different location.

We saw something different. The slope wasn't a problem to overcome - it was an opportunity to create something extraordinary. With proper engineering and creative design, the terrain could become the pool's greatest feature.

After presenting our vision - a multi-level pool terrace with retaining walls that frame the view rather than fight the slope - they were ready to proceed with the "impossible" project.

The Challenge & Our Solution

Turning topography into opportunity

The Challenge

An 18-foot grade change across the proposed pool location, with soil stability concerns and complex drainage requirements.

  • 18 ft elevation change from house to property edge
  • Rocky soil requiring extensive excavation
  • Existing drainage patterns flowing toward house
  • Limited equipment access on steep terrain
  • Township required engineered plans for retaining walls
  • Neighboring property concerns about runoff

Our Solution

A comprehensive engineering approach that works with the terrain, not against it.

  • PE-stamped retaining wall design (engineer on team)
  • Terraced approach with 3 distinct levels
  • French drain system redirecting all water
  • Specialized compact excavation equipment
  • Segmental retaining walls with integrated planters
  • Multi-level composite deck connecting spaces

Key Takeaways

Lessons from challenging terrain installation

"Impossible" Often Isn't

When multiple builders decline a project, it often just means they lack the expertise or willingness to solve complex problems. The right team with engineering capability can make challenging sites work beautifully.

Slope Can Be an Asset

Rather than fighting the terrain, we embraced it. The terraced design creates distinct outdoor living zones and dramatic visual interest that a flat lot simply cannot achieve.

Engineering Prevents Problems

Upfront investment in proper engineering - PE-stamped plans, soil analysis, drainage design - prevents expensive problems later. Retaining wall failures are costly; doing it right the first time isn't.

Specialized Equipment Matters

Projects like this require compact excavators, specialized transport, and crane placement. Having relationships with specialized equipment providers makes difficult access possible.

Have a Challenging Site?

Don't let slopes, access issues, or other site challenges stop you. We specialize in engineering solutions for properties that other builders won't touch.
