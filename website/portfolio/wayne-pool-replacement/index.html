Project Background

After 30 years of concrete pool ownership, these Wayne homeowners were at a crossroads. Their pool needed another $15,000 replastering job, the third in ten years. Equipment was failing. The deck was crumbling. They were spending weekends maintaining instead of enjoying.

They considered three options: major renovation ($40K+), complete concrete replacement ($120K+), or fiberglass replacement. After we walked them through the long-term cost analysis, the choice became clear.

Fiberglass replacement would cost less than concrete renovation when factoring in 10-year maintenance savings, plus they'd get a completely modern pool with a lifetime warranty.

The Replacement Process

Demolition to dream pool

Demolition Phase

Removing 30 years of concrete required careful planning and specialized equipment.

  • Complete pool drain and decommissioning
  • Deck demolition and removal (15 dump trucks)
  • Concrete shell breakup with hydraulic hammer
  • Underground plumbing removal
  • Site grading and preparation
  • Soil compaction testing

New Installation

Once the old pool was gone, we built something truly modern.

  • Fresh excavation to new dimensions (slightly larger)
  • Premium fiberglass shell (Latham Pools)
  • All new plumbing with PVC
  • Variable-speed pump and salt system
  • LED lighting package
  • Travertine paver deck with drainage

Project Timeline

5 weeks from demolition to first swim

Week 1

Demolition & Removal

Complete drain, deck removal, concrete shell demolition, and debris hauling. The old pool was completely gone by Friday.

Week 2

Excavation & Shell Set

New excavation to updated dimensions, proper base preparation, fiberglass shell delivery and precision placement.

Week 3

Plumbing & Electrical

All new plumbing runs, electrical installation, equipment pad construction, and bonding work.

Week 4

Backfill & Deck Prep

Controlled backfill, equipment installation, deck base preparation, and paver setting begins.

Week 5

Decking & Startup

Travertine paver completion, coping installation, pool fill, water chemistry, and homeowner orientation.

Key Takeaways

Lessons for anyone considering pool replacement

Replacement Can Beat Renovation

When concrete pool maintenance costs spiral, the math often favors complete replacement. A new fiberglass pool with low maintenance can cost less over 10 years than continuing to patch an old concrete pool.

Factor Total Cost of Ownership

Initial price is just the beginning. Annual maintenance, resurfacing cycles, energy costs, and equipment life all factor into true pool cost. Fiberglass wins the long game.

Timeline is Faster Than You Think

Many homeowners assume replacement takes all summer. With proper planning, we demolished and completely rebuilt this pool in just 5 weeks - less time than a typical concrete renovation.

It's a Fresh Start

Replacement isn't just about fixing problems - it's about reimagining possibilities. These clients upgraded their pool size, added modern features, and got the deck they always wanted.
