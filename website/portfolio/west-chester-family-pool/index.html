Project Background

With three young children and a love of outdoor activities, this West Chester family knew a pool would be in their future when they built their home. They planned ahead by pre-running electrical and gas lines to the future pool location.

Their primary concern was safety. With a 4-year-old who loved water, they needed a pool design that would allow constant visibility and multiple safety layers. They also wanted features that would grow with their kids.

After researching extensively, they chose fiberglass for its smooth, non-abrasive surface (no scraped knees!) and selected us based on our family-focused design philosophy and safety expertise.

The Challenge & Our Solution

Creating a pool that works for every age

The Challenge

Designing a pool that accommodates children ages 4-10, anticipates future teenage use, and provides multiple layers of safety for parental peace of mind.

  • 4-year-old just learning to swim - needs very shallow area
  • 10-year-old wants to dive and swim laps
  • Parents want to relax while maintaining full visibility
  • Need pool fence that doesn't look institutional
  • Budget-conscious but won't compromise on safety
  • Want features that will be enjoyed for years

Our Solution

A multi-zone pool design with progressive depth, multiple safety systems, and features that adapt to children at every stage.

  • Beach entry (0-12") for safe toddler water play
  • Tanning ledge with bubblers for adult relaxation with clear sightlines
  • Gradual depth progression to 6 ft deep end
  • Removable basketball hoop for growing kids
  • Pool fence with self-closing, self-latching gate
  • Smart pool alarm system with app notifications

Project Timeline

Ready for summer fun in just 4 weeks

Week 1

Excavation & Shell Placement

Dig day excitement for the kids! Excavation designed for one day, shell delivered and set the following day using pre-existing utility connections.

Week 2

Plumbing, Electrical & Equipment

All mechanical systems installed including pump, filter, heater, salt system, and smart pool monitoring. Pool fence posts set.

Week 3

Backfill & Decking

Controlled backfill, concrete decking poured with slip-resistant finish, fence panels installed with self-closing gate.

Week 4

Finish Work & Family Orientation

Coping completion, pool fill, water chemistry, equipment commissioning, and comprehensive family safety and maintenance orientation.

Key Takeaways

Lessons for family-focused pool design

Plan Ahead for Pool Infrastructure

By pre-running electrical and gas lines during home construction, these clients saved thousands and simplified the installation process. If you're building a new home, plan for your future pool now.

Safety is an Investment, Not an Expense

Pool fence, smart alarms, and proper design aren't optional extras - they're essential investments that provide priceless peace of mind. Never compromise on safety features.

Design for Multiple Ages

A beach entry works for toddlers today and becomes a comfortable spot for elderly grandparents tomorrow. Multi-zone designs provide decades of enjoyment across all life stages.

Fiberglass Excels for Families

The smooth, non-abrasive surface of fiberglass means no scraped knees or feet. Combined with faster installation and lower maintenance, it's ideal for busy families who want to swim, not scrub.
