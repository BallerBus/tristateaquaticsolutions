Project Background

With their children grown and careers well-established, this Wilmington couple was ready to create the backyard they'd always dreamed of. They envisioned a space where they could host sophisticated gatherings, enjoy quiet evenings by the fire, and swim laps every morning.

Their biggest concern was managing multiple contractors. They'd heard horror stories of projects where the pool builder, landscaper, and kitchen contractor all blamed each other for delays and budget overruns.

They chose us specifically because we offered full project management - a single contract, single point of contact, and guaranteed coordination between all elements. We would be responsible for delivering the complete vision, not just the pool.

The Challenge & Our Solution

Orchestrating a complex, multi-trade transformation

The Challenge

Coordinating pool installation, hardscaping, landscaping, outdoor kitchen construction, gas lines, electrical, and irrigation into one seamless project.

  • 5+ different specialty contractors to coordinate
  • Sequential dependencies (pool first, then deck, then kitchen)
  • Underground utilities for gas, electric, and water throughout
  • Maintaining site access while work progresses
  • Weather windows for landscaping and concrete work
  • Keeping project on budget with scope this large

Our Solution

A comprehensive project management approach with all trades under our coordination.

  • Single general contract covering all work
  • Dedicated project manager as sole client contact
  • Pre-construction coordination meeting with all trades
  • Master schedule with critical path dependencies mapped
  • Single utility trench for gas, electric, and low-voltage
  • Weekly progress reports with updated budget tracking

Key Takeaways

Lessons from comprehensive backyard transformation

Single Source Simplifies Everything

Having one company responsible for pool, hardscape, kitchen, and landscaping eliminated finger-pointing and ensured accountability. The convenience premium was worth every dollar.

Plan Utilities Together

Running gas, electric, and low-voltage in a single coordinated trench saved weeks of work and thousands in labor. Plan all utilities before breaking ground - not as afterthoughts.

Design for Flow

The layout was designed for natural traffic patterns: house to kitchen for prep, kitchen to pool for serving, fire pit for after-swim gathering. Thoughtful flow makes the space feel larger.

Lighting Transforms Spaces

Professional landscape lighting added perhaps 7% to the budget but 50% to the evening impact. Don't skimp on lighting - it extends usable hours and creates dramatic atmosphere.
