Pool Maintenance Tips & Schedule

Master the art of pool maintenance with our comprehensive guide. Learn the essential tasks, proper schedules, and professional techniques to keep your pool crystal clear all season.

Proper pool maintenance is the key to enjoying clean, safe water all season long. While it may seem overwhelming at first, establishing a routine makes pool care manageable and even enjoyable. This guide breaks down everything you need to know.

Weekly Maintenance Schedule

Consistency is the foundation of good pool care. These tasks, performed weekly, prevent most pool problems before they start.

Daily Tasks

5-10 minutes
Skim surface debris (leaves, bugs, etc.)
Check water level (should be at mid-skimmer)
Empty skimmer baskets if half full
Visual equipment check (listen for unusual sounds)

2-3 Times Weekly

10-15 minutes
Test pH levels (target: 7.2-7.6)
Test chlorine levels (target: 1-3 ppm)
Adjust chemicals as needed
Check pump basket

Weekly Tasks

30-60 minutes
Brush walls, steps, and floor
Vacuum pool (or run automatic cleaner)
Clean waterline tiles/ring
Test alkalinity and calcium hardness
Check filter pressure gauge

Monthly and Seasonal Tasks

Task Frequency Why It Matters
Deep clean filter (backwash or rinse) Monthly or when pressure rises 8-10 PSI Maintains proper water circulation
Shock the pool Every 1-2 weeks, or after heavy use Kills bacteria and removes contaminants
Check and clean salt cell (if applicable) Every 3 months Ensures proper chlorine generation
Inspect pool equipment thoroughly Monthly Catches problems before they become expensive

Brushing: The Most Overlooked Task

Brushing your pool may seem unnecessary when the water looks clear, but it is one of the most important maintenance tasks. Here is why:

  • Prevents algae: Brushing disrupts algae before it can establish and become visible
  • Improves circulation: Loosens debris that has settled so it can be filtered out
  • Enhances chemical effectiveness: Allows sanitizer to reach all surfaces
  • Maintains surface: Prevents buildup that can stain or damage the pool finish

Brushing Technique

Use overlapping strokes and brush toward the main drain so debris can be filtered. Pay extra attention to corners, steps, behind ladders, and areas with poor circulation. Brush in one direction, not back and forth.

Equipment Care and Maintenance

Pool Pump

Run 8-12 hours daily during swim season. Clean strainer basket weekly. Listen for unusual noises that indicate bearing wear. Typical lifespan: 8-12 years.

Filter

Backwash sand/DE when pressure rises 8-10 PSI above clean baseline. Replace sand every 5-7 years. Replace cartridges every 1-2 years.

Heater

Professional inspection annually recommended. Keep area around heater clear for airflow. Check for error codes. Typical lifespan: 10-15 years.

Salt Cell

Inspect for scale buildup every 3 months. Clean with diluted muriatic acid solution if needed. Typical lifespan: 3-7 years.

Common Maintenance Mistakes

  1. Inconsistent testing: Test water 2-3 times weekly, not just when it looks bad
  2. Adding chemicals during the day: Add chemicals in the evening when UV will not immediately degrade chlorine
  3. Ignoring alkalinity: Total alkalinity stabilizes pH; always balance alkalinity first
  4. Running pump too little: Water should circulate completely at least once daily (8-12 hours)
  5. Neglecting the filter: A dirty filter cannot clean your water effectively
  6. Waiting for problems: Reactive maintenance costs more than preventive care

Chemical Safety

Never mix pool chemicals. Store chemicals in a cool, dry place away from each other. Always add chemicals to water, never water to chemicals. Wear protective equipment when handling chemicals.

When to Call a Professional

While regular maintenance is manageable for most pool owners, some situations require professional help:

  • Persistent algae that does not respond to treatment
  • Equipment making unusual noises or not functioning properly
  • Water chemistry that will not balance despite efforts
  • Visible leaks or unexplained water loss
  • Heater not heating or showing error codes
  • Electrical issues or concerns
  • Opening and closing the pool for the season
