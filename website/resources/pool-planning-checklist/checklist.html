Backyard Evaluation Guide

Assess your property to determine the best pool location and identify potential challenges.

  • Available Space Measured

    Minimum recommended: 15' x 30' for pool plus deck. Measure your usable area. ______ ft x ______ ft

  • Setback Requirements Verified

    Check local codes for required distance from property lines, house, and septic (if applicable).

  • Underground Utilities Marked

    Call 811 to have gas, electric, water, and sewer lines marked before any excavation.

  • Equipment Access Route

    Excavator needs 8-10' wide access. Check for gates, fences, or slopes that may block entry.

  • Sun Exposure Analyzed

    Ideal: 6+ hours of direct sunlight. Note: Trees to south/west provide afternoon shade.

  • Drainage Assessment

    Water should drain AWAY from pool. Identify low spots and existing drainage patterns.

  • Soil Conditions Considered

    Rocky soil, high water table, or clay can increase costs. Ask neighbors about their experiences.

  • View & Privacy Evaluated

    Consider sightlines from house and neighbors. Plan for screening if needed.

  • Electrical Panel Capacity

    Pool equipment typically requires 50-60 amp service. Verify your panel has capacity.

  • Water Source Confirmed

    Hose bib nearby? Well water may need treatment. Initial fill: 15,000-25,000 gallons.

Questions to Ask Pool Builders

Essential questions to help you choose the right contractor. Don't sign until you have answers to all of these.

  1. How long have you been in business? Look for 10+ years of experience.
  2. Are you licensed and insured? Verify PA HIC license and liability coverage ($1M+).
  3. Can I see your portfolio? Ask for photos of pools similar to what you want.
  4. Can I speak with recent customers? Get 3-5 references from the past 2 years.
  5. Who will manage my project day-to-day? Get a name and direct contact info.
  6. What's included in your quote? Get itemized list; beware vague "pool packages."
  7. What's NOT included? Electrical, permits, landscaping, fencing?
  8. What warranties do you offer? Shell, equipment, workmanship - get it in writing.
  9. What happens if you find rock during excavation? Get a per-yard or flat-rate quote.
  10. What's your payment schedule? Never pay more than 10% upfront.
  11. Will you handle permits? Most reputable builders do this for you.
  12. What's your realistic timeline? Get start date and completion date in contract.
  13. Do you use subcontractors? If so, are they also licensed and insured?
  14. What pool brands do you install? Research the manufacturers they use.
  15. How do you handle change orders? Get process in writing before signing.
  16. What equipment brands do you recommend? Pentair, Hayward, and Jandy are top tier.
  17. Will you provide pool school? Training on maintenance and equipment operation.
  18. What's your callback policy? How do you handle issues after project completion?
  19. Do you offer maintenance services? Many builders offer ongoing care packages.
  20. What sets you apart from competitors? Listen for specifics, not marketing speak.

Permit Requirements by Area

Local permit requirements vary. This is general guidance - always verify with your specific township.

Chester County, PA

  • Building permit required
  • Zoning approval needed
  • Fence: 4' min, self-closing gate
  • Setback: Usually 10' from property line
  • Electrical permit separate

Delaware County, PA

  • Building permit required
  • Plot plan submission
  • Fence: 4' min with locking gate
  • Setback: Varies by township
  • UCC compliance inspection

Montgomery County, PA

  • Building permit required
  • Stormwater management may apply
  • Fence: 4' min, per township code
  • Survey may be required
  • Final inspection mandatory

New Castle County, DE

  • Building permit required
  • Site plan with pool location
  • Barrier: 48" min height
  • Setback: 6' from property line
  • Electrical inspection required

  • Verify current requirements with your township

    Codes change. Call your local building department before finalizing plans.

  • Check HOA restrictions (if applicable)

    Some HOAs have additional requirements or approvals needed.

  • Confirm builder will handle permit process

    Most reputable builders manage permits as part of their service.
