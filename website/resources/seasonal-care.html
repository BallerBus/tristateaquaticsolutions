Seasonal Pool Care Guide

Protect your pool investment with proper seasonal care. Step-by-step guides for opening in spring, mid-season maintenance, and winterizing for Pennsylvania and Delaware climates.

Pennsylvania and Delaware's climate presents unique challenges for pool owners. With cold winters and the freeze-thaw cycle, proper seasonal care is essential to protect your investment and ensure your pool is ready to enjoy when warm weather arrives.

Pennsylvania/Delaware Pool Season Calendar

Late March - April
Schedule Opening

Book your pool opening early. Popular weekends fill up fast.

April - May
Pool Opening

Once overnight temps stay above 50F consistently, it is time to open.

May - September
Swim Season

Regular maintenance, weekly testing, and enjoying your pool.

September - October
Pool Closing

Close before temps drop below 65F consistently to prevent algae issues.

October - March
Winter Monitoring

Check cover monthly, remove debris and standing water.

Spring Pool Opening Guide

Opening your pool properly sets the stage for the entire season. Take your time and do it right to avoid problems later.

When to Open

Open your pool when overnight temperatures consistently stay above 50F. Opening too early wastes chemicals and energy; opening too late allows algae to establish under warm covers. In PA/DE, this is typically mid-April to early May.

Pool Opening Steps

1

Remove and Clean the Cover

Use a cover pump to remove standing water and a leaf net to remove debris. Do NOT let debris fall into the pool when removing the cover. Clean the cover with cover cleaner or mild soap, let it dry completely, then fold and store in a dry location.

2

Remove Winterizing Plugs and Accessories

Remove plugs from return lines and skimmer. Take out any ice compensator (Gizzmo) from skimmer. Reinstall return eyeballs/fittings and skimmer baskets. Remove any plugs from the equipment pad.

3

Reconnect Equipment

Reinstall pump drain plugs. Reconnect any disconnected plumbing. Open all valves (filter, heater bypass, etc.). Inspect equipment for visible damage, cracks, or wear from winter. Lubricate o-rings as needed.

4

Fill Pool to Proper Level

Add water until the level reaches the middle of the skimmer opening. This may take several hours depending on your water source. Never leave a filling pool unattended.

5

Prime and Start the Pump

Fill pump basket with water if needed to prime. Turn on the pump and check for proper flow. Listen for unusual sounds. Check for leaks at all connections. Verify water is flowing from returns.

6

Clean the Pool

Remove large debris with a leaf net. Brush all walls, steps, and floor thoroughly. Vacuum the pool (may need to vacuum to waste if very dirty). Clean the waterline.

7

Test and Balance Water Chemistry

Test all chemical levels. Balance in this order: (1) Total Alkalinity 80-120 ppm, (2) pH 7.2-7.6, (3) Calcium Hardness 200-400 ppm, (4) Chlorine 1-3 ppm. Add shock treatment to kill any bacteria or algae. Run pump 24 hours initially.

Opening Day Checklist

  • Cover removed, cleaned, dried, and stored
  • All winterizing plugs removed
  • Equipment reconnected and inspected
  • Water level at mid-skimmer
  • Pump running with proper flow
  • No visible leaks
  • Pool brushed and vacuumed
  • Water tested and chemicals balanced
  • Shock treatment added
  • Safety equipment in place (alarms, fence gates working)

Fall Pool Closing Guide

Proper winterization protects your pool from freeze damage and makes spring opening easier. Never rush this process.

When to Close

Close your pool when water temperatures drop consistently below 65F. Closing too early while water is still warm creates ideal conditions for algae to grow under the cover. In PA/DE, this is typically late September to mid-October.

Pool Closing Steps

1

Balance Water Chemistry

Test and adjust all chemical levels before closing. Target: pH 7.2-7.6, Alkalinity 80-120 ppm, Calcium Hardness 200-400 ppm. Balanced water prevents scaling and corrosion during the long off-season.

2

Deep Clean the Pool

Thoroughly brush walls, steps, and floor. Vacuum the entire pool. Clean the waterline. Remove all debris. A clean pool stays clean under the cover.

3

Lower Water Level

For mesh safety covers: Lower water 4-6 inches below the skimmer. For solid covers: Lower water 12-18 inches below the coping. For automatic covers: Follow manufacturer guidelines (often no lowering needed).

4

Blow Out Plumbing Lines

This is critical for freeze protection. Use a shop vac or air compressor to blow water from all plumbing lines - skimmers, returns, main drain, and equipment. Install winterizing plugs in all openings. Add pool antifreeze to lines as extra protection.

5

Winterize Equipment

Remove pump and filter drain plugs - store plugs in pump basket. Drain heater and remove drain plugs. Remove and store chlorinator. Turn off power to equipment at the breaker.

6

Add Winter Chemicals

Add winter algaecide according to pool size. Add enzyme product to break down organic debris. Shock the pool with extra chlorine (2-3x normal dose).

7

Install Winter Cover

Install safety cover with all springs properly tensioned. Or install solid cover with water bags securing edges. Ensure cover is taut with no sagging areas that collect water and debris.

Freeze Damage Warning

Water expands when it freezes. Any water left in plumbing, equipment, or fittings can crack pipes, pumps, filters, and heaters. The cost of freeze damage repair often exceeds $5,000-$10,000. Proper line blowing is the most important step in winterization.

Winter Monitoring

Your pool still needs attention during the off-season. Check it monthly:

  • Remove debris, leaves, and standing water from the cover
  • Check cover tension and anchors after storms
  • Ensure water bags are full and not cracked (solid covers)
  • Verify water level has not dropped significantly (could indicate a leak)
  • After heavy snow, carefully remove excess weight from cover

Professional vs. DIY

While experienced pool owners can handle opening and closing, there are advantages to professional service:

Advantages of Professional Service

  • Proper equipment for blowing lines (prevents freeze damage)
  • Trained eye spots potential problems
  • Saves you 4-6 hours of work each season
  • Correct chemical balancing from the start
  • Cover handling (safety covers are heavy and awkward)

When DIY Makes Sense

  • You have the right equipment (air compressor, etc.)
  • You are comfortable with pool chemistry
  • Your pool has a simple plumbing layout
  • You enjoy hands-on pool care
