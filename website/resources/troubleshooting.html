Water Quality Problems

Green Pool Water

Urgent - Act immediately

Cause

Algae growth due to low chlorine levels, unbalanced pH, inadequate filtration, or warm temperatures. Green water means algae has taken hold and is actively multiplying.

Solution Steps

  1. Test and balance pH - Lower pH to 7.2 for maximum chlorine effectiveness. Alkalinity should be 80-100 ppm.
  2. Brush all surfaces - Thoroughly brush walls, floor, steps, and corners to break up algae colonies.
  3. Triple or quadruple shock - Add 3-4 pounds of shock per 10,000 gallons. For severe cases, even more may be needed.
  4. Run filter 24/7 - Keep pump running continuously until water clears.
  5. Backwash frequently - Clean filter every 8-12 hours as it collects dead algae.
  6. Brush again - Continue brushing daily until clear.
  7. Add algaecide - Once chlorine drops below 5 ppm, add algaecide to prevent recurrence.

Timeline

Light green: 1-3 days to clear. Dark green: 3-5 days. Black/dark green: May require partial drain and refill, professional treatment, or acid wash.

Cloudy Pool Water

Moderate - Address within 1-2 days

Common Causes

  • High pH or alkalinity
  • Inadequate filtration (pump not running enough)
  • Low chlorine levels
  • High calcium hardness
  • Early stage algae (before green is visible)
  • High total dissolved solids (TDS)
  • Dirty or exhausted filter

Solution Steps

  1. Test all chemistry - Check pH, alkalinity, chlorine, and calcium hardness.
  2. Balance pH and alkalinity first - Target pH 7.2-7.4, alkalinity 80-100 ppm.
  3. Shock the pool - Even if chlorine seems okay, shocking helps oxidize contaminants.
  4. Clean or backwash filter - A dirty filter cannot clear water.
  5. Run filter longer - 12-24 hours daily until clear.
  6. Add clarifier - Pool clarifier helps clump tiny particles so filter can catch them.

Still Cloudy?

If water remains cloudy after 48 hours with proper chemistry and filtration, consider: filter media may need replacement, high calcium causing precipitation, or very high TDS requiring partial drain and refill.

Strong Chlorine Smell

Moderate - Indicates chemical imbalance

The Truth About "Chlorine Smell"

Contrary to popular belief, a strong chlorine smell means you need MORE chlorine, not less. The smell comes from chloramines (combined chlorine) - chlorine that has reacted with sweat, urine, oils, and other contaminants. It is no longer sanitizing and is irritating to eyes and skin.

Solution

  1. Test combined chlorine - (Total chlorine minus free chlorine). Should be below 0.5 ppm.
  2. Shock the pool - Breakpoint chlorination destroys chloramines. Use enough shock to reach 10x your combined chlorine reading.
  3. Maintain proper free chlorine - Keep at 1-3 ppm consistently.
  4. Improve circulation - Poor circulation allows chloramines to build up.

Eye and Skin Irritation

Moderate - Indicates imbalanced water

Common Causes

  • pH out of range - The #1 cause. Human eyes are pH 7.4; pool should be 7.2-7.6.
  • High chloramines - Combined chlorine irritates mucous membranes.
  • Low alkalinity - Causes pH to swing, creating irritation.

Solution

  1. Test pH first - Adjust to 7.2-7.6 range.
  2. Check alkalinity - Should be 80-120 ppm to stabilize pH.
  3. Test combined chlorine - Shock if above 0.5 ppm.

Equipment Problems

Pump Problems

Pump Not Priming / Losing Prime

A pump that will not prime or keeps losing prime usually has an air leak on the suction side.

Symptom Check This
Pump basket not filling Water level too low, clogged skimmer, suction leak before pump
Air bubbles in pump basket Pump lid o-ring, fittings before pump, drain plugs
Air bubbles from returns Suction-side air leak - check all connections before pump
Pump runs but no water moves Clogged impeller, closed valve, very low water level

Pump Making Noise

  • Grinding sound: Worn bearings - pump needs service/replacement
  • Screeching: Motor bearings failing - replace motor or pump
  • Gurgling: Air being pulled into system - check for leaks
  • Humming but not starting: Capacitor failed, motor seized, or electrical issue

Pump Safety

Always turn off power at the breaker before working on pump. Never run pump dry - this destroys the seal in minutes. If pump is hot to touch, turn off and let cool before investigating.

Filter Problems

High Filter Pressure

Filter pressure rising 8-10 PSI above clean starting pressure indicates a dirty filter.

  • Sand filter: Backwash until sight glass runs clear
  • DE filter: Backwash and add fresh DE
  • Cartridge filter: Remove and rinse, or replace cartridges

Low Filter Pressure

  • Clogged pump basket or skimmer basket
  • Clogged or closed valve before pump
  • Air leak reducing pump efficiency
  • Worn pump impeller

Filter Returning Debris to Pool

  • Sand filter: Broken lateral, channeling in sand, or sand needs replacement
  • DE filter: Torn grid fabric, cracked manifold
  • Cartridge: Torn or worn cartridge element

Pool Staining

Identifying Stain Types

Stain Color Likely Cause Treatment
Green/Brown Organic (leaves, algae) Shock treatment, brush, enzyme cleaner
Blue/Green/Black Copper (from heater or algaecides) Metal sequestrant, ascorbic acid treatment
Reddish-Brown Iron (from well water or equipment) Ascorbic acid treatment, metal sequestrant
Purple/Black Manganese (from fill water) Ascorbic acid, metal sequestrant
White Scale Calcium buildup Lower pH and alkalinity, scale remover

Vitamin C Test

To identify metal stains: Hold a vitamin C tablet against the stain for 30 seconds. If it lightens, the stain is metal-based and will respond to ascorbic acid treatment.

Foam on Water Surface

Minor - Usually easy fix

Common Causes

  • Body oils, lotions, and cosmetics
  • Laundry detergent residue on swimsuits
  • Cheap or overused algaecides
  • High TDS (total dissolved solids)
  • Low calcium hardness making water "soft"

Solution

  1. Shower before swimming - Removes oils and lotions
  2. Rinse swimsuits - Remove detergent residue
  3. Add enzyme product - Breaks down organic contaminants
  4. Use quality algaecide - Avoid cheap, foaming formulas
  5. Partial drain/refill - If TDS is very high

When to Call a Professional

While many problems are DIY-solvable, some situations warrant professional help:

  • Black algae (requires aggressive treatment, possibly acid wash)
  • Persistent green water that does not respond to multiple shock treatments
  • Equipment making unusual noises or overheating
  • Electrical issues (tripped breakers, shocking sensations)
  • Major leaks or water loss
  • Heater not heating or showing error codes
  • Recurring problems despite proper treatment
