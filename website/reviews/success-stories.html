The Martinez Family: From Dream to Backyard Paradise

Gladwyne, PA
Installed Summer 2025
16x36 Fiberglass Pool

Jennifer and Miguel Martinez had been dreaming of a backyard pool for nearly a decade. With three active children and a love for entertaining, their Gladwyne property seemed perfect for a pool - if only they could find the right builder to make it happen.

The Beginning: Years of Consideration

For Jennifer and Miguel Martinez, the idea of a pool had been simmering for nearly ten years. Like many Main Line families, they had initially dismissed it as too expensive, too complicated, or simply not the right time. Their children were young, their careers were demanding, and there was always something more pressing to address.

"We'd drive past houses with pools and the kids would always point them out," Jennifer recalls. "Every summer, we'd talk about it, but it never seemed like the right moment. Then the pandemic hit, and suddenly we were home all the time. The kids were going stir-crazy, and we realized we'd been putting off something that could genuinely improve our quality of life."

The Martinez family started researching pool options in earnest during the spring of 2024. They quickly became overwhelmed by the choices: vinyl liner, fiberglass, concrete. Above-ground, in-ground. Saltwater, chlorine. The options seemed endless, and every contractor they spoke with seemed to push a different solution.

Finding the Right Partner

After interviewing six different pool companies, the Martinezes were growing frustrated. Some contractors barely listened to their needs before launching into sales pitches. Others quoted prices that seemed impossibly high or suspiciously low. One company couldn't even give them a timeline for completion.

"When we met with Tri-State Aquatic Solutions, it was immediately different. They actually asked us questions about how we wanted to use the pool, who would be swimming in it, what our maintenance tolerance was. It wasn't just about selling us something - they wanted to understand our family."

- Jennifer Martinez

The initial consultation lasted nearly two hours. The design team walked their property, discussed the natural slope of the yard, talked about how the afternoon sun would hit different areas, and asked about their landscaping plans. By the end, the Martinezes felt like they were talking to experts who genuinely cared about getting it right.

Design and Planning

The design process took about three weeks. Tri-State's team created 3D renderings that allowed the family to visualize exactly how the pool would look in their backyard. They made several adjustments based on feedback - moving the pool slightly to preserve a mature oak tree, adjusting the depth profile to accommodate both lap swimming and children's play, and incorporating a tanning ledge that Jennifer had seen in a magazine.

The permit process, which the Martinezes had dreaded, was handled entirely by Tri-State. Lower Merion Township requires extensive documentation for pool installations, including site plans, fence specifications, and electrical layouts. Tri-State's team had been through this process dozens of times and knew exactly what the township needed.

"We literally never had to go to the township office," Miguel says. "They handled everything. We just signed where we needed to sign, and before we knew it, we had our permits."

Installation Day

The installation began on a Monday morning in late May. The crew arrived at 7 AM with all the equipment needed for excavation. By the time the Martinez children got home from school that afternoon, there was already a massive hole in their backyard where a pool would soon be.

"The kids were amazed," Jennifer laughs. "They wanted to sit outside and watch everything. We had to practically drag them inside for dinner."

Over the next twelve days, the transformation was remarkable. The fiberglass shell arrived on a flatbed truck - a moment that drew the neighbors out to watch. The crew carefully lowered the one-piece shell into the excavation, then began the precise work of leveling, backfilling, and connecting plumbing and electrical systems.

The First Swim

The pool was filled on a Friday afternoon. The Tri-State team stayed to walk the family through all the equipment, teach them about the saltwater system, and ensure everything was working perfectly. By Saturday morning, the water had reached the ideal temperature, and the Martinez family took their first swim.

"I'll never forget that moment," Jennifer says, her eyes brightening at the memory. "The kids just ran straight into the water. They didn't even wait for us. We stood there for a moment, watching them splash around, and I remember looking at Miguel and thinking, 'Why did we wait so long?'"

Life After the Pool

That first summer, the Martinez pool became the neighborhood gathering spot. Friends who they used to see once a month were suddenly stopping by every weekend. Their children's friends became regular fixtures, and the family found themselves hosting more than they ever had before.

The health benefits surprised them too. Miguel, who had been meaning to start exercising more, found himself swimming laps before work most mornings. The kids became confident swimmers, and the whole family spent more time outside and less time on screens.

"This pool didn't just change our backyard. It changed our lifestyle. We're healthier, we're more connected as a family, and we've built relationships with neighbors we barely knew before. It's easily the best investment we've ever made in our home."

- Miguel Martinez

A year later, the Martinezes are still in love with their pool. The low maintenance of the fiberglass has been exactly as promised - they spend about 15 minutes a week on upkeep, and the saltwater system keeps the water crystal clear with minimal chemical handling. They've already referred three neighbors to Tri-State Aquatic Solutions.

"If you're on the fence about getting a pool, stop waiting," Jennifer advises. "Find a builder you trust, and just do it. You won't regret it."

The Chen Family: Making the Most of a Compact Space

Hockessin, DE
Installed Spring 2025
10x20 Plunge Pool

David and Lynn Chen loved their cozy Hockessin home, but the modest backyard had always seemed too small for a pool. That is, until they discovered the elegant solution of a plunge pool.

The Challenge of a Small Yard

When David and Lynn Chen purchased their Hockessin home fifteen years ago, they fell in love with its character and charm. The 1960s colonial had been lovingly maintained, with beautiful original hardwood floors and a cozy fireplace in the living room. The only drawback was the backyard - charming, but compact at just over 2,000 square feet.

"We'd looked at larger properties over the years," Lynn admits, "but we loved this neighborhood too much to leave. Our kids grew up here, our friends are here, and the location is perfect for both our commutes. So we learned to work with the space we had."

For years, the Chens assumed a pool was simply not an option. They'd visited friends with pools and enjoyed the experience, but conventional wisdom said their yard was too small. Then, during a trip to a boutique hotel in Arizona, they encountered their first plunge pool.

Discovering the Plunge Pool Concept

"It was this gorgeous little pool, maybe 10 feet by 15 feet," David recalls. "Perfectly designed with a built-in bench and just enough depth for cooling off and relaxation. It was like a private spa. And I remember thinking, 'We could fit something like this in our backyard.'"

Back home in Delaware, they started researching. Plunge pools were gaining popularity on the East Coast, particularly among homeowners with smaller properties or those who wanted a pool primarily for relaxation rather than lap swimming. The smaller footprint meant lower installation costs, reduced maintenance, and faster heating.

"When we called Tri-State and described our situation, they were immediately enthusiastic. They didn't try to talk us into something bigger than we needed. They understood the plunge pool concept and saw the potential in our space right away."

- David Chen

The design consultation was eye-opening. Tri-State's team showed them how a 10x20 plunge pool could be positioned to take advantage of the afternoon sun while preserving the mature landscaping Lynn had cultivated over the years. They proposed a depth of 5.5 feet throughout - deep enough for complete immersion but shallow enough to feel secure.

Working Around Existing Landscaping

One of Lynn's primary concerns was her garden. She'd spent years cultivating a beautiful perennial border along the back fence, with roses, hydrangeas, and ornamental grasses that bloomed in succession throughout the growing season. A traditional pool installation would have destroyed most of it.

Tri-State's approach was different. They proposed accessing the backyard through a side gate, using compact equipment designed for tight spaces. Where larger excavators might need to clear a wide path, they used mini excavators and hand-dug in areas near sensitive plantings. It took longer, but it preserved Lynn's garden almost entirely.

"They lost maybe two hostas and a single ornamental grass," Lynn says. "I couldn't believe it. I'd prepared myself to replant most of the border, and instead it looks virtually untouched. That attention to detail told me we'd chosen the right company."

The Finished Product

The completed plunge pool exceeded the Chens' expectations. The deep blue finish contrasts beautifully with the natural stone coping and complements the surrounding garden. A built-in bench spans one end, providing comfortable seating for two. LED lighting creates ambiance for evening use, and the pool heater ensures comfortable temperatures from April through October.

The Chens installed a small pool house - really just an 8x10 shed - that holds the equipment and provides storage for pool supplies. This keeps the equipment noise away from the pool itself and maintains the peaceful atmosphere they wanted to create.

A New Daily Ritual

Since the pool's completion, the Chens have developed new routines. David swims every morning before work, using the pool for exercise despite its compact size. "I do resistance exercises, holding the edge and kicking," he explains. "It's not lap swimming, but it's a great workout."

Lynn uses the pool primarily in the evenings. After dinner, she'll often spend thirty minutes floating and decompressing from the workday. "It's become my meditation practice," she says. "There's something about being in the water, looking up at the sky, that just clears my mind."

"Everyone who visits asks about the pool. They're amazed at how elegant it looks and how well it fits the space. Several friends have already started calling Tri-State to ask about plunge pools for their own homes."

- Lynn Chen

The Chens' experience proves that you don't need a massive backyard to enjoy pool ownership. With creative design and a builder who understands compact spaces, even the most modest yards can accommodate a beautiful and functional water feature. Their plunge pool has transformed not just their outdoor space but their daily lives.

The Williams Family: A Multi-Generational Gathering Place

Villanova, PA
Installed Summer 2024
16x34 Fiberglass + Spa

For Robert and Patricia Williams, the pool wasn't just about their own enjoyment. As empty nesters with five grandchildren, they wanted to create a destination that would draw their extended family together for years to come.

Creating a Family Legacy

Robert and Patricia Williams raised their three children in their Villanova home. The large colonial on two acres had been the site of countless birthday parties, graduation celebrations, and holiday gatherings. But as the children grew up and started families of their own, the gatherings became less frequent. Everyone was busy with their own lives, and the commute to the Main Line seemed longer each year.

"We'd see the grandchildren maybe once a month," Patricia recalls. "And it was usually for just a few hours - a Sunday dinner or a holiday visit. We wanted something that would make them excited to come over, something that would turn a quick visit into a full day of fun."

The idea for a pool emerged from a simple observation. The grandchildren, ranging in age from four to twelve, loved nothing more than water. Beach trips, water parks, sprinklers in the backyard - anything involving water captured their attention for hours. A pool seemed like the obvious solution.

Designing for All Ages

The Williams' requirements were more complex than a typical family pool. They needed a design that would be safe for young children while still engaging for pre-teens. They wanted a spa where adults could relax while keeping an eye on the swimmers. And they needed plenty of deck space for the inevitable crowd that would gather.

Tri-State's design team rose to the challenge. They proposed a 16x34 fiberglass pool with a graduated entry - a gentle slope that allowed the youngest grandchildren to wade safely in just a few inches of water while older kids could play in the deeper sections. A separate spa with a spillover feature created a visual and auditory connection to the main pool while providing a dedicated space for adults.

"The design team spent hours with us, walking through exactly how different family members would use the space. They thought about sight lines so we could always see the kids, placement of the spa so we could relax without feeling isolated, even where to position umbrella stands for shade. It was incredibly thorough."

- Robert Williams

The deck design was equally thoughtful. Tri-State recommended an expansive stamped concrete surround with multiple seating areas. A pergola-covered dining area would allow the family to eat outside even in full sun, while a dedicated children's zone near the shallow entry included built-in benches at kid-friendly heights.

The Installation Process

Given the scope of the project, installation took about four weeks. The Williams watched the transformation unfold from their breakfast room windows, which overlooked the backyard. Each day brought visible progress: excavation, shell placement, concrete work, landscaping.

"We took pictures every day," Patricia laughs. "We have this complete photo album of the installation. The grandchildren love looking through it - they can't believe there was ever just grass where the pool is now."

The crew faced one unexpected challenge: an underground boulder field that added several days to the excavation. Tri-State handled the situation professionally, bringing in specialized equipment and adjusting the timeline without additional charges. The Williams never felt pressured or stressed about the delay.

A New Tradition

The pool opened just in time for the Fourth of July, and the Williams hosted their first pool party for the entire extended family. Twenty-three people gathered in their backyard - children in the pool, adults in the spa, everyone else scattered across the expansive deck. It was exactly the scene they had envisioned.

"I remember looking around at everyone that day," Robert says, his voice catching slightly. "All three of our kids, their spouses, all five grandchildren, plus some neighbors and friends. The kids were laughing and splashing, the adults were catching up, and I just felt this tremendous sense of... purpose. This is what we built the pool for."

That Fourth of July gathering has become an annual tradition, but it's far from the only time the family gathers. Throughout the summer, the Williams' pool has become the default location for family time. Their children bring the grandchildren over nearly every weekend, often staying for dinner and sometimes sleeping over. The pool has done exactly what Robert and Patricia hoped: it's drawn the family together.

"We see our grandchildren now more in one summer than we used to see them in two years. They ask to come to Grandma and Grandpa's house. They prefer it to camps and playdates. That's worth more than any money we spent on this pool."

- Patricia Williams

Year-Round Enjoyment

The Williams didn't limit their pool enjoyment to summer. The heated spa operates year-round, and it's become Robert's favorite spot for morning coffee and the newspaper. Patricia uses it after her evening walks, finding the warm water helps with her occasional joint stiffness. They've even hosted Christmas morning in the spa, with the grandchildren running between the warm house and the steaming water.

Looking back, the Williams have no regrets about their investment. The pool has transformed their retirement, giving them a reason to stay active in maintaining it and a powerful draw for family gatherings. Their only wish? That they had done it sooner.

The Peterson Family: Overcoming a Challenging Site

Ardmore, PA
Installed Fall 2024
Custom Infinity Edge

When two other pool companies told Andrew and Elizabeth Peterson that their steeply sloped backyard made pool installation impossible, they refused to give up on their dream. Tri-State saw the challenge differently - as an opportunity for something extraordinary.

The "Impossible" Backyard

The Peterson's Ardmore property is stunning - a 1920s stone home perched on a hillside with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. But that hillside location came with a significant challenge: a backyard that dropped nearly fifteen feet from the house to the property line.

"Every contractor who came out would walk around, shake their head, and give us a polite version of 'this can't be done,'" Elizabeth recalls. "They'd talk about retaining walls and engineering challenges and costs that would be astronomical. After the second rejection, we started to think maybe a pool just wasn't in the cards for us."

Andrew wasn't ready to give up. A structural engineer by profession, he understood that challenging sites required creative solutions, not rejection. He researched pool companies known for complex installations and found Tri-State Aquatic Solutions, whose portfolio included several hillside projects.

Seeing Opportunity in Challenge

The initial site visit was different from the previous consultations. Instead of immediately cataloging problems, Tri-State's design team explored possibilities. They walked the entire slope, examined the soil composition, and studied the drainage patterns. Then they asked a question no one else had: "Have you ever considered an infinity edge pool?"

An infinity edge, they explained, could actually work with the slope rather than against it. Instead of fighting the grade change, they would embrace it. The pool would be positioned partway down the slope, with the infinity edge facing the view. A series of terraced retaining walls would create usable space both above and below the pool. The result would be a design that looked intentional - as if the house had been built with this exact pool in mind.

"When they showed us the 3D rendering, Elizabeth and I just looked at each other. It was more beautiful than anything we had imagined. The other contractors saw a problem. Tri-State saw an opportunity to create something extraordinary."

- Andrew Peterson

The engineering required was substantial. Tri-State brought in a geotechnical engineer to assess soil stability and design the retaining wall system. The plans went through multiple revisions to ensure structural integrity while maximizing aesthetic impact. The permit process took longer than typical due to the complexity, but Tri-State's team navigated every requirement.

The Construction Journey

Installation took eight weeks - longer than a standard pool, but necessary for the scope of work involved. The first phase focused on the retaining walls, with crews working carefully on the steep slope. Concrete was pumped rather than poured, and specialized equipment accessed the site via a temporary gravel road.

The pool itself was a custom gunite design, the only option that could be precisely shaped to fit the site's unique contours. The infinity edge required precision - the water level needed to flow evenly across the full length of the edge to create the visual effect of water disappearing into the view beyond.

"We watched the construction every day," Andrew admits. "It was fascinating from an engineering perspective. The crew was clearly experienced with complex projects - they anticipated problems before they arose and adapted smoothly when unexpected conditions appeared."

The Reveal

When the pool was finally completed and filled, the Petersons understood why Tri-State had been so confident. The infinity edge created a stunning visual effect - the water appearing to merge with the sky and the rolling hills beyond. The terraced deck provided multiple levels for lounging, dining, and entertaining. What had seemed like a liability - the steep slope - had become the property's greatest asset.

"Our neighbors were astonished," Elizabeth says. "People who had lived nearby for years and knew our yard was 'unusable' couldn't believe what we'd created. We've become the unofficial neighborhood attraction."

The pool has transformed not just the backyard but the Petersons' relationship with their home. Previously, they spent most of their time in the front of the house, where the grade was manageable. Now, the back of the house has become the heart of their living space, with the pool visible from the kitchen, dining room, and master bedroom.

"Every morning, I have coffee looking out at that pool and those views. Every evening, we swim as the sun sets behind the hills. This pool didn't just add value to our home - it fundamentally changed how we experience living here."

- Elizabeth Peterson

Advice for Others with Challenging Sites

The Petersons have become advocates for persistence. When friends mention challenging lots or contractor rejections, Andrew and Elizabeth encourage them to keep looking. "The right company sees challenges as opportunities," Andrew says. "If someone tells you it can't be done, they might just be telling you they can't do it. Find someone who can."

They also emphasize the importance of patience. Complex projects take time - time for proper engineering, time for careful construction, time for getting it right. The eight weeks of construction sometimes felt endless, but the result was worth every day of waiting.

The Richardson Family: Healing Through Water

Bryn Mawr, PA
Installed Spring 2024
12x24 Therapy Pool

For Thomas Richardson, a pool wasn't about luxury or leisure - it was about recovery. After a serious knee injury and subsequent surgery, aquatic therapy became essential to his rehabilitation. Building a private therapy pool changed everything.

An Unexpected Need

Thomas Richardson was 52 when he suffered a devastating knee injury during a recreational basketball game. What should have been a friendly weekend game ended with a torn ACL, MCL, and meniscus. Surgery was successful, but the orthopedic surgeon was clear: full recovery would require extensive physical therapy, and aquatic therapy would be essential.

"The surgeon explained that water-based exercises would allow me to rebuild strength without putting stress on the healing joint," Thomas recalls. "For the first six months, most of my PT would need to happen in a pool."

The challenge was access. The nearest pool that offered aquatic therapy sessions was a 25-minute drive each way. With three weekly sessions scheduled, Thomas was looking at hours of travel time on top of the therapy itself. For a busy attorney still maintaining a partial work schedule during recovery, this was unsustainable.

A Therapeutic Solution

It was Thomas's wife, Karen, who first suggested building their own pool. Initially, Thomas dismissed the idea - they didn't need a full swimming pool, just access to warm water for exercises. But as they researched, they discovered that a purpose-built therapy pool could be exactly what Thomas needed, at a size and cost far smaller than a traditional swimming pool.

Tri-State Aquatic Solutions had experience with therapy pools, having installed several for clients with mobility challenges or chronic conditions requiring aquatic exercise. They met with Thomas and his physical therapist to understand the specific requirements: uniform depth of 4 feet, water temperature maintained at 88-92 degrees, grip bars along the edges, and a bench for seated exercises.

"Most pool companies looked at us like we were crazy when we described what we needed. Tri-State immediately understood. They'd done this before, and they knew exactly how to design a pool optimized for therapy rather than recreation."

- Karen Richardson

The design was compact but perfectly suited to its purpose. At 12x24 feet with a consistent 4-foot depth, the pool provided enough space for walking, resistance exercises, and range-of-motion work. A powerful heater maintained the elevated temperature needed for therapeutic benefit, and a cover system helped retain heat when the pool wasn't in use.

The Recovery Journey

The pool was completed just two months after Thomas's surgery, allowing him to begin home-based aquatic therapy earlier than anticipated. His physical therapist visited twice weekly to guide sessions, while Thomas supplemented with prescribed exercises on his own.

"Having the pool at home transformed my recovery," Thomas explains. "Instead of driving to appointments, I could do a therapy session before breakfast. Instead of being limited to scheduled times, I could work in extra exercises whenever I felt up to it. The convenience wasn't just about time - it removed every barrier to doing the work."

The warm water provided immediate relief from pain and stiffness. Thomas found himself using the pool not just for prescribed exercises but for general movement throughout the day. A 15-minute session before bed became part of his routine, helping him sleep better and wake with less stiffness.

Beyond Recovery

Six months after surgery, Thomas's orthopedic surgeon was amazed at his progress. The knee had healed completely, range of motion was fully restored, and Thomas was cleared to resume all normal activities. The surgeon credited the intensive aquatic therapy - and the consistency that home access had made possible.

But the pool's usefulness didn't end with Thomas's recovery. Karen, who had developed mild arthritis in her hands and wrists, found that regular soaking in the warm water helped with her symptoms. The pool became part of her daily wellness routine, providing relief that no medication had quite matched.

"We thought we were building this pool for Thomas's knee," Karen says. "Now we realize it's become essential to both our health. The warm water helps my hands, helps Thomas's back when it flares up, helps both of us sleep better. It's become our most-used home investment."

"A gym membership would cost a few hundred dollars a year. This pool cost considerably more. But it's paid for itself many times over in reduced medical costs, avoided therapy appointments, and improved quality of life. Not everything valuable can be measured in dollars."

- Thomas Richardson

A Resource for Others

Thomas has become an advocate for therapeutic pool ownership, particularly for those facing significant rehabilitation challenges. He's connected with several patients through his orthopedic surgeon's office, sharing his experience and offering advice on what to look for in a therapy pool design.

"If you're facing a long recovery that will require aquatic therapy, consider the math," he advises. "Add up the travel time, the scheduling constraints, the co-pays for each session. Compare that to the cost of a purpose-built pool. For many people, especially those with chronic conditions requiring ongoing water therapy, a home pool makes both medical and financial sense."

The Richardson's story illustrates that pools aren't just about summer fun and backyard entertaining. For some families, they're essential medical equipment - a tool for healing, recovery, and ongoing wellness. Tri-State's ability to design and build for this specific use case made an enormous difference in Thomas's recovery and the family's long-term health.
