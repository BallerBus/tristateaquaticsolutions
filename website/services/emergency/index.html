Emergency Services

We Handle Pool Emergencies

When your pool has a critical problem, we respond quickly with the expertise to resolve it. Here are the emergencies we handle.

Pump Failures

Complete pump failure, motor burnout, or circulation loss. We carry common replacement parts and can often restore operation same-day.

Major Leaks

Rapid water loss, visible cracks, or underground pipe breaks. We locate and repair leaks to prevent property damage and water waste.

Electrical Issues

Tripped breakers, GFCI failures, or electrical hazards near the pool. Safety first - we diagnose and repair electrical problems to code.

Green Pool Rescue

Severely algae-contaminated water that is unsafe or unsightly. We shock treat, filter, and restore water clarity, typically within 24-72 hours.

Equipment Malfunctions

Heater failures, filter breakdowns, automation system crashes, or salt cell issues. We troubleshoot and repair all major equipment brands.

Storm Damage

Debris in pool, damaged covers, displaced equipment, or flooding from severe weather. We clean up and restore your pool after storms.

Safety First

While Waiting for Service

Follow these steps to stay safe and minimize damage until our technician arrives.

  • Turn Off Equipment

    If safe to do so, turn off all pool equipment at the main breaker. This prevents further damage and electrical hazards.

  • Keep Everyone Away

    Keep people and pets away from the pool area, especially with electrical issues or if water quality is compromised.

  • Document the Damage

    Take photos or video of visible damage. This helps with diagnosis and may be needed for insurance claims.

  • Do Not Attempt Repairs

    Avoid DIY fixes, especially for electrical or structural issues. Improper repairs can make problems worse or void warranties.

Electrical Emergencies

If you notice sparking, burning smells, or any electrical hazard near your pool, turn off power at the main panel immediately and do not enter the pool area until a professional has inspected it.

Common Questions

Emergency Service FAQ

Answers to frequently asked questions about our emergency pool service.

What qualifies as a pool emergency?

Pool emergencies include pump failures, major leaks, electrical hazards, complete equipment breakdown, storm damage, and severely green or unsafe water conditions. If your pool poses a safety risk or will sustain significant damage without immediate attention, call us.

How fast can you respond to a pool emergency?

We aim to respond to emergency calls within 2-4 hours during regular hours and within 4-6 hours for after-hours emergencies in our service area covering Main Line PA and Northern Delaware. Response times may vary based on current demand and your location.

Do you charge extra for emergency pool service?

Emergency service rates are higher than standard service calls due to the urgent response required. After-hours, weekend, and holiday calls have additional premiums. We always provide upfront pricing before beginning work so there are no surprises.

What should I do while waiting for emergency pool service?

Turn off all pool equipment at the breaker if safe to do so, keep people and pets away from the pool area, document any visible damage with photos, and avoid attempting repairs yourself especially for electrical issues. See our detailed tips section above.

What areas do you serve for emergency pool calls?

We provide emergency pool service throughout Main Line Philadelphia including Wayne, Bryn Mawr, Gladwyne, Villanova, Radnor, and surrounding areas, plus Northern Delaware including Wilmington, Hockessin, Greenville, and Centreville. Call to confirm coverage for your location.

Can you fix any brand of pool equipment?

Yes, our technicians are trained to service all major pool equipment brands including Pentair, Hayward, Jandy, Zodiac, Polaris, Raypak, Sta-Rite, and Waterway. We carry common replacement parts for faster repairs.
