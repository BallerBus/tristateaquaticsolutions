Filter Types

Types of Pool Filters We Service

We service all three major pool filter types. Each has unique advantages and maintenance requirements.

Sand Filters

Reliable and low-maintenance filtration using specially graded silica sand.

  • Filtration: 20-40 microns
  • Maintenance: Backwash weekly
  • Media life: 5-7 years
  • Best for: Large pools, low maintenance

Cartridge Filters

Energy-efficient filtration with easy-to-clean pleated polyester elements.

  • Filtration: 10-15 microns
  • Maintenance: Clean every 2-4 weeks
  • Cartridge life: 2-3 years
  • Best for: Energy savings, no backwash

DE Filters

Superior filtration using diatomaceous earth for the clearest water possible.

  • Filtration: 2-5 microns
  • Maintenance: Backwash + add DE
  • Grid life: 7-10 years
  • Best for: Crystal clear water

Warning Signs

Signs Your Filter Needs Service

Do not wait until your water turns green. Watch for these warning signs that indicate filter problems.

High Pressure Readings

Pressure 8-10 PSI above normal indicates a dirty or clogged filter that needs cleaning or media replacement.

Cloudy Water

If water remains cloudy despite proper chemical balance, your filter may not be removing fine particles effectively.

Sand or DE in Pool

Finding filter media in your pool indicates damaged internals like broken laterals, torn grids, or cracked manifolds.

Short Filter Cycles

Needing to clean or backwash more frequently than normal suggests worn media or undersized filtration.

Visible Damage

Cracked housings, torn cartridge fabric, or damaged valve handles require immediate attention to prevent leaks.

Water Leaks

Leaking around the filter tank, valves, or plumbing connections indicates worn seals or cracked components.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about pool filter service and maintenance.

Cartridge filters should be cleaned every 2-4 weeks during swimming season. Sand filters need backwashing when pressure rises 8-10 PSI above normal. DE filters require backwashing and fresh DE powder when pressure increases. We recommend professional deep cleaning at least once per season for all filter types.

Signs your filter needs replacement include: consistently high pressure readings even after cleaning, visible tears or damage to cartridge elements, cloudy water despite proper chemical balance, sand in the pool (for sand filters), shortened filter cycles, and the filter is over 5-7 years old for cartridges or 7-10 years for sand media.

Sand filters use silica sand to trap particles down to 20-40 microns and require backwashing. Cartridge filters use pleated polyester fabric to filter down to 10-15 microns and need periodic cleaning. DE (Diatomaceous Earth) filters provide the finest filtration at 2-5 microns using fossilized diatom powder, offering the clearest water but requiring more maintenance.

Filter cleaning typically costs $75-150 depending on filter type and condition. Cartridge replacement ranges from $50-300 per cartridge. Sand media replacement costs $200-400 including labor. DE grid replacement runs $300-600. Complete filter system replacement ranges from $800-2,500 depending on size and type. Contact us for a specific quote.

Yes, we regularly upgrade pool filters to improve water quality and reduce maintenance. Common upgrades include switching from sand to cartridge filters for easier maintenance, upgrading to larger capacity filters for better flow, or installing variable speed pump-compatible filters for energy savings. We will assess your system and recommend the best options.

High filter pressure indicates the filter is dirty and restricting water flow. For cartridge filters, remove and clean the element. For sand filters, backwash until water runs clear. For DE filters, backwash and add fresh DE powder. If pressure remains high after cleaning, the filter media may need replacement or there could be an internal issue requiring professional service.
