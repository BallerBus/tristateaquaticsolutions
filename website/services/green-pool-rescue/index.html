Why It Happens

What Causes a Green Pool?

Understanding why your pool turned green helps us treat it effectively and prevent it from happening again.

Algae Growth

Microscopic algae spores are always present. When chlorine drops, they multiply rapidly, turning water green within days.

Neglect

Extended vacations, busy schedules, or simply forgetting regular maintenance allows algae to take hold.

Equipment Failure

A broken pump, clogged filter, or failed chlorinator can quickly lead to stagnant water and algae blooms.

Chemical Imbalance

Wrong pH levels reduce chlorine effectiveness. Even with chlorine present, imbalanced water cannot fight algae.

Our Process

How We Rescue Your Pool

Our proven 6-step process transforms even the worst green pools back to swim-ready condition.

  1. Assessment and Testing

    We test water chemistry, inspect equipment, and evaluate algae severity to create your custom treatment plan.

  2. Remove Debris

    Skim leaves, brush walls and floor to break up algae colonies, and remove any visible organic matter.

  3. Shock Treatment

    Apply professional-grade shock at levels calculated for your pool size and algae severity to kill active algae.

  4. Algaecide Application

    Add specialized algaecide to attack remaining algae and prevent regrowth during the clearing process.

  5. Filter and Circulate

    Run your filtration system 24/7 with regular backwashing/cleaning until water clears completely.

  6. Balance and Verify

    Final water balance, equipment check, and verification that your pool is safe and swim-ready.

Prevention

Keep Your Pool From Turning Green Again

Once we rescue your pool, follow these tips to prevent future green pool emergencies.

Run Your Pump

  • Run pump 8-12 hours daily minimum
  • Circulate water completely at least once per day
  • Use a timer for consistent operation
  • Increase run time in hot weather

Balance Chemicals

  • Test water at least twice per week
  • Maintain chlorine at 2-4 ppm
  • Keep pH between 7.2-7.6
  • Shock weekly during swim season

Regular Maintenance

  • Skim surface debris daily
  • Brush walls and floor weekly
  • Clean filter regularly
  • Empty skimmer baskets often

FAQ

Green Pool Questions Answered

How long does it take to clear a green pool?

Most green pools can be transformed back to crystal clear water within 3-7 days, depending on the severity of the algae growth. Mild cases may clear in 2-3 days, while severely neglected pools with black algae may take up to 10-14 days.

Why did my pool turn green?

Pools turn green due to algae growth, which thrives when chlorine levels drop too low, pH becomes imbalanced, the filter is not running properly, or the pool has not been maintained. Common triggers include equipment failure, extended vacations, chemical imbalance, and general neglect.

Should I drain my green pool?

In most cases, no. Draining a pool is expensive (water costs $500-$1,500+), wastes thousands of gallons, and can damage the pool structure - especially fiberglass and vinyl pools which can pop out of the ground. Professional green pool rescue can restore almost any pool without draining. Only in extreme cases of contamination do we recommend partial or full draining.

How much does green pool rescue cost?

Green pool rescue typically costs between $350-$800 depending on pool size and severity of the algae problem. This includes all chemicals, multiple visits, filter cleaning, and our satisfaction guarantee. Weekly maintenance plans can prevent recurrence for $150-$400 per month.

Can you fix a pool with black algae?

Yes, we can treat black algae, though it requires a more intensive treatment protocol. Black algae has deep roots and is chlorine-resistant, so we use specialized algaecides, aggressive brushing with steel brushes (on concrete/plaster), and extended treatment periods. Full eradication typically takes 10-14 days and may require ongoing vigilance.

Is it safe to swim in a green pool?

No, you should not swim in a green pool. Green water indicates the presence of algae and potentially harmful bacteria. The water may also harbor other contaminants like E. coli. Wait until the water is crystal clear and all chemical levels have been properly balanced before swimming.
