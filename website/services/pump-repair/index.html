Pump Types

Pumps We Service

We repair and install all types of pool pumps, from basic single speed to advanced variable speed models.

Single Speed

Traditional pumps that run at one constant speed. Reliable and affordable, but less energy efficient than modern options.

Variable Speed

Advanced pumps with programmable speeds. Save 70-90% on energy costs while providing superior filtration and quieter operation.

Two-Speed

Pumps with high and low speed settings. A middle ground between single and variable speed, offering some energy savings.

Booster Pumps

Secondary pumps for pressure-side cleaners like Polaris. Essential for automatic pool cleaning systems.

Common Issues

Problems We Fix

We diagnose and repair all common pool pump issues quickly and correctly the first time.

Motor Failure

The most common pump issue, often caused by overheating, age, or electrical problems.

  • Pump won't start or hums without running
  • Motor runs hot and shuts off
  • Burning smell from motor
  • Tripped breaker when pump starts

Impeller Issues

Debris, wear, or damage to the impeller reduces water flow and pump efficiency.

  • Reduced water flow to pool
  • Pump cavitating or making grinding sounds
  • Debris stuck in impeller housing
  • Worn or cracked impeller blades

Seal Leaks

Failed shaft seals allow water to leak from the pump, potentially damaging the motor.

  • Water dripping under pump
  • Wet motor housing
  • Visible rust on motor shaft
  • Pump losing prime frequently

Priming Problems

Pump fails to draw water, running dry and potentially causing damage.

  • Air bubbles in pump basket
  • Pump strainer not filling
  • Suction-side air leaks
  • Clogged skimmer or pump basket

Strange Noises

Unusual sounds indicate specific problems that need immediate attention.

  • Grinding or screeching (bearings failing)
  • Loud humming (capacitor or motor issue)
  • Gurgling or sucking sounds (air leak)
  • High-pitched whining (cavitation)

Electrical Issues

Wiring, capacitor, and control problems that prevent proper operation.

  • Pump trips breaker repeatedly
  • Intermittent starting problems
  • Timer or automation not controlling pump
  • Variable speed display errors
Decision Guide

Repair or Replace?

We help you make the right choice based on your pump's condition, age, and the cost-effectiveness of each option.

When to Repair

Repair is often the best choice for newer pumps with isolated issues.

  • Pump is less than 5-7 years old
  • Single component failure (seal, capacitor)
  • Repair cost is under 50% of replacement
  • First time needing major service
  • Variable speed pump with motor warranty
  • Impeller or basket replacement needed

When to Replace

Replacement makes more sense for older pumps or when upgrade benefits outweigh repair costs.

  • Pump is 8+ years old
  • Multiple component failures
  • Repair cost exceeds 50% of new pump
  • Second major repair in 2 years
  • Single speed pump with high energy bills
  • Want to upgrade to variable speed for savings

FAQ

Pool Pump Questions

Common questions about pump repair, replacement, and maintenance.

How do I know if my pool pump needs repair or replacement?

Signs your pump needs attention include unusual noises (grinding, screeching, humming), reduced water flow, the pump not priming, visible leaks around the pump housing, or the motor running hot. If your pump is over 8-10 years old and requires frequent repairs, replacement with a variable speed pump is often more cost-effective due to energy savings.

How much does pool pump repair cost?

Pool pump repair costs typically range from $150-$500 depending on the issue. Motor replacement runs $300-$800, seal replacement $150-$300, and impeller replacement $200-$400. A complete variable speed pump replacement costs $1,200-$2,500 installed but can save $50-$100+ monthly on electricity.

What are the benefits of upgrading to a variable speed pump?

Variable speed pumps can reduce energy costs by 70-90% compared to single speed pumps, saving $1,000-$1,500 annually. They run quieter, last longer due to reduced motor stress, provide better filtration at lower speeds, and many utility companies offer rebates for installation. Most homeowners see a return on investment within 2-3 years.

Why is my pool pump making loud noises?

Common causes of pump noise include: cavitation (gurgling/sucking sounds) from air leaks or clogged baskets, bearing failure (grinding/screeching) requiring motor replacement, vibration from loose mounting or internal debris, and humming without starting which indicates capacitor or motor issues. We diagnose the specific cause and recommend the most cost-effective solution.

How long does a pool pump typically last?

Single speed pumps typically last 8-12 years, while quality variable speed pumps can last 10-15+ years with proper maintenance. Factors affecting lifespan include run time, water chemistry, electrical conditions, and regular maintenance. Running a pump 24/7 or in areas with frequent power surges will shorten its life.

Why won't my pool pump prime?

Priming issues are commonly caused by: air leaks in suction lines or pump lid O-ring, clogged skimmer or pump baskets, a stuck or worn check valve, low water level in the pool, or a failed impeller. We systematically check each potential cause to identify and fix the issue quickly.
