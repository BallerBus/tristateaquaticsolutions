Salt Water Benefits
Why Salt Water

Benefits of Salt Water Pools

Salt water pools offer a superior swimming experience with numerous health and maintenance advantages.

  • Silky-Soft WaterSalt water feels noticeably softer and more luxurious on your skin compared to traditional chlorine pools.
  • Gentler on Eyes & SkinLower chloramine levels mean less eye irritation, dry skin, and that harsh chlorine smell on hair and swimsuits.
  • Consistent Chlorine LevelsThe salt cell produces chlorine continuously, maintaining steady sanitizer levels without the peaks and valleys of manual dosing.
  • Lower Long-Term CostsSalt is inexpensive compared to chlorine tablets. After the initial system investment, ongoing chemical costs are significantly lower.
  • No Chemical StorageEliminate the need to buy, store, and handle harsh chlorine chemicals. Salt is safe and easy to add when needed.
Specialized Care

Salt Water Maintenance Needs

Salt water pools require specialized knowledge and care beyond traditional pool maintenance.

  • Salt Level ManagementMonitor and maintain optimal salt concentration (typically 3000-3500 ppm) for efficient chlorine generation.
  • Cell Inspection & CleaningRegular inspection for calcium scale buildup with acid cleaning when deposits affect chlorine output.
  • pH MonitoringSalt systems tend to raise pH over time. Regular testing and adjustment prevents scaling and maintains water balance.
  • Stabilizer (CYA) BalanceProper cyanuric acid levels protect the chlorine from UV degradation while avoiding over-stabilization.
  • Calcium Hardness ControlManaging calcium levels prevents scaling on the salt cell and pool surfaces in our hard water region.
  • Equipment ProtectionMonitor and protect metal fixtures, heaters, and other equipment that can be affected by salt water.

FAQ

Salt Water Pool Questions

How often should salt cells be cleaned?

Salt cells should be inspected every 3 months and cleaned as needed. In areas with hard water like parts of Pennsylvania and Delaware, cleaning may be required more frequently to remove calcium buildup and maintain optimal chlorine production. Our service plans include regular cell inspection and cleaning.

What is the ideal salt level for my pool?

Most salt water chlorine generators operate best between 2700-3400 ppm (parts per million) of salt. The specific ideal range depends on your equipment manufacturer. Pentair IntelliChlor recommends 3200-3400 ppm, while Hayward AquaRite operates best at 3200 ppm. We test and maintain optimal levels at every visit.

How long do salt cells last?

A well-maintained salt cell typically lasts 3-7 years depending on usage, water chemistry, and maintenance. Regular cleaning and proper water balance can significantly extend cell life. We monitor cell health during every service visit and will alert you when replacement is approaching.

Can I convert my chlorine pool to salt water?

Yes, most pools can be converted to salt water. The conversion involves installing a salt chlorine generator, adding the appropriate amount of salt to your pool (typically 400-500 lbs for an average pool), and adjusting your maintenance routine. We handle the entire conversion process and train you on your new system.

Is salt water bad for pool equipment?

Salt water is slightly corrosive and can affect certain metals and finishes over time. However, with proper water chemistry and equipment rated for salt water use, your pool and equipment will remain in excellent condition. We ensure all equipment is compatible and properly maintained to prevent issues.

Why does my salt cell show low salt even after adding salt?

This is often caused by calcium scale buildup on the salt sensor inside the cell, requiring cleaning. It can also indicate a faulty sensor, control board issue, or that the salt has not fully dissolved and circulated. Our technicians can diagnose the exact cause and resolve the issue.
